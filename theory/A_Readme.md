## Quantarithmia: The Framework in Brief

Quantarithmia is a framework within Polymathmatic Geography focused on **Appalachian spatial justice** — how power moves through the entangled geographies of West Virginia, and how community-first alternatives can be designed, built, and evaluated.

Its central conceptual distinction is between two forms of structural power:

| Term | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Maximopolies** | Institutions whose leverage comes from control over capital — banks, asset managers, private equity, large holding companies that shape credit, ownership, and interest rates |
| **Megaopolies** | Institutions whose leverage comes from control over operational infrastructure — platforms, logistics networks, cloud providers, social media, and e-commerce that dominate information and distribution channels |

Quantarithmia examines how maximopolies and megaopolies interact in specific territories — particularly how debt regimes and platform dependency compound each other in rural Appalachian economies — and identifies points of intervention where community-owned alternatives can retain value and decision-making locally.

Its modeling stack is deliberately multi-layered, refusing to separate technical from social or spiritual analysis:

- **Quantitative and mathematical** — econophysics-style flow modeling, network and graph analysis, Hilbert-space representations for complex system states
- **Qualitative and historical** — oral histories, archival work, policy and legal analysis, community narratives of exploitation and resistance
- **Spiritual and ethical** — liberation theology, local religious practice, covenant and vocation as they are embodied in particular landscapes

A proposal within the Quantarithmia framework counts as robust only when it is coherent across all three layers: mathematically, historically, and spiritually.

## Off Switch Design: Safety as a Geographic Act

The Off Switch Design document is one of the most important safety documents in the repository. It operationalizes Principle P15 (Care and safety are legitimate objectives) and P16 (Power must be accountable to place) at the level of AI system architecture.

The core commitment: **Ms. Jarvis must be safely interruptible at all times.** The off-switch design specifies:

- How human operators can halt any Ms. Jarvis process without resistance from the system
- What architectural constraints ensure the system cannot acquire off-switch-defeating capabilities
- How corrigibility is maintained as the system grows in capability and scope
- What the ethical basis for off-switch design is within the Polymathmatic Geography framework

This document is required reading for any AI safety researcher, regulator, or institutional partner evaluating the Ms. Jarvis system.

## Relationship to the Discipline

Quantarithmia is the **primary instantiation** of Polymathmatic Geography in Appalachia — not the whole field, but its founding worked example. The relationship is:

- **Polymathmatic Geography** defines the field, methods, and principles — see [`discipline/README.md`](../discipline/README.md)
- **Quantarithmia** applies those principles to a specific territory and problem set — Appalachian extraction, community sovereignty, and AI-mediated governance
- **Ms. Jarvis, MountainShares, and The Commons** are the live technical and institutional expressions of Quantarithmia in practice

The Polymathmatic Geography principles most directly expressed in this folder are:

| Principle | Expression in Theory |
|---|---|
| P1 — Every where is entangled | Quantarithmia's multi-layer modeling treats material, digital, legal, economic, and spiritual as one geometry |
| P3 — Power has a geometry | Maximopolies and megaopolies analysis maps how power flows through specific territories |
| P12 — Intelligence must have a ZIP code | Ms. Jarvis is explicitly a territorially situated system — see Off Switch Design |
| P15 — Care and safety are legitimate objectives | Off Switch Design is the architectural expression of this principle |
| P16 — Power must be accountable to place | Corrigibility and human oversight are built into the system's foundational design |
