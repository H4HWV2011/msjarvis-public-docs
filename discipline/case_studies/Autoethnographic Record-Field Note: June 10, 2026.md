## Theoretical Nodes

### Section 2.3 — Weber: Legitimation and Rational-Legal Authority
The researcher challenged the municipality's framing of the RV/tiny home permit by citing the federal regulatory hierarchy (HUD standards, IRC classification, NPS regional planning documents). This is a live Weber event: a counter-claim to rational-legal authority over spatial categories, grounded not in personal opinion but in documented federal and regional records. The council packet, the regional plan, and the federal citations constitute the researcher's legitimation record — the formal grounds on which the challenge stands.

### Section 3.2 — Entangled Space: Layered Materiality and Braided Authority
The RV/tiny home dispute is a paradigm case of entangled space. In a single site (Mount Hope, WV 25880), the following layers are simultaneously active:
- **Material:** land, existing structures, utility connections, road access
- **Legal-institutional:** municipal zoning code, federal housing standards, NPS regional planning authority
- **Computational:** permit databases, public comment records, the researcher's public-facing broadcast channel
- **Narrative/theological:** community stories about housing scarcity, dignity, and who belongs in the town
- **Economic:** absentee ownership patterns, benefits-sensitive household economics, local labor

The Facebook wall functions in this context not only as a personal platform but as a node in the computational layer of entangled space — a channel through which the researcher broadcasts documented civic analysis to a public audience.

### Section 6.6 — The PALCO Correction: Legitimation Failure and Correction
The former organizational collaborator event maps structurally to the PALCO detection architecture case.
