# MS.___.ALLIS Corridor Partner Participation Agreement

> This agreement is intended for authorized representatives of municipalities, public agencies, nonprofit organizations, or other institutional entities invited to participate as **Corridor Partners** in the MS.___.ALLIS program under the MountainShares framework. By accepting this Agreement, the individual confirms they are acting on behalf of the organization named in the registration form (the "Corridor Partner Organization"). In any conflict with the MountainShares Terms and Conditions or applicable law, those higher-level instruments control.
>
> *This document is a general template. Specific community names, installation addresses, and coverage areas are established separately in a Community Participation Addendum executed alongside this Agreement.*

---

## 1. Role and Purpose

By accepting this Agreement, you agree to participate as a **Corridor Partner** in the MS.___.ALLIS program under the MountainShares framework.

- Your primary purpose is to provide site access, designate an operational point of contact, and participate in good faith in the governance, evaluation, and stewardship of the connectivity and intelligence infrastructure deployed in or adjacent to your community.
- Your organization's contribution is treated as **commons work** — in-kind participation documented in the recognition ledger — not as a purchase, a grant, an employment relationship, or an investment.
- You act as an authorized representative of the Corridor Partner Organization identified during registration, committing that organization to the terms described here.

---

## 2. Relationship to MountainShares and the Program Operator

- This Agreement establishes a limited **Corridor Partner relationship** between the program operator ("Operator") and the Corridor Partner Organization for the purposes described here.
- It does **not** create an employment relationship, joint venture, procurement contract, or investment arrangement between the Corridor Partner Organization and the Operator or the MountainShares DAO.
- The Corridor Partner Organization participates voluntarily and may end its participation at any time, subject to any obligations that reasonably survive — for example, data stewardship commitments for information already collected under this Agreement.

---

## 3. In-Kind Contribution and EMS Recognition

To participate as a Corridor Partner, the Corridor Partner Organization agrees to:

- Designate an individual point of contact authorized to coordinate with the Operator on siting, access, and operational matters.
- Provide reasonable access to agreed installation locations on or adjacent to structures or land under the organization's control, subject to any required internal approvals, historic-preservation review, or regulatory processes.
- Participate in good faith in pilot evaluation activities — including providing qualitative feedback on coverage, usability, and community fit during the defined evaluation period.

The Operator agrees that:

- The Corridor Partner Organization's in-kind contributions — site access, staff time, and evaluation participation — are documented in the MountainShares recognition ledger as Earned MountainShares (EMS) hours associated with the organization's community UEI.
- EMS represent documented **hours and contributions of commons work**, not a right to cash, financial returns, or ownership of any kind.
- EMS-based governance participation and any EMS conversions, if and when available, are governed by separate published program rules, which may change through documented governance processes.

---

## 4. Scope of Participation

As a Corridor Partner, the Corridor Partner Organization's participation includes:

**Site Access and Coordination**
- Granting access for installation, maintenance, and removal of agreed hardware components — backbone radio, endpoint radio, satellite terminal, enclosures, cabling, and supporting equipment — on structures or parcels identified in the Community Participation Addendum.
- Cooperating with historic-preservation, siting, and documentation requirements applicable to the installation locations.
- Notifying the Operator in advance of any changes to site access, ownership, or use that may affect installed equipment.

**Operational Point of Contact**
- Maintaining a designated individual who can receive operational communications, coordinate access for maintenance, and serve as the organization's voice in pilot governance activities.

**Pilot Evaluation**
- Participating in the defined evaluation period — including review meetings, feedback sessions, and any jointly agreed data-review activities — to assess whether the infrastructure is serving its intended purpose.
- Providing honest, good-faith input on what is working, what is not, and whether continued or expanded participation makes sense for the community.

**Governance Participation**
- Participating, where invited, in lightweight regional working-group discussions about corridor-wide data practices, content standards, and infrastructure decisions — without being expected to make technical or administrative decisions about hardware or software.

The Corridor Partner Organization is **not** expected to:
- Administer, configure, or troubleshoot hardware or software.
- Fund any aspect of procurement, installation, or ongoing service.
- Accept any financial liability for infrastructure owned, installed, and maintained by the Operator or its designees.

---

## 5. No Cost to the Corridor Partner Organization

Participation in MS.___.ALLIS at the no-cost tier is provided at no financial expense to the Corridor Partner Organization. Specifically:

- The Corridor Partner Organization pays no fees for hardware, installation labor, satellite service, software, or ongoing support during the pilot period.
- The Corridor Partner Organization incurs no obligation to fund, procure, or contract for any component of the program.
- If, at a future date, the Corridor Partner Organization wishes to explore an expanded service arrangement beyond the no-cost tier, that would be a separate conversation governed by the MountainShares agreements in place at that time. No such arrangement is implied, promised, or required by this Agreement.

## 6. Data Stewardship

### What Is Collected

The program collects only the data needed to deliver visitor connectivity, safety communications support, and heritage content services within the defined coverage area. Data categories include:

- **Operational network data:** device status, throughput, error logs, uptime records
- **Aggregate usage data:** connection counts, content access patterns, coverage performance metrics — collected in aggregate and without individual identification
- **Content and reference data:** maps, trail and heritage narratives, safety protocols, and locally cached reference materials

No personal data identifying individual visitors or residents is intentionally collected. No continuous location tracking of individuals occurs.

### Local-First Principles

All data is collected and stored locally on infrastructure within the community first. Data does not leave the local environment except:

- When aggregate or de-identified summaries are shared for regional analysis or academic review with the explicit knowledge of the Corridor Partner Organization's designated point of contact; or
- When technically necessary for satellite backhaul synchronization, which is governed by the Operator's data minimization practices.

### Ownership and Control

Data generated by infrastructure within the Corridor Partner Organization's community is treated as a community resource. The Corridor Partner Organization retains the right to:

- Request a summary of what data has been collected within its community at any time.
- Request deletion of locally stored data upon ending participation, subject to any minimum retention requirements for safety or audit purposes.
- Decline specific data-sharing requests for regional or academic use without affecting its participation in the program.

### Access and Protection

Access to operational and location data is restricted to authorized roles. Any transfer of data outside the local environment for research, training, or regional analysis uses de-identified or aggregated information wherever possible. Retention periods are defined per data category, with automatic deletion where technically feasible.

## 7. Infrastructure Ownership and Liability

- Physical assets installed under this Agreement — radios, satellite terminals, enclosures, mounts, cabling, and UPS units — remain the property of the Operator or its designated community-benefit partner, not the Corridor Partner Organization, unless a separate written agreement provides otherwise.
- The Corridor Partner Organization is not liable for damage to equipment caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including weather events, acts of third parties, or structural changes to host buildings, provided the Corridor Partner Organization notifies the Operator promptly upon becoming aware of such events.
- The Operator is responsible for ensuring that installations meet applicable safety, structural, and preservation standards and for maintaining appropriate insurance coverage for the installed equipment.
- Either party may request removal of installed equipment with reasonable advance notice. The Operator is responsible for removal and restoration of any mounting surfaces to their prior condition.

---

## 8. Ethical Use of Locally Hosted Intelligence

Where the program includes locally running artificial learning models or location intelligence services:

- These services are designed to support decisions — suggesting routes, surfacing relevant heritage content, highlighting nearby points of interest — not to make automated decisions about individuals or enforcement actions.
- No profiling of individual visitors or residents occurs.
- The Corridor Partner Organization's designated point of contact may request a plain-language description of what models are running locally and what they do at any time.
- Models may be corrected, retrained, or removed through governance processes in which the Corridor Partner Organization has a voice.

---

## 9. Confidentiality

The Corridor Partner Organization agrees to treat as confidential any non-public technical details, vulnerability information, or proprietary implementation specifics shared with it in the course of participation — and not to disclose such information to third parties without the Operator's written consent, except as required by law.

The Corridor Partner Organization may describe its participation in general terms (for example, "our community participates in a connectivity pilot program") without disclosing technical specifics.

---

## 10. Governance Rights

- The Corridor Partner Organization's governance voice in regional working-group discussions is grounded in its in-kind contribution and tenure as a Corridor Partner.
- Serving as a Corridor Partner does not grant veto power over program decisions made by other participating communities or the Operator.
- Serving as a Corridor Partner does not entitle the Corridor Partner Organization to any share of treasury assets, program revenues, or intellectual property developed under the program.

---

## 11. Intellectual Property

- The Corridor Partner Organization retains ownership of any heritage content, maps, narratives, photographs, or other materials it contributes to the program's local content store.
- The Corridor Partner Organization grants the Operator a limited, non-exclusive, royalty-free right to cache, serve, and display contributed content to visitors and park users within the coverage area, for the duration of this Agreement.
- Upon ending participation, the Operator will cease serving contributed content from program infrastructure within a reasonable transition period.
- The Corridor Partner Organization does not receive ownership of the MountainShares platform, Ms. Allis models, infrastructure hardware, or any software developed under the program by participating in this Agreement.

---

## 12. Term, Changes, and Termination

- This Agreement becomes effective when accepted during onboarding and remains in effect for the duration of the Corridor Partner Organization's participation in the program.
- The Operator may update this Agreement from time to time. Material changes will be communicated through official channels with reasonable notice, and continued participation after notice of changes may be treated as acceptance of the updated terms.
- Either the Corridor Partner Organization or the Operator may end participation at any time with reasonable advance written notice. Ending participation:
  - does not erase EMS hours already documented in the recognition ledger;
  - does not relieve either party of data stewardship obligations for information already collected; and
  - triggers the Operator's obligation to remove installed equipment within an agreed timeframe.

---

## 13. Governing Terms

This Agreement is subject to the MountainShares Terms and Conditions and any applicable federal, state, or local law. In the event of conflict, applicable law controls, followed by the MountainShares Terms and Conditions, followed by this Agreement.

Nothing in this Agreement limits the Corridor Partner Organization's obligations under federal historic-preservation law, the National Environmental Policy Act, or any other applicable regulatory framework that governs activities on or adjacent to its property.
