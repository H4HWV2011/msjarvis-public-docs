## Recitals

H4H is a West Virginia nonprofit corporation operating the MountainShares community benefit program. H4H seeks to develop The Commons, a social media and community marketplace platform, and the MountainShares mutual credit system as the economic and governance infrastructure underlying that program.

KTS is engaged to design, architect, and develop that platform and infrastructure on H4H's behalf. The parties agree that the MountainShares mutual credit system and The Commons platform are being built for H4H as part of its charitable mission, and that KTS retains ownership of any independent platform tools or intelligence systems it develops in the course of this engagement, which may be made available to H4H under a separate license arrangement.

All work performed under this SOW is contributed by KTS as an in-kind donation. KTS receives no monetary compensation.

---

## Section 1 — Scope of Services

KTS shall design, architect, and develop the following for H4H:

### 1.1 MountainShares Mutual Credit System

- Smart contract architecture and proof-of-concept for a commons-based mutual credit system
- Mutual credit ledger backend — transaction logic, balances, issuance rules
- Commons governance database schema supporting decentralized decision-making
- DAO governance framework — voting mechanics, proposal management, treasury structure
- Rewards program infrastructure — participation tracking, credit issuance, member standing
- Integration hooks for geographic property and community resource data
- Audit logging for transparent, community-accountable resource allocation

### 1.2 The Commons Platform

- Core application architecture for a social media and community marketplace serving MountainShares members
- Member-facing interfaces — profiles, participation, community features
- API architecture — endpoints, authentication, authorization, and access control
- Integration of MountainShares rewards and governance mechanics into the platform UI
- Backend infrastructure for community content, marketplace listings, and resource coordination
- Platform documentation sufficient for ongoing development and third-party review

## Section 4 — Compensation

All work performed by KTS under this SOW constitutes an **in-kind donation** by Kidd's Technical Services to Harmony for Hope, Inc. KTS receives no monetary compensation under this SOW.

H4H may recognize this contribution on its financial statements as donated services under applicable GAAP guidance and may use documented hours as in-kind match for grant applications. Neither party should treat this as a tax-deductible charitable contribution of services under IRC §170.

---

## Section 5 — Work-for-Hire Designations

The following deliverables are designated **work-for-hire** and shall be owned by Harmony for Hope, Inc. upon delivery:

- D-01: MountainShares smart contract proof of concept
- D-02: Mutual credit ledger backend
- D-03: Commons governance schema and DAO framework
- D-04: MountainShares rewards infrastructure
- D-05: The Commons platform application features and configurations
- D-06: Platform documentation created specifically for H4H

Any independent platform tools, intelligence systems, or infrastructure developed by KTS in the course of this engagement that are not specific to H4H's application remain the property of KTS and may be made available to H4H under a separate license arrangement to be documented between the parties.

---

## Section 6 — Special Conditions

**6.1 Independent Platform Tools**
The parties acknowledge that KTS may develop independent technical infrastructure in the course of this engagement. Ownership of such infrastructure shall be addressed in a separate agreement between the parties. Nothing in this SOW transfers ownership of any such independent tools to H4H beyond the work-for-hire designations in Section 5.

**6.2 Scope Adjustments**
The scope described in Section 1 reflects the parties' best understanding at the time of this SOW. As development progresses, the parties may agree in writing to expand, reduce, or modify scope. Subsequent SOWs may be issued to govern additional work.

**6.3 In-Kind Contribution Records**
KTS shall maintain reasonable time records documenting hours contributed under this SOW for H4H's grant application and financial reporting purposes.
