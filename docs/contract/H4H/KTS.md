## 1. Purpose

This document clarifies the current legal and operational status of Kidd’s Technical Services in relation to Harmony for Hope, Inc. and the MountainShares system.

Its purpose is to ensure that all contracts, schedules, technical references, and governance documents accurately describe:

- the present legal reality of the contractor relationship,
- the intended future KTS entity structure,
- the separation between charitable and private value,
- and the boundaries between H4H, the MountainShares Commons, and the future KTS commercial layer.

This document is interpretive and structural. It is not itself a grant of equity, an assignment of intellectual property, or a substitute for formal entity-formation documents.

---

## 2. Current Legal Status

As of the date of this document, **Kidd’s Technical Services** is a trade name used by **Carrie Ann Kidd** and is not yet a separately formed West Virginia limited liability company.

Accordingly:

- references to “KTS” in current contracts and planning documents refer to Carrie Ann Kidd operating personally,
- no separate liability shield currently exists,
- no separate KTS EIN yet exists,
- and any contract that names “Kidd’s Technical Services, LLC” should be understood as requiring correction, amendment, or later novation unless and until the entity is formally formed.

Until formal entity formation occurs, all contractual rights and obligations described as belonging to KTS remain personal to Carrie Ann Kidd to the extent legally effective.

---

## 3. Intended Future Structure

The intended future structure is as follows:

- **Harmony for Hope, Inc.** remains the charitable anchor and nonprofit sponsor.
- **MountainShares Commons / DUNA** serves as the community-governed, benefits-sensitive, non-equity mutual-credit and governance layer.
- **KTS** becomes the founder-side for-profit entity that holds ALLIS-related intellectual property, commercial engine functions, and private founder-capital arrangements.

In the intended structure, KTS is the proper location for:

- proprietary ALLIS and GBIM commercial ownership,
- private founder capital or deferred equity instruments,
- licensing arrangements with H4H and the Commons,
- and commercial services that should not be housed inside H4H.

This future structure does not exist merely because it is described here. It becomes operative only after lawful formation, EIN issuance, governing-document adoption, and formal execution of the relevant agreements.

---

## 4. Relationship to Harmony for Hope, Inc.

H4H is the existing legal organization and charitable sponsor through which current MountainShares and related program work is being conducted.

H4H may contract with Carrie Ann Kidd d/b/a Kidd’s Technical Services for:

- technical services,
- software and infrastructure support,
- geospatial and AI system development,
- documentation,
- maintenance,
- and other professional work supporting H4H’s mission and programs.

H4H does not pass founder equity through itself. H4H does not convert charitable value into private ownership interests through EMS, DAO participation, or participant account systems.

Any contractual relationship between H4H and KTS, whether current or future, must remain consistent with nonprofit law, related-party rules, and conflict-of-interest procedures.

---

## 5. Relationship to the MountainShares Commons

The MountainShares Commons is the community-governed, non-equity, benefits-sensitive mutual-credit and governance layer.

The Commons governs:

- EMS and M$ community instruments,
- resident and participant account structures,
- governance participation,
- treasury and reserve oversight,
- and community operating rules.

KTS is not the Commons. KTS is not the owner of resident account balances, EMS balances, M$ balances, or the community treasury.

The founder’s role in relation to the Commons is steward, not owner.

No KTS-side private capital arrangement may be embedded in resident-facing MountainShares balances or represented as participant ownership of KTS.

---

## 6. Intellectual Property Position

ALLIS, GBIM, and related proprietary technical architecture are intended to reside on the KTS side of the structure.

At the current stage, where formal KTS entity formation has not yet occurred, such proprietary rights are best understood as being held personally by Carrie Ann Kidd and intended for later placement into the properly formed KTS entity or successor commercial vehicle.

This means:

- H4H may use certain systems under contract, license, or interim operational authority,
- the Commons may later receive arm’s-length licensed use through H4H or directly through the DUNA structure,
- but ownership of proprietary system architecture is not transferred merely by operational deployment inside H4H.

No current use of ALLIS, GBIM, or related systems by H4H should be construed as an implied full assignment of those proprietary rights unless expressly stated in a separate signed instrument.

---

## 7. Separation of Charitable Value and Founder Value

The controlling structural rule is that charitable value and founder private value must be documented separately.

Accordingly:

- technical work donated to H4H may be recognized as charitable in-kind contribution,
- private founder-capital or deferred commercial value may be documented separately on the KTS or founder-private side,
- and one category may not be silently converted into the other.

This separation is especially important where the same underlying labor or technical contribution is being tracked in more than one way for internal planning, accounting, or later structuring purposes.

No charitable contribution to H4H becomes founder equity merely because it was important, extensive, or mission-critical.

## 8. Contracting Implications

Until KTS is formally formed, contracting documents should use one of the following approaches:

1. Contract directly with **Carrie Ann Kidd d/b/a Kidd’s Technical Services**.
2. State expressly that the referenced “KTS” party is not yet a formed entity and that obligations run to Carrie Ann Kidd personally.
3. Replace current KTS references later through written amendment, assignment, or novation after lawful entity formation.

Any agreement that presently names **Kidd’s Technical Services, LLC** without entity formation should be revised to avoid implying a presently existing legal entity where none has yet been formed.

This includes, as applicable:

- master service agreements,
- statements of work,
- fee amendments,
- founder capital instruments,
- technical investment statements,
- and licensing documents.

---

## 9. Conflict of Interest and Recusal

Because Carrie Ann Kidd may serve in roles on both sides of the H4H–KTS relationship, all current and future related-party transactions require documented conflict-of-interest and recusal procedures.

This applies to:

- contracts between H4H and Contractor,
- any later H4H–KTS licensing arrangements,
- future KTS-side founder capital documentation,
- treasury-funded transition work,
- and any board or governance action affecting the relationship.

At minimum, the process should include:

- disclosure of the overlapping roles,
- abstention where required,
- disinterested review and approval where required,
- and written records sufficient for later audit or legal review.

- West Virginia entity formation is complete,
- an EIN has been obtained,
- governing organizational documents are adopted,

At that point, the parties may transfer ongoing contractual relationships from Carrie Ann Kidd personally to the newly formed entity, subject to appropriate approvals and retained rights regarding prior work.

No document should imply that this transition has already happened before those steps are completed.
