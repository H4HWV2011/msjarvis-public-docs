## Executive Summary

Kidd's Technical Services (KTS) is a sole-proprietor technology firm based in Mount Hope, West Virginia, operating as a for-profit geoanalytical and AI platform company. KTS is the sole owner and developer of **ALLIS** — the Artificial Learning & Location Intelligence System — a locally hosted, place-anchored AI platform with a documented founder investment of 6,515 hours (~$977,000 at $150/hour) fully auditable for grant in-kind match.

KTS is currently under a **Master Services Agreement with Harmony for Hope, Inc. (H4H)**, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Mount Hope, WV, under which KTS developed and operates the **MountainShares Commons** platform. ALLIS is licensed to H4H at **no cost** as a public-benefit contribution — KTS retains full IP ownership. The commercial revenue model — paid nodes, cluster membership fees, and geospatial services contracts — targets regional businesses, public safety, healthcare, and infrastructure operators across southern West Virginia and the broader Appalachian corridor.

KTS is seeking SBA guidance and capital access to fund the transition from founder-funded development into a commercially operational geoanalytical firm capable of serving the deployment pipeline already in progress.

## The Business: What KTS Does

**KTS is a technology-based services company with two primary revenue lines:**

1. **ALLIS Platform Commercial Licensing** — Named AI intelligence nodes (`____.ALLIS`) deployed for commercial clients, including node deployment fees, cluster membership fees, monthly operations fees, and scoped integration/data expansion work. Government and nonprofit nodes are provided free via H4H sponsorship; commercial clients pay KTS directly.

2. **Geoanalytical Services** — GIS infrastructure development, PostGIS database administration, geospatial data ingestion and management, address data quality, and spatial analysis for municipalities, agencies, and commercial clients. This is the core professional services capacity that supports ALLIS deployments and stands independently as a billable service in southern West Virginia's underserved market.[1][2]

**The core technology:**

ALLIS runs on a production-stable, containerized microservices stack (Docker, FastAPI, Uvicorn) grounding **26.9 million West Virginia geospatial features** — critical infrastructure, parcels, roads, and facilities — each individually addressable, semantically searchable, and probabilistically updatable as a geospatial belief. The platform is live and in Phase 0 red team testing at https://egeria.mountainshares.us. Academic validation has been provided by Dr. Anurag K. Srivastava, Raymond J. Lane Professor and Chair of the WVU Lane Department of Computer Science and Electrical Engineering.

## The Market Opportunity: Why Southern WV Needs a Geoanalytical Firm

Southern West Virginia has no locally based geoanalytical firm. The region's municipalities, county agencies, utilities, and rural health systems currently rely on distant contractors or go without modern geospatial services entirely. KTS's target market includes:

- **Municipal and county government** — address data management, parcel data quality, 911/E-911 GIS, infrastructure mapping
- **Rural electric cooperatives and utilities** — spatially distributed asset management and hazard overlays
- **NPS and federal land managers** — corridor heritage interpretation and visitor connectivity infrastructure (New River Gorge corridor, 53 miles, 7 gateway communities)
- **Regional healthcare and public safety** — locally hosted AI operating within data governance requirements
- **Regional employers and economic development organizations** — location intelligence for workforce, logistics, and investment decisions

The New River Gorge National Park received national park designation, accelerating tourism growth across the nine-county New River Highlands region. Hundreds of thousands of annual visitors pass through gateway communities — Mount Hope, Fayetteville, Oak Hill, Hinton, Ansted, Thurmond — none of which have locally owned digital or geospatial infrastructure. KTS's ALLIS mesh pilot is the first deployable technology solution for this corridor, with active grant applications through the New River Highlands Development Fund ($7,592 ask) and Greater Mount Hope Community Fund ($15,684 ask) as pilot seed funding.

KTS is at the transition point between founder-funded development and commercial operation. The following needs are immediate:

### Priority 1 — Working Capital for Commercial Deployment Pipeline
KTS has a documented deployment workflow, crew-ready installation guides, and a growing pipeline of community nodes that generate proof-of-deployment necessary for commercial sales. Capital is needed for:

## Recommended First Steps Before the SBA Meeting

1. **Contact WVSBDC first** — (888) 982-7232 or askme@wv.gov. Request a free counseling session to help format financial projections and business plan for SBA presentation. WVSBDC can also help identify which loan program fits best.[8][9]

2. **Register in SAM.gov** — Begin System for Award Management registration now to open federal contracting eligibility. This is a prerequisite for any NPS, ARC, USDA, or EDA contract work.[4]

3. **Formalize KTS as an LLC if not already done** — Confirm Articles of Organization are filed with the WV Secretary of State; operating agreement in place.

4. **Separate KTS financials clearly from H4H** — Ensure bank accounts, bookkeeping, and financial statements are entirely distinct from H4H's nonprofit accounts. Lenders will want clean separation.

5. **Prepare the use-of-funds narrative** — Be specific: hardware for X nodes ($Y), labor for installation ($Z), legal ($A), working capital for 6 months of operations ($B). Concrete use-of-funds statements are the single biggest differentiator for startup lending decisions.[12]
