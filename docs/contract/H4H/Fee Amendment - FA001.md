# Fee Amendment — FA-001

## Under Master Services Agreement  
### Harmony for Hope, Inc. and Carrie Ann Kidd d/b/a Kidd’s Technical Services

**Amendment Number:** FA-001  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** July 6, 2025  
**Status:** Draft — Structural Rewrite for Board and Counsel Review  
**Related Documents:** Master Services Agreement, SOW-000, SOW-001, FTIS-001, KTS Relationship and Status Memorandum

---

## 1. Purpose

This Fee Amendment (**FA-001**) amends and clarifies the compensation, value-allocation, and technical-support treatment applicable to work performed by **Carrie Ann Kidd d/b/a Kidd’s Technical Services** for **Harmony for Hope, Inc.** under the Master Services Agreement and related Statements of Work.

This amendment is intended to correct and replace any prior drafting logic that could be read to:

- place founder private appreciation inside H4H-administered systems,
- route founder value through EMS or community mutual-credit balances,
- imply that KTS is already a formed limited liability company when it is not,
- or misstate the physical server location associated with H4H operations.

This amendment establishes a cleaner structure: charitable value remains charitable, community instruments remain non-equity, and any appreciating founder-side instrument belongs outside H4H and outside the resident-facing Commons.

---

## 2. Parties and Current Entity Status

This amendment is between **Harmony for Hope, Inc.** and **Carrie Ann Kidd**, an individual doing business as **Kidd’s Technical Services**.

As of the date of this amendment:

- Kidd’s Technical Services is not yet a separately formed West Virginia limited liability company,
- no separate KTS EIN has yet been issued,
- and all rights and obligations described here run to Carrie Ann Kidd personally unless and until a later written assignment, amendment, or novation is executed after lawful entity formation.

Any reference in earlier documents to **Kidd’s Technical Services, LLC** should be treated as requiring conforming correction.

---

## 3. Corrected Location Reference

Any prior reference in contract materials, technical schedules, or fee narratives identifying the relevant server location as **706 Main Street** is corrected to **704 Main Street, Mount Hope, West Virginia**, to match the physical Fayette County Community Arts Center operating site and server-cluster location.

For clarity:

- **706 Main Street** remains the mailing and principal-officer address reflected in records,
- **704 Main Street** is the physical operating location for H4H program activity and server infrastructure,
- and this amendment controls wherever earlier internal fee language implied that the operating server environment was located at 706.

---

## 4. Core Compensation Framework

The parties acknowledge the internal rate framework already used for technical contribution tracking:

- **Fair-market rate:** $300 per hour
- **Charitable in-kind portion to H4H:** $150 per hour
- **Private founder-capital / deferred commercial portion:** $150 per hour

This amendment confirms that the split is conceptual and structural, not a mechanism for converting charitable labor into private ownership through H4H systems.

The charitable half may be recognized as in-kind contribution to H4H. The private-side half, if documented at all, must be documented separately on the founder-private or future KTS side.

---

## 5. No Appreciation Inside Community EMS

This amendment expressly rejects any compensation model in which founder value appreciates through:

- EMS balances,
- community-issued mutual-credit units,
- vaulted EMS constructs,
- participant treasury allocations,
- resident account balances,
- or any similar H4H-administered or Commons-administered instrument.

Community EMS is a non-appreciating, closed-loop mutual-credit instrument of the community layer. It is not a private investment mechanism and not a lawful founder-capital surrogate.

Any appreciating founder-side instrument, if one is later created under counsel-reviewed documentation, must exist solely outside the community system and never in resident hands.

---

## 6. Charitable Portion

The charitable portion of technical work covered by this amendment:

- is contributed to H4H,
- may be used for in-kind support, grant-match support, and internal valuation narratives where appropriate,
- must be recorded consistently with nonprofit accounting treatment,
- and does not create a compensatory ownership claim against H4H.

No charitable in-kind amount may be recharacterized later as equity in H4H, equity in the Commons, or a founder cash-out right through community balances.

---

## 7. Private Founder-Side Portion

The private-side portion of the same technical contribution is not donated to H4H.

That portion:

- remains outside H4H,
- may be documented only through separate founder-side or KTS-side instruments,
- may not be embedded inside resident or community balances,
- and remains personal to Carrie Ann Kidd until a lawful KTS entity is formed and a written transfer or capitalization instrument is executed.

This amendment does not itself create equity, issue securities, grant membership units, or establish a present right to payment based on appreciation.

---

## 8. Future KTS-Side Treatment

The intended long-term structure places appreciating founder-side rights, ALLIS-related commercial ownership, and deferred founder capitalization on the KTS side of the structure.

However, because KTS is not yet formed, any such future treatment remains contingent upon:

- lawful entity formation,
- EIN issuance,
- counsel review,
- formal documentation,
- conflict-of-interest compliance,
- and, where required, board approval or disinterested approval.

Until those steps occur, all language concerning private-side capitalization should be read as describing potential future treatment only, not current legal status.

---

## 9. Treasury Reserve for Formation and Procurement Costs

Where applicable and lawfully authorized under governing MountainShares and H4H transition rules, certain future **Phase 2** costs may be funded from the **Treasury Reserve**.

These may include, as applicable:

- DUNA formation and structuring costs,
- KTS formation costs,
- legal and securities counsel costs,
- valuation and appraisal work,
- filing and compliance costs,
- licensing and procurement costs related to transition architecture,
- and other approved formation or implementation expenses necessary to move from the current H4H-operated structure into the later arm’s-length configuration.

This Treasury Reserve concept applies to transition and procurement costs only where properly authorized. It does not create founder ownership rights inside the community system.

---

## 10. Operational Technology and Infrastructure Support

This amendment applies to fee logic associated with technical labor supporting, among other things:

- software engineering,
- systems architecture,
- server and container operations,
- ALLIS-related deployment,
- geospatial services,
- documentation,
- maintenance,
- and continuity planning.

To the extent such work supports physical or hosted infrastructure associated with H4H operations, the operative site reference for current server deployment is **704 Main Street**.

Nothing in this amendment changes ownership of proprietary ALLIS, GBIM, or related background intellectual property.

---

## 11. Intellectual Property Boundary

This amendment does not transfer ownership of:

- ALLIS,
- GBIM,
- associated proprietary frameworks,
- commercial engine components,
- or background technical materials.

Operational use by H4H under contract is distinct from ownership.

Any broader licensing, commercialization, or proprietary-rights transfer must be addressed in a separate written instrument and, where necessary, after KTS is lawfully formed.

---

## 12. Conflict of Interest and Related-Party Procedure

Because Carrie Ann Kidd may hold roles on both sides of the H4H–Contractor relationship, all approvals, payments, reimbursements, valuation treatments, amendments, and transition-cost authorizations under this amendment require documented conflict-of-interest and recusal procedures.

At minimum, the parties should ensure:

- disclosure of overlapping roles,
- abstention where required,
- disinterested review and approval where required,
- and written record retention sufficient for later audit or legal review.

These procedures are material to the enforceability and defensibility of the fee structure.

---

## 13. Superseding Interpretation

To the extent any prior fee language, appendix, schedule, or narrative suggests that:

- EMS is an appreciating founder asset,
- founder compensation is realized through community mutual-credit appreciation,
- KTS is already a formed LLC,
- or the relevant server location is 706 rather than 704,

that prior language is superseded by this amendment.

From the effective date of this amendment forward, compensation and value-allocation language must be interpreted in accordance with the structural boundaries set out here.

---

## 14. No Equity Created by This Amendment

This amendment does not itself create:

- equity in H4H,
- equity in the MountainShares Commons,
- equity in the DUNA,
- membership units in KTS,
- securities rights,
- participant investment rights,
- or a present cash-payment obligation based on appreciation theory.

Its purpose is corrective, clarifying, and structural.
