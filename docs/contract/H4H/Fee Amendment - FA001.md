## 1. Purpose

This Fee Amendment establishes the cost framework, procurement authority, reimbursement terms, and ownership transfer conditions for hardware required to deploy the MS.MHHWT.ALLIS Heritage Reference Implementation Cluster at 706 Main Street and the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail trailhead kiosk, Mount Hope, West Virginia.

This amendment is issued pursuant to Section 5 of COS-001, which deferred hardware procurement costs to a separate Fee Amendment. It does not alter any other term of SOW:000 or COS-001.

---

## 2. Hardware Scope

The MS.MHHWT.ALLIS Heritage Reference Implementation Cluster requires the following hardware categories. Specific unit models, quantities, and final costs are subject to procurement research, vendor availability, and grant funding timelines. This amendment establishes the framework and cost ceiling; a Hardware Procurement Schedule (HPS-001) will document final selections, purchase orders, and serial numbers upon procurement.

| Category | Description | Deployment Location | Status |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| **Primary Intelligence Node** | Local-first AI/ML inference hardware; edge compute capable of running Ms. Jarvis GBIM inference locally without cloud dependency | 706 Main Street interior | Specification complete; procurement pending FA-001 execution |

| **Trailhead Heritage Kiosk Node** | Weatherproof outdoor-rated compute and display hardware appropriate for trailhead public-facing interaction; vandal-resistant enclosure | Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail trailhead kiosk, bank parking lot | Specification complete; site access pending H4H / City of Mount Hope written permission per COS-001 Section 6.2 |
| **Power Infrastructure** | UPS, surge protection, and power conditioning appropriate to each node location | 706 Main Street + trailhead kiosk | Site assessment required prior to procurement |

| **Spare and Redundancy Components** | Critical spare components identified during heritage node cluster design phase to ensure operational continuity without extended procurement delays | KTS inventory | To be specified in HPS-001 |

## 3. Cost Ceiling and Funding Source

### 3.1 Estimated Cost Ceiling

The estimated hardware procurement cost for the full MS.MHHWT.ALLIS Heritage Reference Implementation Cluster is:

| Scenario | Estimated Range | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Minimum viable deployment | $8,000 – $12,000 | Primary node + SKLink + basic trailhead heritage kiosk hardware |
| Full heritage reference implementation | $15,000 – $25,000 | All categories above, weatherproof enclosures, redundancy components |
| Extended corridor-ready deployment | $25,000 – $40,000 | Full heritage implementation + corridor extension infrastructure pre-positioned |

**Cost ceiling for this amendment: $40,000.**

No procurement expenditure under this amendment may exceed $40,000 in aggregate without a written amendment to this FA-001 executed by both parties.

### 3.2 Funding Source

Hardware procurement under this amendment is contingent on funding availability. Authorized funding sources, in priority order:

1. **Grant award proceeds** specifically designated for hardware, technology infrastructure, or heritage ALLIS deployment — primary intended funding source
2. **H4H restricted fund** established for MountainShares / The Commons infrastructure, if available
3. **KTS advance procurement** — KTS may, at its sole discretion, advance procurement costs on H4H's behalf pending grant funding; any such advance is documented in HPS-001 and reimbursed to KTS from the first available grant disbursement designated for hardware

### 3.3 No Obligation Without Funding

H4H has no obligation to authorize hardware procurement under this amendment until a funding source in Section 3.2 is confirmed in writing. KTS has no obligation to advance procurement costs. This amendment establishes the framework; procurement fires only when funding is confirmed.

## 4. Procurement Process

### 4.1 Authorization Sequence

1. Funding source confirmed in writing by H4H Board or designated officer
2. KTS submits Hardware Procurement Schedule (HPS-001) to H4H for review — itemized list of proposed hardware, vendors, unit costs, and total procurement cost
3. H4H provides written approval of HPS-001 within 10 business days of submission
4. KTS proceeds with procurement upon written approval
5. All purchase receipts and documentation filed in the governance record as HPS-001 Exhibit A

### 4.2 Vendor Selection

KTS is responsible for vendor identification and selection. KTS shall:

- Obtain a minimum of two price quotes for any single item exceeding $1,000
- Prioritize vendors with documented availability and warranty support appropriate to a multi-year heritage deployment
- Avoid vendors whose supply chain or support terms create long-term operational risk for the heritage reference implementation

### 4.3 Procurement Timeline

Hardware procurement shall not commence before the later of:

- Written H4H approval of HPS-001 per Section 4.1; or
- October 1, 2027 (aligned with COS-001 hardware procurement milestone of October 31, 2027)

KTS may begin vendor research and quote solicitation prior to this date at its own discretion and cost.

---

## 5. Hardware Ownership and Transfer

### 5.1 KTS Ownership During Procurement

KTS retains full ownership of all hardware procured under this amendment from the date of purchase until full reimbursement has been received from H4H or confirmed grant proceeds, consistent with COS-001 Section 6.3.

### 5.2 Ownership Transfer to H4H

Ownership of all hardware procured under this amendment transfers to H4H upon:

- Full reimbursement of KTS procurement costs by H4H or confirmed grant disbursement to KTS; and
- Execution of a Hardware Transfer Receipt (HTR-001) by both parties documenting serial numbers, condition, and transfer date

### 5.3 Transfer Does Not Affect Operations

Hardware ownership transfer to H4H does not affect KTS's operational authority over the MS.MHHWT.ALLIS Heritage Reference Implementation Cluster during the engagement term defined in COS-001. KTS retains full operational and configuration authority over all deployed heritage node cluster hardware through the COS-001 end date of November 27, 2028, regardless of ownership status.

---

## 6. Insurance and Risk

### 6.1 During KTS Ownership

While hardware is owned by KTS, KTS is responsible for insuring it against loss, theft, and damage during storage and transit. KTS's existing business property coverage applies.

### 6.2 After Transfer to H4H

Following ownership transfer, H4H is responsible for insuring all heritage node cluster hardware against loss, theft, and damage. H4H shall add deployed ALLIS heritage node hardware to its property insurance coverage within 30 days of HTR-001 execution.

### 6.3 Outdoor Heritage Node Risk

The trailhead heritage kiosk node is deployed in a public outdoor environment. H4H acknowledges the elevated risk of vandalism, weather damage, and theft for outdoor-deployed heritage nodes and accepts responsibility for that risk following ownership transfer. KTS will specify appropriate weatherproof and vandal-resistant hardware in HPS-001 to mitigate but not eliminate this risk.

---

## 7. Relationship to MountainShares Treasury

Hardware procurement costs under this amendment are **not** funded from the MountainShares treasury reserve. They are funded from grant proceeds or H4H restricted infrastructure funds as defined in Section 3.2. The MountainShares treasury reserve is not a capital expenditure fund and shall not be drawn upon for hardware procurement under any circumstances.

This separation is intentional. The reserve exists to back outstanding M$ and sustain the phase thresholds defined in the Phase Specifications. Infrastructure capital costs — including heritage ALLIS node cluster hardware — are the responsibility of H4H's grant and operational funding streams, not the commons treasury.

---

## 8. Reporting and Transparency

All hardware procured under this amendment shall be:

- Documented in HPS-001 with full itemization, vendor, cost, and serial number
- Reported in the annual H4H governance and financial report under the MountainShares / The Commons infrastructure section
- Referenced in the MountainShares Pre-Launch Audit Report (Phase 0 Audit Template) as confirmed heritage node cluster infrastructure
