## Overview

This document serves as the operational grant planning calendar for the H4H/KTS corridor initiative covering the period 2026–2028. It maps the preparation milestones, foundation relationship-building activities, and federal grant application cycles necessary to move the MS.___.ALLIS mesh pilot from a locally endorsed community initiative to a fully funded regional infrastructure program.

The strategy is sequenced deliberately: build the institutional endorsement stack first, establish foundation relationships second, and enter competitive federal grant cycles third — with each layer strengthening the one that follows.

## Phase 2 — First Grant Applications (January–May 2027)

**Goal:** Submit two entry-level grants to establish funder relationships and secure infrastructure/data funding before the ARC POWER cycle opens.

### RWJF Local Data for Equitable Communities

**Award:** Up to $50,000 | **Performance period:** 9 months | **Up to 30 awards nationally**

The Robert Wood Johnson Foundation's Local Data for Equitable Communities program funds organizations building community data projects from existing relationships and change efforts. The ALLIS GIS data layer and community resource mapping component is a direct fit — locally hosted, community-controlled, built on existing H4H relationships in Fayette County.

This is the **first foundation relationship builder** — a successful RWJF award opens the door to RWJF's larger health equity and community resilience portfolios.

| Milestone | Projected FY2027 Date |
|---|---|
| CFP launches | ~January 27, 2027 |
| Optional applicant webinar | ~February 5, 2027 |
| **Full proposals due** | **~March 3, 2027 (3 p.m. ET)** |
| Awardees notified | ~May 11, 2027 |
| Performance period starts | ~July 1, 2027 |

**Key requirements:** Community-based organization, existing data infrastructure, demonstrated community relationships, focus on equitable data access.

**Application anchor:** GIS community resource mapping layer of ALLIS + H4H community relationships in Mount Hope + Fayette County.

🔗 https://www.rwjf.org/en/grants/active-funding-opportunities/2026/local-data-for-equitable-communities.html

### USDA Community Connect Grant

**Award:** $50,000–$300,000 | **Cash match:** 15% required | **Eligibility:** Rural areas lacking 10Mbps/1Mbps service

The USDA Community Connect program funds broadband deployment in rural communities without adequate connectivity. Fayette County rural areas almost certainly qualify on connectivity thresholds. This grant funds the **physical infrastructure layer** of the ALLIS mesh — radios, enclosures, satellite terminal, and backbone hardware — making it a natural complement to the software and governance work already documented.

| Milestone | Projected FY2027 Date |
|---|---|
| Application portal opens | ~February 20, 2027 |
| **Application deadline** | **~April 21, 2027** |

**Key requirements:** Rural eligibility certification, 15% cash match, community anchor institution partnership, free public access requirement.

**Application anchor:** Mount Hope and Thurmond node deployments + NPS partnership + municipal endorsement + 15% cash match through H4H/MountainShares.

🔗 https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/telecommunications-programs/community-connect-grants

## Phase 3 — ARC POWER Anchor Application (May–July 2027)

**Goal:** Submit the primary federal grant application with the complete endorsement stack, university partnership, and two prior grant relationships already established.

### ARC POWER Initiative — Partnerships for Opportunity and Workforce and Economic Revitalization

**Award:** $400,000–$2,500,000 | **Planning grants:** up to $50,000 separately available

The ARC POWER Initiative is the anchor grant for this project and the one for which all prior phases are preparation. POWER funds economic revitalization projects in Appalachian coal communities — Fayette County and the New River Gorge corridor are directly in scope. Multi-partner applications with university involvement and federal agency endorsement score significantly higher.

That is not a typical POWER application. That is a corridor-scale initiative with documented multi-sector alignment.

## Notes on Strategy

**Why this sequence works:** Each phase builds evidence for the next. The LOS stack proves community endorsement. The RWJF grant proves foundation trust. The USDA grant proves infrastructure readiness. All three together make the ARC POWER application nearly self-evident to reviewers.

**Why the repo matters:** Federal agencies and foundations increasingly perform due diligence by searching for an organization's public presence. A well-organized, versioned, publicly accessible governance repository is evidence of organizational maturity that grant narratives alone cannot convey.

**Why university partnership matters:** ARC POWER scores higher for multi-partner applications. Marshall University as a co-applicant adds institutional credibility, access to research grant streams, and student workforce components that strengthen the economic revitalization narrative.

**Why Humanity AI is a long game:** With $500M over five years and ten of the nation's largest foundations aligned behind it, Humanity AI represents the most significant philanthropic commitment to community-centered AI ever assembled. Getting on their radar in 2026 — before competitive calls open — positions H4H/KTS as a practitioner they discover, not an applicant they screen.
