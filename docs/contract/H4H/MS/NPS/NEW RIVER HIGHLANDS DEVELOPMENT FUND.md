## ABOUT YOUR ORGANIZATION

Harmony for Hope, Inc. (H4H) is a Fayette County–based nonprofit founded in 2017 and headquartered at 704 & 708 Main Street in Mount Hope. H4H's mission is to unite West Virginia through music, art, and Appalachian heritage through technology.

H4H operates two active community assets in Mount Hope: the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail — a 1.25-mile heritage trail through sixteen points of architectural significance, supported by 48 interpretive signs and currently generating 65 documented monthly visitors through Google discovery alone — and the Fayette County Community Arts Center (FCCAC) at 704 Main Street, an active cultural programming facility supporting a resident artist and serving the wider Fayette County region.

H4H also operates MountainShares Commons, a community-serving local economy and heritage platform operated under a no-cost license and currently live at https://egeria.mountainshares.us, and sponsors the MountainShares community deployment tier for the Artificial Learning & Location Intelligence System (ALLIS) — a locally hosted mesh intelligence platform operated under a no-cost license and currently live and in active testing.

## PROJECT DESCRIPTION

### The Economic Development Problem

New River Gorge National Park and Preserve is one of the fastest-growing national parks in the United States. Its designation as a national park accelerated tourism growth across southern West Virginia — and Fayette County sits at the center of that growth corridor. Visitors arrive by the hundreds of thousands annually to hike, climb, raft, and explore.

They move through gateway towns like Mount Hope on the way. And when they get there, they encounter an infrastructure gap that costs Fayette County real economic activity every year.

Visitors arriving in Mount Hope — a coalfield community with a documented 1.25-mile heritage trail, 48 interpretive signs, a historic main street, and a community arts center — have almost no reliable connectivity. They cannot load trail maps. They cannot access the interpretive content that would extend their visit and deepen their spending. They cannot discover the businesses, events, and experiences that would bring them back. The heritage assets exist. The visitors are coming. The infrastructure to connect them is missing.

This is not a cultural problem. It is an economic development problem. Every visitor who arrives in Mount Hope without connectivity and leaves without engaging the trail, the arts center, or Main Street is a lost economic transaction — for local businesses, for the tax base, and for the regional tourism economy that Fayette County depends on.

### The Solution: Heritage Connectivity Infrastructure as Economic Infrastructure

The Mount Hope Heritage Connectivity Infrastructure project installs the physical backbone that converts Mount Hope's existing heritage assets into a functioning economic development node on the New River Gorge tourism corridor.

The system has three economic functions:

**1. Visitor capture and extension.**
A visitor who connects to the locally hosted ALLIS node at the Historic Walking Trail kiosk receives trail maps, heritage interpretation, and — critically — information about businesses, events, and services in Mount Hope and the surrounding corridor. Connectivity at the trailhead is the difference between a visitor who walks the trail and leaves, and a visitor who walks the trail, eats lunch on Main Street, and plans a return trip.

**2. Tourism infrastructure for the corridor.**
Mount Hope is the pilot node in a planned corridor-scale mesh serving New River Gorge gateway communities including Fayetteville, Oak Hill, Hinton, Thurmond, and Ansted. The infrastructure deployed in Mount Hope with this grant is the reference implementation that demonstrates the model to every other gateway community in the nine-county region — and positions Fayette County as the economic development leader of the corridor.

**3. Leverage for significantly larger investment.**
This $9,500 grant, combined with a concurrent Greater Mount Hope Community Fund grant, completes the hardware and installation budget for the Mount Hope node. That completed node is the documented proof-of-concept that supports H4H's pending applications to the Appalachian Regional Commission, USDA Rural Development, and the Economic Development Administration for corridor-scale expansion. The New River Highlands Development Fund's investment directly enables those larger economic development grants to move forward.
