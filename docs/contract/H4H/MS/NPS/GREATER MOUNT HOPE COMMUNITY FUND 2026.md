## ABOUT YOUR ORGANIZATION

### Organizational Narrative

Harmony for Hope, Inc. is a Mount Hope-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit serving southern West Virginia through music, art, Appalachian heritage, public education, and community-centered technology. The organization’s IRS mailing address is 706 Main Street in Mount Hope, and its current project work is centered on the 704–708 Main Street block, including the Fayette County Community Arts Center and related Main Street heritage programming.

Harmony for Hope was created to preserve local culture while building practical community infrastructure around it. In Mount Hope, that work is not abstract. It includes the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail, the Fayette County Community Arts Center, local arts and heritage programming, and the development of MountainShares Commons as a community-serving heritage and local information platform.

Harmony for Hope’s major programs and community assets include:

The Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail — a 1.25-mile self-guided heritage trail through sixteen architectural and historical points of interest. The trail helps residents and visitors understand Mount Hope’s story from Indigenous pathways and early settlement, through the coal and rail era, the Fire of 1910, company-town development, labor history, architecture, and the community’s ongoing work to preserve and revitalize Main Street. The trail is supported by printed materials and interpretive signage and is already discoverable through online search and heritage platforms.

The Fayette County Community Arts Center — a Main Street cultural anchor located at 704 Main Street. The center supports local arts, heritage interpretation, community programming, and the long-term preservation of Mount Hope’s historic downtown corridor.

MountainShares Commons — a community-serving local economy, heritage, and information platform developed to help local communities share stories, resources, events, services, and place-based information in ways that remain rooted in local governance and local benefit.

ALLIS — the Artificial Learning & Location Intelligence System — a locally hosted, location-aware community information platform developed and supported by Kidd’s Technical Services and made available to Harmony for Hope for community deployment. For this project, ALLIS is not being presented as a finished commercial product or a replacement for existing public systems. It is a carefully scoped pilot tool for locally hosted heritage content, visitor information, resident-facing updates, and community resilience testing in a real Mount Hope setting.

Harmony for Hope’s role in this project is to serve as the nonprofit applicant, community sponsor, heritage content steward, and local implementation lead. Kidd’s Technical Services provides technical infrastructure, no-cost platform access, hardware specification, documentation, and technical support under the existing Harmony for Hope–Kidd’s Technical Services relationship, with conflict-of-interest governance handled through Harmony for Hope’s board processes.

This project has also received concept-stage and professional support from outside partners, including New River Gorge National Park and Preserve, Starry Eyes Media, the Town of Thurmond, and Dr. Anurag K. Srivastava of West Virginia University in his personal professional capacity. These letters support continued exploration, grant development, and responsible pilot testing. They do not remove the need for permitting, compliance, technical review, or future site-specific approvals.

## NEED / PROBLEM / OPPORTUNITY

Mount Hope has already done much of the hardest heritage work. The story exists. The trail exists. The buildings still stand. The community has preserved a Main Street with deep architectural, coalfield, rail, labor, civic, and cultural significance. The Fayette County Community Arts Center is already operating as a cultural anchor, and the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail already gives residents and visitors a way to move through town and encounter that history.

The missing piece is infrastructure.

At present, Mount Hope’s heritage experience still depends heavily on printed signs, printed brochures, scattered online listings, and whatever cellular service a visitor happens to have. Residents face a similar problem from the other direction: there is no single local information layer tied to the Main Street corridor that can carry heritage content, community updates, event information, safety notices, and locally relevant resources from the same trusted place.

This project addresses that gap by installing a small but durable community connectivity backbone at the Fayette County Community Arts Center and the Historic Walking Trail kiosk. The system will allow Harmony for Hope to host Mount Hope heritage content locally, connect trail users to deeper interpretation, support a public-facing landing page for the trail and arts center, and test a low-power backup communication layer for community information during service disruptions.

The opportunity is timely for four reasons.

First, Mount Hope is a gateway community within the broader New River Gorge region. Visitors are already searching for local heritage experiences, and the New River Gorge corridor continues to draw regional and national attention. Mount Hope has the history and location to benefit from that attention, but it needs better local infrastructure to turn visitor interest into community value.

Second, the Fayette County Community Arts Center is the right physical anchor. The center sits on Main Street, near the Historic Walking Trail, inside the community this project is meant to serve. It can function as the backbone location for locally hosted heritage content, community information, and project coordination.

Third, the project is resident-first. This is not a tourism project that happens to mention residents. It is a community infrastructure project that also improves the visitor experience. Residents benefit through stronger local communications, a better-supported arts center, more visible community resources, and a heritage system that keeps Mount Hope’s story under local stewardship.

Fourth, this is a practical pilot with defined limits. The grant request does not ask the Greater Mount Hope Community Fund to fund a broad regional concept. It asks the fund to install specific equipment in Mount Hope, build a specific public-facing landing page for Mount Hope, support a specific heritage trail node in Mount Hope, and create measurable outcomes by the end of the grant period.

The Greater Mount Hope Community Fund is the right match because the project is local, visible, durable, and rooted in existing community assets. The equipment remains in service beyond the grant period, the content continues to grow, and the benefits stay centered in Mount Hope.

## PROJECT DETAILS

The Mount Hope Heritage & Community Connectivity Infrastructure project will install and activate the physical, digital, administrative, and governance backbone needed to connect the Fayette County Community Arts Center, the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail, and the Main Street corridor as a living heritage and community information district.

The project includes eight core components.

### Component 1 — Local ALLIS Server at the Fayette County Community Arts Center

A small local server will be installed at the Fayette County Community Arts Center. This server will host Mount Hope-specific heritage content, trail interpretation, local resource information, and resident-facing community updates. The purpose is to keep important local content available from within the community itself rather than relying only on distant cloud services or scattered social media posts.

The server will support the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail content layer, community information posts, and testing of the MS.MHHWT.ALLIS node for the Mount Hope trail deployment.

### Component 2 — Starlink Connectivity at the Fayette County Community Arts Center

Starlink equipment will be installed to provide reliable project connectivity at the Fayette County Community Arts Center. This connection will support the local server, content updates, the public-facing landing page workflow, and the heritage and community information tools used by Harmony for Hope.

This is not presented as a replacement for commercial broadband or public emergency systems. It is a practical connectivity layer for the arts center, the heritage trail project, and the local information infrastructure being built through this grant.

### Component 3 — Trailhead Kiosk Connectivity and Solar Support

A small trailhead node will be installed at or near the Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail kiosk, subject to site control, property-owner permission, safety review, and any required municipal or local approval. The current kiosk is already part of the visitor experience because trail users encounter printed information there. This component extends that experience by connecting visitors to locally hosted digital content.

The kiosk component includes a small device, weather-resistant housing, signage or instructions for connecting, and a solar power system designed to support basic operation without relying entirely on building power.

### Component 4 — Low-Power LoRa Mesh Backup Layer

A low-power LoRa mesh backup layer will be installed and tested along the Main Street project area. LoRa is not a replacement for cell service, internet service, 911, emergency management, or official public alert systems. Its purpose in this project is narrower and more practical: to test a resilient, low-bandwidth communication layer for project status messages, basic local information, system health, and community notices during ordinary service disruptions.

Coalfield communities regularly experience weather-related power and communication challenges. A low-power backup layer gives Harmony for Hope a way to test how local information can continue moving when conventional systems are limited.

### Component 5 — Public Website and Landing Page by Starry Eyes Media

Starry Eyes Media, a West Virginia-based communications and digital infrastructure partner, will develop a public-facing website or landing page for the Mount Hope heritage and connectivity project. This page will serve as the digital front door for the Historic Walking Trail, the Fayette County Community Arts Center, and the ALLIS content layer.

The landing page will help residents and visitors find the trail, understand the project, access community information, and connect with Harmony for Hope programming. It will also support measurement through search and visitor analytics.

### Component 6 — Professional Electrical Installation and Site Preparation

The project includes professional installation support for indoor and outdoor equipment, including review of the kiosk location, safe mounting, weatherproofing, cable management, solar and battery installation, grounding and basic electrical safety, and contingency for site conditions discovered during installation.

Because this project includes outdoor equipment, solar power, battery storage, and public-facing hardware, professional installation is necessary. Harmony for Hope will not install hardware at any site without appropriate permission, safety review, and required approvals.

### Component 7 — Project Management and Grant Administration

This project requires active coordination from award through completion. Project management will include procurement coordination, installation scheduling, partner communication, documentation, reporting, community feedback collection, board coordination, grant compliance tracking, and final outcome reporting.

This component ensures the project is not only purchased, but properly implemented, documented, and sustained. It also provides the administrative structure necessary to manage a project involving nonprofit governance, technical infrastructure, public-facing equipment, community partners, and measurable grant outcomes.

### Component 8 — Legal Review, Governance, and Compliance Support

The project includes a legal counsel retainer for project-specific governance, compliance, agreements, data stewardship, intellectual property review, site permissions, partner documentation, and risk management.

This legal support is preventive and administrative. It is not for litigation. Because the project involves nonprofit governance, a related technical services provider, public-facing infrastructure, locally generated content, possible municipal coordination, and future corridor partnerships, legal review is necessary to protect Harmony for Hope, the community, participating partners, and the long-term integrity of the project.

## PARTICIPANT FOCUS

This project serves two overlapping groups: Mount Hope residents and Mount Hope visitors. The primary beneficiary is the Mount Hope community.

### Primary Participants — Mount Hope Residents

Mount Hope residents benefit because the project strengthens a local cultural anchor, improves the community’s ability to share local information, and gives the Historic Walking Trail and Main Street a stronger public presence. Residents will be able to access community updates, local resources, event information, arts center programming, and heritage content through a locally rooted system.

The project also supports residents indirectly by strengthening Main Street’s identity as a heritage and cultural district. A better-supported walking trail and arts center can help increase community pride, improve public awareness of local history, support small businesses, and create new reasons for residents to gather, participate, and volunteer.

Residents across all age groups may benefit, including youth learning local history, adults participating in community programs, business owners seeking visibility, and older residents who value local information and preservation.

### Secondary Participants — Heritage Visitors and Trail Users

Visitors already come to Mount Hope and search for heritage experiences. The Mount Hope Historic Walking Trail gives them a reason to stop, walk, learn, and spend time in the community. This project gives those visitors a deeper and more complete experience.

Instead of encountering only printed signs and brochures, visitors will be able to connect to locally hosted interpretation about the Fire of 1910, coal and rail history, the architectural significance of Main Street, the labor and community stories that shaped Mount Hope, and current community resources.

This improves the visitor experience while keeping Mount Hope’s story under local stewardship.

### Project Principle

This is a community infrastructure project that also improves tourism. It is not a tourism project that leaves residents behind.
