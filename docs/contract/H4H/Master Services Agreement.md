## 1. Parties

This Master Services Agreement (the **Agreement**) is entered into by and between **Harmony for Hope, Inc.**, a West Virginia nonprofit corporation and 501(c)(3) public charity (**H4H**), and **Carrie Ann Kidd**, an individual doing business as Kidd’s Technical Services (**Contractor**).

The parties acknowledge that, as of the date of this Agreement, Kidd’s Technical Services is a trade name and not yet a separately formed West Virginia limited liability company. All rights and obligations of the Contractor under this Agreement therefore run directly to Carrie Ann Kidd unless and until this Agreement is formally assigned to a duly formed successor entity by written amendment.

---

## 2. Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to establish the general legal and operational framework under which Contractor may provide technical, architectural, software, systems, geospatial, documentation, infrastructure, and related professional services to H4H.

This Agreement is intended to support H4H’s charitable and programmatic work, including the planning, development, implementation, and maintenance of MountainShares-related systems and associated community technology infrastructure.

This Agreement does not create any equity interest, ownership right, or investor claim in H4H, the MountainShares Commons, the DUNA, or any participant account system.

---

## 3. Scope of Master Relationship

This Agreement governs the overall relationship between the parties. Specific work shall be authorized through one or more written Statements of Work (**SOWs**) executed by the parties.

Each SOW may define, as applicable:

- services to be performed,
- timelines and milestones,
- deliverables,
- acceptance criteria,
- compensation terms,
- expense treatment,
- data responsibilities,
- support obligations,
- and project-specific legal or operational requirements.

If an SOW conflicts with this Agreement, the SOW controls only for the specific subject matter expressly addressed in that SOW. In all other respects, this Agreement controls.

---

## 4. Services

Contractor may provide services including, without limitation:

- software architecture and engineering,
- systems administration,
- database and geospatial infrastructure design,
- data integration and ETL work,
- AI and reasoning-system development,
- documentation and technical writing,
- deployment and maintenance of community systems,
- product and interface design,
- grant-related technical implementation support,
- and strategic technical advisory services.

Services may support H4H charitable operations, MountainShares program infrastructure, the MountainShares Commons, partner integrations, and other H4H-approved projects described in applicable SOWs.

Contractor shall perform services in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with the standard of care ordinarily exercised by similarly situated technical professionals performing comparable work.

---

## 5. Compensation

### 5.1 General Rule

Compensation shall be defined in the applicable SOW or written amendment.

Compensation may include one or more of the following, as expressly approved in writing:

- cash fees,
- deferred payment obligations,
- charitable in-kind contribution recognition,
- reimbursable expenses,
- or other lawful compensation structures consistent with H4H’s nonprofit status.

### 5.2 No Equity Through H4H

No compensation under this Agreement shall be construed to create founder equity inside H4H, inside EMS, inside the MountainShares Commons, or through any participant-facing DAO or treasury mechanism.

Any founder-capital, deferred equity, appreciation-based instrument, or private commercial participation right associated with Contractor’s work shall be documented separately through KTS-side or founder-side instruments and not through this Agreement.

### 5.3 In-Kind and Deferred Structures

Where the parties agree that a portion of Contractor’s work is contributed as charitable in-kind support to H4H, that contributed portion shall be documented separately in H4H’s books and records in accordance with applicable accounting and nonprofit rules.

Where the parties agree that a separate portion of Contractor’s work is associated with private founder capital or deferred commercial participation, that portion shall be documented through separate instruments and shall not be treated as charitable compensation through H4H.

This Agreement may coexist with both categories, but it does not itself convert charitable contribution into founder equity.

---

## 6. Statements of Work and Deliverables

Each SOW shall specify the deliverables, if any, required for that project.

Deliverables may include:

- software,
- configurations,
- infrastructure setups,
- APIs,
- documentation,
- schemas,
- geospatial data products,
- dashboards,
- deployment scripts,
- operational procedures,
- or strategic plans.

Acceptance procedures, testing standards, revision windows, and operational support periods may be set forth in the applicable SOW.

Unless otherwise specified in an SOW, H4H shall review submitted deliverables within a commercially reasonable period and either:

- accept the deliverable,
- identify reasonable requested revisions,
- or provide written notice that the deliverable materially fails to satisfy the applicable SOW.

---

## 7. Intellectual Property

### 7.1 Background IP

Each party retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property, tools, systems, methods, know-how, templates, trade secrets, documentation, and proprietary materials developed independently of this Agreement.

Contractor’s retained background intellectual property includes, as applicable, ALLIS, GBIM, related reasoning systems, proprietary models, software frameworks, system architecture, and associated technical materials, except to the extent expressly transferred by separate signed agreement.

### 7.2 Project Deliverables

Unless otherwise stated in an applicable SOW, H4H receives a non-exclusive right to use the deliverables created for its internal charitable and programmatic purposes.

If the parties intend for specific deliverables to be assigned to H4H, jointly owned, or licensed on broader terms, that treatment must be stated expressly in the applicable SOW or separate written instrument.

### 7.3 Licensing Structure

Where H4H uses Contractor-owned or KTS-owned proprietary systems, including ALLIS or GBIM, such use shall occur under license rather than by implied transfer of ownership.

Any long-term production use by H4H, the MountainShares Commons, or the DUNA of proprietary Contractor systems may be governed by separate licensing, hosting, maintenance, or support agreements in addition to this Agreement.

### 7.4 No Implied Transfer

Except as expressly stated in a signed writing, no provision of this Agreement transfers ownership of Contractor’s underlying proprietary systems, commercial engine, or private founder-capital rights to H4H.

---

## 8. Data, Access, and Security

Contractor may receive access to systems, records, accounts, data, facilities, and infrastructure reasonably necessary to perform the services.

Contractor shall:

- use such access only for authorized project purposes,
- protect confidential and sensitive information,
- follow reasonable security procedures provided by H4H,
- and return or securely destroy H4H-controlled materials upon request or termination, subject to retention obligations and backup practices.

Where services involve participant data, community information, or systems touching benefits-sensitive use cases, the parties shall follow any applicable privacy, security, and governance policies adopted by H4H or the MountainShares Commons.

---

## 9. Independent Contractor Status

Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, joint venturer, or agent of H4H except to the limited extent expressly authorized in writing.

Contractor is solely responsible for:

- taxes arising from payments made under this Agreement,
- business registrations and filings applicable to Contractor,
- insurance maintained by Contractor unless otherwise agreed,
- and employment obligations with respect to any assistants, subcontractors, or workers engaged by Contractor.

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create a partnership, joint venture, or equity relationship between the parties.

---

## 10. Conflict of Interest and Related-Party Review

The parties acknowledge that Carrie Ann Kidd may serve in roles related to H4H, MountainShares, the Commons, and Contractor activities.

Accordingly:

- any related-party transaction under this Agreement shall be subject to documented conflict-of-interest and recusal procedures,
- H4H board or committee approvals shall be obtained where required,
- and Carrie Ann Kidd shall abstain from approval decisions where fiduciary duty, nonprofit rules, or policy require recusal.

The parties further acknowledge that related-party compliance is a material term of this Agreement.

---

## 11. Representations and Warranties

Contractor represents and warrants that:

- Contractor has the authority to enter into this Agreement in an individual capacity,
- Contractor will perform the services in a professional and lawful manner,
- and Contractor will not knowingly infringe third-party intellectual property in materials specifically created and delivered under an SOW, except to the extent H4H directs use of third-party materials or systems.

H4H represents and warrants that:

- H4H has authority to enter into this Agreement,
- and H4H will use the services and deliverables in accordance with applicable law and the governing terms of its programs.

Except as expressly stated in this Agreement, no additional warranties are made, and services may involve experimental, developmental, or evolving technical systems.

---

## 12. Confidentiality

Each party may receive confidential or proprietary information from the other in connection with the services.

Each party agrees to:

- use confidential information only for purposes of performing or administering this Agreement,
- protect such information using reasonable care,
- not disclose such information except to those with a need to know and appropriate confidentiality obligations,
- and comply with lawful disclosure requirements where applicable.

Confidential information does not include information that:

- is or becomes public without breach,
- was already lawfully known,
- is independently developed without misuse,
- or is lawfully received from a third party without confidentiality restriction.

---

## 13. Term and Termination

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and remains in force until terminated in accordance with this section.

Either party may terminate this Agreement:

- for convenience upon written notice,
- for material breach if the breach is not cured within the specified notice period,
- or immediately where continued performance would create legal, fiduciary, safety, or regulatory risk.

Termination of this Agreement does not automatically terminate any rights or obligations that by their nature survive termination, including:

- payment obligations accrued before termination,
- confidentiality,
- intellectual property provisions,
- dispute provisions,
- and any SOW-specific survival terms.

Any active SOW may be completed, wound down, or separately terminated as provided in the SOW or by written agreement.

---

## 14. Assignment and Future Entity Formation

Contractor may not assign this Agreement without H4H’s prior written consent, except that, upon the lawful formation of Kidd’s Technical Services as a separate entity, the parties may assign or novate this Agreement to that entity by written amendment or replacement agreement.

Any such assignment or novation shall:

- expressly identify the successor entity,
- confirm assumption of obligations,
- preserve H4H’s rights against pre-assignment performance unless otherwise agreed,
- and maintain all conflict-of-interest and nonprofit compliance safeguards.

Until such formal assignment occurs, all Contractor obligations remain personal to Carrie Ann Kidd.

---

## 15. Liability and Risk Allocation

Neither party shall be liable to the other for indirect, incidental, special, or consequential damages arising from this Agreement, except where prohibited by law or arising from fraud, willful misconduct, confidentiality breach, or unlawful misuse of intellectual property.

Because some services may involve early-stage systems, research, experimental deployment, or evolving infrastructure, the parties acknowledge that prudent staging, testing, governance review, and reserve safety controls are essential operational safeguards.

Nothing in this Agreement shall require H4H to take actions that would jeopardize its nonprofit status, charitable mission, or legal compliance.

---

## 16. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of West Virginia, except to the extent federal law controls a specific issue.

The parties shall first attempt in good faith to resolve disputes informally through written notice and designated representatives.

If a dispute is not resolved informally, the parties may pursue mediation, equitable relief, or other lawful remedies as appropriate to the matter.

---

## 17. General Provisions

- This Agreement constitutes the master understanding between the parties regarding the subject matter addressed herein and supersedes prior inconsistent oral understandings on that subject.
- Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
- Failure to enforce any provision is not a waiver of future enforcement.
- If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect to the maximum extent permitted by law.
- Headings are for convenience only and do not control interpretation.
