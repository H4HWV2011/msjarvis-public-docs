### Harmony for Hope, Inc. and Carrie Ann Kidd d/b/a Kidd’s Technical Services

**SOW Number:** SOW-000  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** July 6, 2025  
**Status:** Draft — For Board and Counsel Review  
**Master Agreement:** Master Services Agreement between Harmony for Hope, Inc. and Carrie Ann Kidd d/b/a Kidd’s Technical Services  
**Project Phase:** Pre-Formation / Early Infrastructure and Program Buildout

---

## 1. Parties

This Statement of Work (**SOW-000**) is entered into by and between **Harmony for Hope, Inc.** (**H4H**) and **Carrie Ann Kidd**, an individual doing business as **Kidd’s Technical Services** (**Contractor**), pursuant to the Master Services Agreement between the parties.

The parties acknowledge that, as of the date of this SOW, Kidd’s Technical Services is not yet a separately formed limited liability company. Accordingly, all work performed under this SOW is performed by Carrie Ann Kidd personally unless and until a formal written assignment or novation is executed.

---

## 2. Purpose

The purpose of this SOW is to define the initial technical, architectural, operational, and documentation services to be provided in support of H4H’s charitable programs and early-stage MountainShares system development.

This SOW is intended to cover the foundational buildout required to design, stand up, document, and stabilize the initial operating environment for:

- MountainShares program infrastructure,
- community-facing technical systems,
- ALLIS-enabled support systems,
- geospatial and data services,
- and associated nonprofit operational technology.

This SOW does not create any founder equity, member equity, DAO equity, or participant ownership rights.

---

## 3. Scope of Services

Contractor shall provide foundational professional services that may include, without limitation:

- systems architecture and planning,
- infrastructure design and deployment,
- server setup and maintenance,
- software engineering and application configuration,
- API and integration development,
- geospatial systems design and implementation,
- data modeling, ETL, and database work,
- documentation and operational writing,
- technical support for H4H program execution,
- security, backup, continuity, and recovery planning,
- and technical project management necessary to support early program launch.

Services under this SOW may support both immediate H4H operational needs and the broader MountainShares system architecture, provided that all community-facing work remains consistent with H4H’s charitable purpose and the non-equity structure of the Commons.

---

## 4. Initial Workstreams

The initial work under this SOW may include the following workstreams:

### 4.1 Infrastructure and Hosting

- Stand up and maintain core server and application infrastructure.
- Configure compute, storage, container, and database environments.
- Establish baseline monitoring, backup, and operational continuity procedures.
- Support infrastructure located at or associated with H4H’s physical operating site at 704 Main Street, Mount Hope, West Virginia, together with any approved remote or supplemental systems.

### 4.2 MountainShares System Buildout

- Design and implement foundational components for the MountainShares Commons.
- Support EMS and M$ system logic at the technical implementation level, subject to governing rules and nonprofit/legal constraints.
- Build or maintain participant-facing, administrator-facing, and partner-facing tools as directed by H4H.

### 4.3 ALLIS and Geospatial Services

- Deploy or support ALLIS-related service layers used for community, mapping, reasoning, and program functions.
- Build and maintain geospatial databases, map layers, feature services, and related analytics pipelines.
- Integrate geospatial and operational data into program workflows where approved.

### 4.4 Documentation and Governance Support

- Draft and maintain technical documentation, operating procedures, service descriptions, and implementation records.
- Support document development for grants, governance, program controls, risk management, and technical planning.
- Maintain sufficient written records to support continuity, review, audit, and transition.

### 4.5 Program and Partner Support

- Provide technical implementation support for H4H community programs and approved collaborations.
- Assist with systems needed for funder demonstrations, partner communications, and public-interest technology deployment.
- Support onboarding, troubleshooting, and system adaptation within the scope approved by H4H.

---

## 5. Deliverables

Deliverables under this SOW may include, as applicable:

- deployed infrastructure and configured environments,
- service documentation and architecture summaries,
- software modules and application configurations,
- databases, schemas, and geospatial layers,
- operational procedures and support documents,
- reports, inventories, and implementation logs,
- and other technical work product reasonably required by the scope above.

Because this SOW covers foundational and iterative technical work, some deliverables may be ongoing operational outputs rather than single final handoff items.

Where a specific milestone deliverable is required, the parties may identify it in a written work update, amendment, or milestone schedule incorporated into this SOW.

---

## 6. Compensation Structure

### 6.1 General Framework

Compensation under this SOW shall follow the Master Services Agreement and any approved fee schedule, amendment, or related controlled document adopted by H4H.

The parties acknowledge a dual-structure framework for Contractor’s time and contribution record:

- one portion may be recognized as charitable in-kind contribution to H4H, and
- a separate portion may be tracked as private founder-capital or deferred commercial contribution outside H4H.

### 6.2 Charitable Portion

Any charitable in-kind contribution associated with services under this SOW shall be recorded only as charitable contribution to H4H and shall not be converted into founder equity inside H4H, EMS, or the Commons.

To the extent H4H uses contributed technical value for grant matching, financial support narratives, or internal accounting, that use shall remain limited to the charitable portion only.

### 6.3 Separate Founder-Capital Treatment

Any founder-capital, deferred equity, or appreciation-based private right associated with the same or related technical work must be documented separately through founder-side or KTS-side instruments and not through this SOW.

Nothing in this SOW shall be interpreted as granting Contractor equity through H4H or through community system balances.

### 6.4 Cash Fees and Reimbursements

If cash compensation, reimbursements, or later fee amendments are approved, such amounts shall be documented in a written addendum, schedule, or amendment signed by the parties.

---

## 7. Acceptance and Review

H4H shall review major deliverables, milestone submissions, and material system changes within a commercially reasonable time.

For ongoing operational work, acceptance may occur through:

- continued use of the delivered system,
- written confirmation,
- approval of milestone documentation,
- or board, officer, or authorized project-lead acknowledgment.

H4H may request reasonable corrections, updates, or refinements where work materially departs from the approved scope.

---

## 8. Intellectual Property and Licensing

Contractor retains all background intellectual property, including proprietary tools, frameworks, ALLIS-related systems, GBIM-related structures, and pre-existing technical materials, except where a separate signed writing expressly provides otherwise.

To the extent H4H uses proprietary Contractor-controlled systems in connection with this SOW, such use occurs under implied interim project license or under separate express license terms, as later documented.

This SOW does not transfer ownership of ALLIS, GBIM, or related proprietary systems to H4H.

Any long-term production use, commercialization, sublicensing, or Commons-wide licensing arrangement shall be governed by separate architecture, licensing, or commercial agreements.

---

## 9. Data, Security, and Records

Contractor shall use reasonable care in handling H4H systems, data, records, and access credentials.

Contractor shall:

- maintain appropriate backups and system hygiene,
- document material technical configurations where reasonably necessary,
- protect confidential and sensitive information,
- and support continuity planning for key program systems.

Where services touch participant-facing systems or benefits-sensitive program infrastructure, Contractor shall coordinate with H4H regarding privacy, governance, and operational safeguards.

---

## 10. Related-Party and Conflict Procedures

Because Carrie Ann Kidd may serve in both Contractor and H4H-related roles, any approvals, reimbursements, amendments, or related-party actions under this SOW shall be subject to documented conflict-of-interest and recusal procedures.

Where required by policy, fiduciary duty, or nonprofit compliance standards:

- Carrie Ann Kidd shall abstain from approval decisions,
- disinterested review shall be used,
- and records of approval shall be maintained.

Compliance with these procedures is a material condition of this SOW.

---

## 11. Term

This SOW becomes effective on the date signed by both parties and continues until:

- the work is completed,
- replaced by a later SOW,
- terminated under the Master Services Agreement,
- or amended by written agreement of the parties.

The parties may continue work on an ongoing basis under this SOW while documenting material scope changes through addenda or successor SOWs.

---

## 12. Transition and Entity Formation

If Kidd’s Technical Services is later formed as a separate legal entity, the parties may assign or replace this SOW by written agreement.

Any such transition must:

- clearly identify the successor entity,
- preserve H4H’s rights regarding prior work,
- maintain nonprofit and conflict-of-interest safeguards,
- and avoid retroactively implying that a legal entity existed before formation.

Until such written transition occurs, this SOW remains personal to Carrie Ann Kidd.

---

## 13. Special Structural Clarifications

For avoidance of doubt:

- H4H is the charitable anchor and nonprofit contracting party.
- The MountainShares Commons is the community governance and mutual-credit layer and is not an equity vehicle.
- Community EMS is non-appreciating closed-loop mutual credit and is not a founder-capital mechanism.
- Any appreciating founder instrument belongs, if at all, on the KTS or founder-private side under separate documentation.
- This SOW supports technical services only and does not create ownership rights in H4H, the Commons, or any participant system.
