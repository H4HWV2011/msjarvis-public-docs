# Fayette County Community Arts Center Resident Artist & Facility Use Agreement

This Resident Artist & Facility Use Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into between Harmony for Hope, Inc., including the Fayette County Community Arts Center (FCCAC), and the undersigned participant ("Resident Artist"). This Agreement supplements the Harmony for Hope handbook and related FCCAC policies and applies to any participant approved for recurring use of FCCAC space, display areas, storage, exhibitions, or other ongoing access associated with resident artist activities.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this Agreement is to clarify expectations for participation, use of space, storage of personal property, public conduct, and adherence to FCCAC and Harmony for Hope policies.

## 2. Relationship to Existing Policies

By signing this Agreement, the Resident Artist acknowledges receipt of, or access to, the Harmony for Hope handbook and agrees to comply with all applicable organizational and FCCAC policies, including but not limited to the following:

- Code of Conduct.
- FCCAC Code of Conduct.
- Facility Usage and Maintenance requirements.
- Social Media and Website policies.
- Grievance and Complaint Procedures.
- Disciplinary Procedures.
- Termination and Exit Procedures.
- Confidentiality and Data Protection requirements.
- Risk Management and Conflict Resolution procedures.

This Agreement is intended to work alongside those policies and does not replace them.

## 3. Nature of Participation

Resident Artist participation is a limited privilege granted by Harmony for Hope and FCCAC for mission-related programming and creative use of space.

Resident Artist status does not create:

- Employment status.
- Tenancy or housing rights.
- Indefinite access rights.
- Keyholder rights unless separately granted in writing.
- Leadership, governance, or spokesperson authority unless separately granted in writing.

## 4. Standards of Conduct

The Resident Artist agrees to conduct themselves in a manner consistent with the mission, values, and policies of Harmony for Hope and FCCAC.

The Resident Artist agrees to:

- Treat staff, volunteers, board members, artists, participants, and visitors with respect.
- Follow reasonable instructions related to safety, scheduling, facility use, setup, cleanup, storage, and public programming.
- Maintain appropriate professional and community conduct while participating in FCCAC activities or using FCCAC space.
- Avoid conduct that disrupts programming, interferes with the safe use of the facility, or undermines the mission or operations of the organization.
- Respect personal, organizational, and facility boundaries established by Harmony for Hope or FCCAC leadership.

## 5. Public Communications and Social Media

Resident Artists are expected to use sound judgment in public communications, including social media, when discussing FCCAC, Harmony for Hope, affiliated programs, participants, or experiences arising from organizational involvement.

A Resident Artist may not use public communications to:

- Harass, intimidate, or retaliate against another participant, volunteer, staff member, board member, or visitor.
- Make false or misleading statements about the organization or individuals associated with it.
- Publicly disclose confidential or private information obtained through organizational participation.
- Engage in conduct reasonably likely to create material disruption to organizational operations, programming, partnerships, or safe use of the facility.

Concerns regarding safety, misconduct, abuse, or policy violations should be reported through organizational procedures or appropriate external authorities, consistent with law and policy, rather than through disruptive public conduct.

## 6. Use of Space and Storage of Property

Any permission to use FCCAC space, display artwork, store materials, or access designated areas is conditional, limited, and revocable.

The Resident Artist acknowledges and agrees that:

- Space assignments, display opportunities, and storage permissions are subject to organizational needs and may be modified or withdrawn.
- Personal property brought into FCCAC remains the responsibility of the Resident Artist.
- Stored materials must be kept in approved areas and may be subject to labeling, inventory limits, reorganization, relocation, or removal deadlines.
- Personal property may not obstruct operations, public access, emergency egress, or the use of shared spaces.
- Upon request, the Resident Artist must remove personal property within the timeframe set by the organization.

If personal property is not removed within a stated timeframe, Harmony for Hope may take reasonable steps consistent with organizational policy and applicable law.

## 7. Access and Security

Access to FCCAC facilities is limited to approved purposes, approved areas, and approved times.

Keys, codes, and access privileges:

- Remain the property of Harmony for Hope.
- May only be granted by authorized leadership.
- May be suspended, limited, or revoked for safety, operational, conduct, or policy reasons.

## 8. Reporting and Safety Responsibilities

The Resident Artist agrees to report known safety concerns, threats, property damage, policy violations, or serious operational issues to the designated FCCAC or Harmony for Hope contact person as soon as practicable.

The Resident Artist also agrees to cooperate with reasonable safety measures, incident review processes, and organizational directives designed to protect participants, property, and operations.

## 9. Representation and Authority

A Resident Artist may not represent themselves as a coordinator, manager, director, spokesperson, keyholder, or authorized decision-maker for FCCAC or Harmony for Hope unless formally appointed in writing by authorized leadership.

Regular participation, volunteer assistance, display support, cleaning, event help, or frequent presence at the facility does not by itself create any official title, authority, or right to continued appointment or access.

## 10. Corrective Action and Termination of Access

Failure to comply with this Agreement or related organizational policies may result in corrective action consistent with Harmony for Hope procedures.

Corrective action may include:

- Verbal guidance or warning.
- Written warning.
- Restriction of access hours or areas.
- Suspension of participation, display privileges, or use of storage areas.
- Revocation of resident artist status.
- Revocation of keys, access codes, or other facility access.
- Removal from FCCAC or Harmony for Hope activities, consistent with applicable procedures.

Harmony for Hope may implement interim measures when reasonably necessary to protect safety, maintain operations, or reduce risk pending review of a concern or incident.

## 11. Acknowledgment

By signing below, the Resident Artist acknowledges that they:

- Have received or been given access to the Harmony for Hope handbook.
- Understand that this Agreement supplements existing policy and procedures.
- Understand that use of FCCAC space and resources is conditional on continued compliance with organizational expectations.
- Understand that no informal custom, recurring practice, or volunteer activity creates permanent rights of access, storage, title, or authority.
