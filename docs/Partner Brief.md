
## Partner Brief: Foundational Technical Capital for MountainShares & Ms. Allis

### Overview

Kidd’s Technical Services, LLC has invested more than 5,300 hours of senior technical labor into building the MountainShares mutual credit system and the Ms. Allis Geospatial Intelligence Platform. This work represents a substantial pre-development capital contribution—comparable in value to a multi-person engineering team operating over several years—and establishes a production-grade infrastructure stack for community wealth-building and geospatial governance in Central Appalachia.

This brief summarizes that investment for potential regional partners, including universities, CDFIs, and public agencies, and frames it as part of the capital stack for future collaborative projects.

---

### Foundational Capital Contribution

- **Founder Labor Invested:** Over 5,300 hours of senior systems architecture, geospatial engineering, and AI integration.  
- **Fair Market Valuation:** Benchmarked at $150/hour, consistent with regional rates for senior software and geospatial architects.  
- **Implied Capital Stack:** Approximately $800,000 in pre-award, founder-provided technical capital.

This contribution has been documented in detailed day-level timesheets, including dates, hours, and task descriptions, suitable for audit, grant review, and internal due diligence.

---

### Infrastructure Already Built

The investment to date has produced a coherent, working infrastructure bundle that partners can build on rather than fund from scratch:

- **MountainShares Mutual Credit System**  
  - Mutual credit ledger backend and smart contract proof of concept.  
  - Governance-oriented database schema tied to real communities and assets.  
  - Integration points for land, housing, and other commons-related data.

- **Ms. Allis Geospatial Intelligence Platform**  
  - Containerized microservices (Docker, FastAPI, Uvicorn) suitable for modern deployment practices.  
  - Custom GeoDB with PostGIS indexing and geospatial query optimization.  
  - Vector-based semantic search and LLM orchestration for narrative queries over spatial data.  
  - Monitoring, logging, and recovery procedures tested through multiple real infrastructure incidents and rebuilds.

- **Data & Governance Layer**  
  - ETL pipelines for geospatial and administrative data.  
  - Community governance backend capable of supporting commons-oriented decision-making and audit logging.

This stack is not a prototype on paper; it has been iteratively deployed, broken, rebuilt, and hardened under real-world constraints.

### Why This Matters for Partners

For regional partners—universities, CDFIs, ARC-aligned entities—this founder investment reduces risk and cost in several ways:

1. **Pre-Development Risk Absorption**  
   The most speculative and failure-prone phase (architecture, integration, low-level debugging) has already been undertaken and paid for by the founder. Partners are invited to fund applications and deployments, not basic feasibility.

2. **Match and Leverage**  
   The documented in-kind contribution can function as part of the local capital stack alongside grants, loans, and operating funds. It strengthens proposals that require match, pre-investment, or demonstration of capacity.

3. **Execution Capacity**  
   The same person/entity who built the stack is available to guide deployments, train local teams, and collaborate with academic or financial partners. This shortens the learning curve and increases the likelihood that funded projects will actually ship.

4. **Commons-Aligned Governance**  
   The systems have been deliberately designed to support non-extractive, commons-based governance rather than venture-capital extraction models. This makes the work a strong fit for mission-driven institutions focused on regional resilience and community wealth-building.

### Governance and Ownership

The current arrangement is:

- The MountainShares mutual credit system and governance configurations are being developed for use by Harmony for Hope and aligned community partners as part of a broader commons initiative.  
- The Ms. Allis Geospatial Intelligence Platform remains proprietary to Kidd’s Technical Services, LLC, but is made available on a non-exclusive, royalty-free basis for community-benefit projects under mutually agreed terms.  
- The documented founder investment is treated as a form of non-extractive, non-dilutable capital—supporting community governance and long-term stewardship rather than outside profit extraction.

Partners are invited into a shared infrastructure strategy, not asked to underwrite a private, closed system.
