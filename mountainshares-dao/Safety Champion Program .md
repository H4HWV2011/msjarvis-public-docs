## 1. Purpose and Design Intent

The Safety Champion Program creates a structured, recognized path for West Virginians and WV-grounded institutions to move from system users to active co-stewards of MountainShares, The Commons, and Ms. Allis. It is the primary mechanism by which the system remains accountable to the communities it serves rather than to external benchmarks, remote reviewers, or generic AI-safety frameworks that were not designed with Appalachian economic realities in mind.

The program exists because the system's most important safety risks are not found in code audits or reserve ratio dashboards. They are found in the gap between what the system thinks it knows about a place, a program, or a family's situation — and what is actually true on the ground in Boone County, McDowell County, Fayette County, and across every WV community MountainShares serves.

Safety work done by Champions is **commons contribution**. It is logged as EMS, recognized publicly (with consent), and reported into governance through a direct channel that bypasses the friction of general community comment. Champions are not volunteers helping a vendor. They are co-stewards of a commons they have a stake in.

## 2. Community Champions (Resident Red Team)

### 2.1 Who They Are

Community Champions are West Virginia residents, practitioners, and frontline workers who know their communities, their programs, and their realities well. They come from the counties MountainShares serves — Fayette, Raleigh, Kanawha, Boone, McDowell, Nicholas, and others — and they bring knowledge that no dashboard can surface:

- How public benefits programs actually operate county by county
- Where Ms. Allis resource data is missing, outdated, or wrong for local conditions
- How advice that sounds reasonable in general creates real harm in specific circumstances
- Which populations are missing from the system's current data and design assumptions
- What local merchants, caregivers, and mutual aid networks actually need from the system

### 2.2 What They Do

Community Champions serve as a structured **resident red team** — a designated role with a formal reporting channel and EMS recognition. Their work includes:

- **Resource verification:** Reviewing Ms. Allis recommendations and knowledge base entries for their county or region; flagging outdated, missing, or inaccurate information
- **Participant experience review:** Testing how MountainShares behaves for real households — including benefits-sensitive participants, rural participants, participants with limited connectivity, and participants navigating complex family economic situations
- **Design gap identification:** Identifying places where program rules, interface design, or Ms. Allis logic disadvantages particular groups, geographies, or economic circumstances
- **Benefits interaction review:** Monitoring for any cases where MountainShares activity — even within the Benefits-Sensitive Account protections — may interact unexpectedly with specific county-level or program-specific benefits rules; any benefits or tax consequences of MountainShares participation are under benefits-counsel review and no MountainShares-specific PLR has been obtained
- **Reporting:** Submitting structured safety reports through the Champion reporting channel to the Steward team and, where appropriate, the DAO governance dashboard

### 2.3 How They Are Recognized

Community Champion work is verified community labor. It is recognized as follows:

| Recognition Type | Mechanism | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| EMS recognition | Logged as verified EMS hours at the current base rate | Subject to phase rules, per-user caps, and reserve conditions — same as all EMS |
| Public recognition | Named as Community Champion in program materials and reports (with explicit consent) | Opt-in; consent is per-use and revocable |
| Governance voice | Direct reporting channel to the Steward team | Reports are not routed through general community comment; they receive a designated response SLA |
| Participation Weight | Champion months count toward Phase 3 Participation Weight accumulation | Sustained Champion participation builds Phase 3 eligibility on the same terms as all participation |

EMS earned through Champion work is not wages and not employment income. It is commons contribution recognition, subject to all governing EMS rules. Securities-law treatment of EMS is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS is or is not a security. Any benefits or tax consequences of EMS recognition are under benefits-counsel review; no MountainShares-specific PLR has been obtained.

### 2.4 Scope and Limitations

Community Champions operate within defined scope:

- They do not have access to other participants' personal data, financial records, or BSA flag status
- They do not have the ability to modify system parameters, override Ms. Allis outputs, or alter EMS records
- They do not represent H4H or speak on H4H's behalf in external communications without explicit authorization
- Their safety reports are advisory — the Steward team and DAO governance make decisions about remediation

## 3. Safety Champions (Institution-Linked Red Team)

### 3.1 Who They Are

Safety Champions are individuals who serve in a formal role at a WV-grounded institution — a university center, nonprofit, agency, legal aid organization, benefits counseling organization, or local government — and who participate both as Community Champions in their own right and as authorized representatives of a Safety Champion Organization.

Safety Champions bring additional capacity that community-level review cannot easily access on its own:

- Technical safety testing: reserve math, protocol controls, smart contract logic, and data pipeline integrity
- Legal and regulatory expertise: benefits law, nonprofit compliance, consumer protection, and payment regulation as applied to WV-specific contexts
- Research and documentation capacity: translating community findings into formal reports, case studies, and design change recommendations
- Institutional authority: signing safety partnership agreements, participating in formal governance reviews, and contributing to audits and external assessments

### 3.2 What They Do

Safety Champions take on deeper, more technical safety review in addition to Community Champion baseline work:

- **Treasury and reserve review:** Verifying that reserve metrics, phase thresholds, and reversion triggers are correctly calculated and correctly reported on public dashboards
- **Protocol and administrative controls review:** Testing whether operational controls — benefits-sensitive account protections, EMS cap enforcement, fee routing — behave as documented in the Program Rules and Phase Specifications
- **Data access and privacy review:** Reviewing how personal data, contribution metadata, and UEI records are stored, accessed, and protected at 704 Main Street and in IPFS-anchored records
- **Ms. Allis integration review:** Testing how Ms. Allis interacts with on-chain and platform components; identifying cases where automated reasoning or knowledge base content produces unsafe, biased, or incorrect outputs for WV-specific contexts
- **Benefits intersection analysis:** Providing formal analysis of how MountainShares activity intersects with specific WV-administered and federally-administered benefits programs — Medicaid, SNAP, WV Works, SSI, SSDI, housing assistance, and others; all such analyses are advisory and any benefits or tax conclusions remain under benefits-counsel review

### 3.3 How Institutions Participate

A lab, center, department, or organization becomes a Safety Champion Organization by signing a Safety Champion Agreement that:

- Acknowledges the role as a limited safety partnership — not employment, not ownership, not a vendor relationship, and not a governance position
- Commits to basic oversight and good-faith cooperation on safety findings, including timely response to Steward team follow-up
- Defines the scope of authorized access for institution-affiliated Champions
- Permits EMS recognition to be aggregated (where appropriate) to the organization's UEI — allowing institutional-scale contributions to be tracked and recognized
- Includes a conflict-of-interest disclosure process so that Champion findings from institutions with any financial relationship to H4H, KTS, or MountainShares partners are appropriately flagged

Staff, students, or affiliates of a Safety Champion Organization may then serve as Safety Champions with clear scope, boundaries, reporting expectations, and EMS recognition pathways.

### 3.4 How They Are Recognized

| Recognition Type | Mechanism | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| EMS recognition | Logged as verified EMS hours at the current base rate | Same governing rules as all EMS recognition |
| Institutional EMS aggregation | Safety Champion Organization UEI receives aggregated contribution credit | Per agreement terms; does not create institutional equity or ownership interest |
| Public recognition | Named as Safety Champion Organization in governance reports and program materials (with consent) | Opt-in per use |
| Formal governance participation | Safety Champion Organizations are eligible to receive formal governance observer status in DAO governance sessions | Observer status only — no voting rights beyond standard UEI participation |
| Capstone and practicum pathways | Formal framework for academic capstones, practicums, or fellowships structured around Champion work | Documented via Safety Champion Agreement |

## 4. Reporting, Response, and Accountability

### 4.1 Champion Reporting Channel

Champions submit safety reports through the designated Champion reporting channel — separate from general community comment, and with a guaranteed response path. Reports are triaged by the Steward team within the following SLAs:

| Report Category | Triage SLA | Resolution SLA |
|---|---|---|
| Benefits-sensitive participant harm (actual or potential) | 24 hours | 7 days |
| Ms. Allis knowledge base error affecting WV resource accuracy | 48 hours | 14 days |
| Reserve or treasury calculation discrepancy | 24 hours | Per Audit and Risk Management Plan incident protocols |
| Design gap disadvantaging a specific population or geography | 5 business days | 30 days |
| General improvement suggestion | 10 business days | Next quarterly governance review |

### 4.2 Escalation Path

If a Champion report is not acknowledged within the SLA, the Champion may escalate directly to the H4H Board liaison for the Safety Champion Program. If the escalated report involves a potential benefits harm, reserve discrepancy, or structural boundary issue, it is automatically treated as a minimum Level 2 incident under the Audit and Risk Management Plan.

### 4.3 Non-Retaliation Guarantee

No Champion will face any reduction in EMS recognition, loss of Champion status, removal from governance channels, or any other adverse program action as a consequence of submitting a safety report — including reports that identify significant system failures, design flaws, or compliance concerns. This guarantee is enforced by H4H Board policy and is not subject to override by any DAO vote or operational decision.

### 4.4 Public Reporting

Non-sensitive summaries of Champion reports, findings, and Steward team responses are published quarterly to the governance repo. Individual Champions are identified in published summaries only with explicit consent. Institutional Safety Champion Organizations are identified by default unless they request anonymization.

## 5. EMS Recognition Governance for Champion Work

Champion work EMS recognition follows all governing EMS rules without exception. Key constraints:

- EMS earned through Champion work is subject to the same per-user monthly caps as all other EMS
- Phase-based buying-power expansion does not apply to Champion EMS more favorably than to any other EMS
- No Champion EMS balance creates a vested right to future higher conversion value
- Community EMS holders receive no equity, ownership, or profit-participation rights in H4H or KTS; securities-law treatment of EMS is under review by securities counsel and this document makes no representation as to whether EMS is or is not a security
- Aggregated institutional EMS (Safety Champion Organization UEI) is subject to institutional-scale caps defined in the Safety Champion Agreement — it does not accumulate unbounded
- Benefits-Sensitive Account rules apply to Champion participants who carry BSA flags — Champion status does not exempt a participant from BSA protections

Champion EMS recognition is not income and not wages. It is recognition of verified community contribution to the commons, documented in the EMS ledger, visible on the participant's dashboard, and governed by all Program Rules. Any benefits or tax consequences of EMS recognition are under benefits-counsel review; no MountainShares-specific PLR has been obtained.

## 6. What This Program Builds

The Safety Champion Program is not a compliance mechanism. It is a community infrastructure investment with three long-horizon outcomes:

**Co-stewardship at scale.** As MountainShares moves through Phase 2 and Phase 3, the volume and geographic complexity of the commons far exceeds what any small Steward team can monitor alone. Champions are the distributed nervous system of the safety architecture — the people closest to each place, each community, each program interaction, who can see problems before they become incidents.

**Institutional knowledge integration.** WV's universities, legal aid organizations, benefits counselors, and community agencies hold knowledge about WV-specific program interactions, regulatory landscapes, and community vulnerabilities that no outside AI safety framework has. The Safety Champion Program is the mechanism that gets that knowledge into the system's design, its data, and its governance.

**A documented safety and fairness record.** Joint reports, case studies, and formal safety findings produced through the program create a body of evidence that MountainShares is governed by community accountability — not self-certification. That record matters for funders, regulators, and institutional partners who need to assess the program's trustworthiness before committing resources or reputation.

## 7. Program Governance and Review

The Safety Champion Program is governed by H4H and reviewed annually by the DAO. The annual review includes:

- Champion participation metrics: number of active Champions, reports submitted, resolution rates, EMS recognized
- Safety Champion Organization participation and agreement status
- Assessment of whether Champion report SLAs are being met
- Review of any non-retaliation policy compliance issues
- Proposals for program expansion, new county activation, or new institutional partnerships

Any material change to Champion recognition rates, reporting SLAs, scope of access, or non-retaliation guarantees requires a DAO governance vote with standard quorum and majority thresholds.

## Appendix A: Definitions

- **Community Champion:** A WV resident who serves as a structured resident red-team member for MountainShares, The Commons, and Ms. Allis. Recognized through EMS. Has a direct reporting channel to the Steward team.
- **Safety Champion:** An individual serving in a formal role at a Safety Champion Organization who participates as a Community Champion and provides deeper technical, legal, or research-level safety review.
- **Safety Champion Organization:** A WV-grounded institution that signs a Safety Champion Agreement and authorizes its staff, students, or affiliates to serve as Safety Champions.
- **Safety Champion Agreement:** The governing document for institutional participation in the program. Defines scope, access, EMS aggregation terms, conflict-of-interest disclosure, and reporting obligations.
- **EMS (Earned MountainShares):** Recognition credits for verified community contribution. Community EMS holders receive no equity, ownership, or profit-participation rights in H4H or KTS. Securities-law treatment of EMS is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS is or is not a security. Subject to all Program Rules and phase conditions. Not wages, not income.
- **Resident Red Team:** The Community Champion role, framed as a structured adversarial review function — participants are specifically looking for what is wrong, missing, or harmful, not just confirming that things are working.
- **Steward Team:** The H4H-designated operational team responsible for Champion report triage, response, and escalation.
- **Non-Retaliation Guarantee:** H4H Board policy protecting Champions from adverse program consequences for submitting safety reports. Not subject to DAO override.
- **Benefits-Sensitive Account (BSA):** Enhanced-protection account for participants receiving or potentially receiving means-tested public benefits. Champion status does not modify BSA protections.
- **Participation Weight (PW):** Phase 3 dividend allocation score — Verified Months Active × Activity Multiplier. Champion months count on the same terms as all participation.
