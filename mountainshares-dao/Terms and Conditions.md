## 2. Nature of the System

MountainShares is a community mutual-credit and governance system designed to support local participation, local exchange, recognition of contribution, and community coordination.

MountainShares is not:

- a bank account,
- a deposit account,
- a savings product,
- an equity program,
- or a promise of profit.

EMS and M$ are community instruments used within the MountainShares Commons. They are not shares, stock, partnership units, or ownership interests in Harmony for Hope, Inc., the DUNA, Kidd's Technical Services, or any other entity. Community EMS holders receive no equity, ownership, or profit-participation rights in H4H or KTS. Securities-law treatment of EMS and M$ is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS or M$ is or is not a security.

No participant should treat EMS or M$ as a speculative asset or financial investment. Any benefits or tax consequences arising from MountainShares participation are under benefits-counsel review; no MountainShares-specific Private Letter Ruling (PLR) has been obtained. Participants are responsible for obtaining independent advice regarding legal, tax, and benefits consequences specific to their circumstances.

## 3. Structural Roles of Participating Entities

### 3.1 Harmony for Hope, Inc.

Harmony for Hope, Inc. serves as the initial charitable anchor and operational sponsor for MountainShares during early implementation and transition. H4H is not the money transmitter for MountainShares; that role belongs to the MountainShares DAO/DUNA as licensee of record.

H4H may provide:

- administrative support,
- grant administration,
- community programming,
- recordkeeping,
- compliance support,
- and other startup functions necessary to operate the Commons safely.

H4H does not issue founder equity through MountainShares, does not convert EMS into private founder ownership rights, and does not hold participant balances as private property for insider extraction.

### 3.2 MountainShares Commons / DUNA

The MountainShares Commons is the community governance and mutual-credit system through which participants engage in local exchange, contribution recognition, and collective decision-making.

The MountainShares DAO/DUNA is the licensee of record for money-services activities, including the West Virginia Money Transmitter License (WV MTL), the FinCEN MSB registration, the required surety bond, BSA/AML compliance program obligations, and the cash-out rails. The Commons operates through the DUNA legal wrapper as the community layer for:

- the EMS ledger,
- the M$ system,
- governance participation,
- the resident economy,
- treasury functions,
- money-services licensing obligations,
- and benefits-sensitive participation rules.

The Commons is member-governed and does not create equity or ownership interests in any entity. Securities-law treatment of community EMS is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS is or is not a security.

### 3.3 Kidd's Technical Services

Kidd's Technical Services is the founder-side technical and commercial vehicle for proprietary systems, intellectual property, and related commercial functions.

Any founder capital, founder equity, appreciation-based instrument, licensing income, or private commercial participation is handled through KTS-side agreements and instruments, not through EMS, not through M$, and not through any resident-facing MountainShares participant account.

## 5. EMS and M$ Rules

### 5.1 EMS Recognition

EMS is the contribution-recognition layer of the MountainShares Commons.

EMS may be earned, recorded, weighted, or recognized according to the Program Rules, participation formulas, and approved contribution standards.

EMS does not create:

- equity,
- partnership interests,
- private treasury ownership,
- or appreciation-based founder rights.

Securities-law treatment of EMS is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS is or is not a security.

### 5.2 M$ Mutual Credit

M$ is the internal mutual-credit instrument used for approved community exchange under MountainShares rules.

M$ is not legal tender, is not fiat currency, is not a bank deposit, and is not redeemable on demand except as expressly provided under applicable phase rules and safety restrictions.

All M$ functions remain subject to system caps, reserve protections, account controls, and any lawful restructuring required before expanded conversion features are activated.

### 5.3 Buying-Power Expansion and Securities Disclaimer

Community EMS maintains stable baseline exchange value throughout all phases. The system includes a buying-power expansion schedule, but that schedule is strictly conditional, deferred, and reserve-gated. It does not represent a present appreciation right for any resident. No resident holds a vested appreciation right prior to threshold clearance.

Community EMS holders receive no equity, ownership, or profit-participation rights in H4H or KTS. Securities-law treatment of EMS and any buying-power expansion feature is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS is or is not a security.

Any appreciating or founder-capital instrument exists, if at all, exclusively on the KTS side under separate, counsel-reviewed documentation and outside participant account structures.
