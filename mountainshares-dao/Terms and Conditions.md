## 1. Acceptance and Scope

These Terms and Conditions govern participation in the MountainShares Commons, including EMS recognition, M$ mutual-credit participation, governance access, reserve protections, benefits-sensitive safeguards, and related community systems.

By enrolling in, accessing, contributing to, or using MountainShares, each participant accepts these Terms and agrees to comply with the Program Rules, related specifications, safety policies, and duly adopted governance decisions.

These Terms apply to all participants, including residents, volunteers, organizers, vendors, institutional partners, administrators, stewards, and any other user of the MountainShares Commons.

---

## 2. Nature of the System

MountainShares is a community mutual-credit and governance system designed to support local participation, local exchange, recognition of contribution, and community coordination.

MountainShares is not:

- a bank account,
- a securities offering,
- an investment program,
- a deposit account,
- a savings product,
- an equity program,
- or a promise of profit.

EMS and M$ are community instruments used within the MountainShares Commons. They are not shares, stock, partnership units, or ownership interests in Harmony for Hope, Inc., the DUNA, Kidd's Technical Services, or any other entity.

Community EMS is non-appreciating and operates as a closed-loop mutual-credit and recognition system. No participant should treat EMS or M$ as a speculative asset or financial investment.

## 3. Structural Roles of Participating Entities

### 3.1 Harmony for Hope, Inc.

Harmony for Hope, Inc. serves as the initial charitable anchor and operational sponsor for MountainShares during early implementation and transition.

H4H may provide:

- administrative support,
- grant administration,
- community programming,
- recordkeeping,
- compliance support,
- and other startup functions necessary to operate the Commons safely.

H4H does not issue founder equity through MountainShares, does not convert EMS into private founder ownership rights, and does not hold participant balances as private property for insider extraction.

### 3.2 MountainShares Commons / DUNA

The MountainShares Commons is the community governance and mutual-credit system through which participants engage in local exchange, contribution recognition, and collective decision-making.

The Commons operates through the DUNA legal wrapper as the community layer for:

- the EMS ledger,
- the M$ system,
- governance participation,
- the resident economy,
- treasury functions,
- and benefits-sensitive participation rules.

The Commons is member-governed and does not create equity or ownership interests in any entity.

### 3.3 Kidd's Technical Services

Kidd's Technical Services is the founder-side technical and commercial vehicle for proprietary systems, intellectual property, and related commercial functions.

Any founder capital, founder equity, appreciation-based instrument, licensing income, or private commercial participation is handled through KTS-side agreements and instruments, not through EMS, not through M$, and not through any resident-facing MountainShares participant account.

## 5. EMS and M$ Rules

### 5.1 EMS Recognition

EMS is the contribution-recognition layer of the MountainShares Commons.

EMS may be earned, recorded, weighted, or recognized according to the Program Rules, participation formulas, and approved contribution standards.

EMS does not create:

- equity,
- securities rights,
- partnership interests,
- private treasury ownership,
- or appreciation-based founder rights.

### 5.2 M$ Mutual Credit

M$ is the internal mutual-credit instrument used for approved community exchange under MountainShares rules.

M$ is not legal tender, is not fiat currency, is not a bank deposit, and is not redeemable on demand except as expressly provided under applicable phase rules and safety restrictions.

All M$ functions remain subject to system caps, reserve protections, account controls, and any lawful restructuring required before expanded conversion features are activated.

### 5.3 Non-Appreciation Rule

Community EMS and M$ are non-appreciating system instruments.

No provision of these Terms, the Program Rules, or any related MountainShares document shall be interpreted to grant appreciation, investment return, passive yield, or equity-like upside to ordinary participant balances.

Any appreciating or founder-capital instrument exists, if at all, outside the community system and outside participant account structures.

## 6. Treasury, Reserve, and Phase Controls

### 6.1 Treasury Purpose

The MountainShares treasury exists to support safe operation of the Commons, including reserve protection, program administration, community infrastructure, compliance, and duly approved operational purposes.

Treasury assets may not be used for undisclosed insider extraction or private conversion arrangements inconsistent with the non-equity nature of the Commons.

### 6.2 Treasury Reserve

The Treasury Reserve is maintained to support system safety, operational continuity, phase gating, and lawful transition into later operating stages.

The Treasury Reserve may fund approved Phase 2 transition costs, including:

- DUNA formation and structuring,
- KTS formation and setup,
- legal counsel,
- valuation and compliance work,
- filing costs,
- and related transition expenses necessary for lawful program evolution.

Such use must be disclosed, tracked, and authorized under the governing rules.

### 6.3 Phase-Based Limits

Access to EMS functions, M$ use, redemption pathways, or any expanded system capability may be limited by phase.

Phase controls may include:

- reserve-ratio floors,
- per-user caps,
- category-based restrictions,
- benefits-sensitive limits,
- treasury-protection triggers,
- and legal-compliance checkpoints.

No participant has a vested right to any future feature until it is lawfully activated and made available under the governing rules then in effect.

## 7. Conversion, Cash-Out, and Legal Compliance

Any conversion of internal system instruments into external value shall remain subject to:

- phase activation rules,
- reserve-ratio protections,
- applicable law,
- compliance review,
- and any licensing, registration, restructuring, or suspension required by counsel.

No participant may assume that M$ or any related system balance is automatically redeemable for U.S. dollars or other fiat value.

Any proposed cash-out, ATM-style access, fiat conversion bridge, or related payment feature may be delayed, modified, suspended, or removed if required to avoid unlawful money transmission, licensing, securities, tax, or consumer-protection risk.

Founder-specific EMS conversion rights are not created by these Terms and shall not be implied from any founder contribution, leadership role, or historical system authorship.

## 9. Founder, Steward, and Related-Party Safeguards

Where a proposed action implicates founder-side private instruments, KTS agreements, licensing arrangements, treasury allocations, or related-party transactions, the matter shall be reviewed and approved under documented conflict procedures.

No participant-facing MountainShares balance may be used to disguise, route, or simulate private founder equity.

## 11. Benefits-Sensitive Participation

MountainShares is designed with benefits-sensitive safeguards intended to reduce avoidable harm to participants whose eligibility for public benefits may be affected by financial structure or reporting.

- community instruments are structured as non-appreciating mutual credit,
- program rules may impose caps and protective restrictions,
- certain accounts may be subject to additional safeguards,
- and system changes with benefits implications may receive heightened review before activation.

Participants remain responsible for obtaining independent advice regarding legal, tax, and benefits consequences specific to their circumstances.
