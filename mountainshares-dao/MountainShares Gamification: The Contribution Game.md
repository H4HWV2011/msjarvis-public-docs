## 1. Purpose

The MountainShares contribution game is the **long game.** It is civic, longitudinal, and serious. It answers the question: *who has shown up, done the work, and earned the right to help shape this commons?*

Progress is slow by design. Rank cannot be bought, gifted, or gamed. Every level represents real documented hours of real work in real Appalachian communities — logged with UEI, geo, and timestamp on the recognition ledger.

This game is not about entertainment. It is about building a permanent, auditable record of contribution that translates directly into governance standing, community role eligibility, and economic recognition within the bounds of the Economic Safety Specification.

---

## 2. The Currency: EMS Hours

The base unit is one verified hour of qualifying community work, logged as an EMS hour in the recognition ledger.

- Hours accumulate **permanently.** They cannot be spent, transferred, lost, or taken away.
- Hours are logged with full **UEI, geo, org, and timestamp metadata.**
- Hours are the basis of **voice** in MountainShares governance.
- Hours are a record of who you are in this commons.

EMS hours are earned through qualifying activities defined in the [Program Rules](MountainShares%20Program%20Rules(Draft).md) and fed directly from The Commons game layer. Every qualifying activity in The Commons — mutual aid, education, cultural preservation, mapping, data stewardship, events — logs EMS hours here.

## 3. Rank Progression

Rank is determined solely by accumulated EMS hours and account tenure. It cannot be purchased, assigned, or accelerated by financial contribution.

| Rank | EMS Hours Required | Account Age | What It Unlocks |
|------|--------------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Newcomer** | 0 | Any | Wallet, UEI, access to The Commons app, Ms. Allis chatbot |
| **Bronze** | 10 hrs | 30 days | Voting eligibility (1 UEI = 1 vote), Tier 1 EMS caps, Steward candidacy path |
| **Silver** | 100 hrs | — | Steward eligibility, expanded quest access in The Commons, regional proposal co-sponsorship |
| **Gold** | 300 hrs | — | Captain eligibility, regional proposal rights, localized Ms. Allis analytics access |
| **Champion** | 750 hrs | — | Elder nomination eligibility, full Ms. Allis governance analytics, Safety Champion Organization path |
| **Elder** | 750+ hrs, nominated + confirmed | ≥ 2 years active | Constitutional review role, Safety and Ethics working group, institutional memory |
| **Groundbreaker** *(Phase 0 only)* | 10 hrs during Phase 0 | 30 days (Phase 0) | Permanent founding cohort status — never available after Phase 1 opens |

> **Note on Tier 2 EMS caps:** Tier 2 caps (verified caregiving and high-priority roles) are assigned by role verification — org co-sign, geo-fence, and Commons trust signals — not by rank alone. However, rank history and EMS diversity are strong signals in Tier 2 eligibility review.

## 4. Community Champion Role — Fluid by Design

Community Champions are not defined by a permanent job description. Their focus evolves as the program matures and as governance identifies where they are most needed.

| Phase | Primary Champion Focus |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Phase 0** | Red-team security testing, governance document review, system hardening |
| **Phase 1 (early)** | Ground truthing — collecting and verifying local data, contact information, place records |
| **Phase 1 (mature)** | Community anchoring, onboarding new participants, local quest coordination |
| **Phase 2+** | Governance leadership, regional stewardship, data quality oversight, mentoring |

Champion responsibilities for any given period are defined through governance and published in the current-phase Champion scope document. EMS hours are earned for whatever the current Champion focus is — the ledger records what was done and when, preserving the full history even as the role evolves.

## 5. Visual Badge Design Language

Badges are the visible record of who you are in this commons. They are designed to feel **earned, place-specific, and Appalachian** — not generic gamification icons.

### Design Principles

- **Material metaphor:** Badges evoke hand-sewn patches, carved wood seals, and stamped metal — things made by hand and worn with pride.
- **Color encodes phase:**
  - Phase 0 — **Iron/Charcoal** — forged before the fire, before anyone was watching
  - Phase 1 — **Earth tones (brown, green, clay)** — roots going down, work being done
  - Phase 2 — **Copper/Amber** — things starting to glow, economy coming alive
  - Phase 3 — **Gold/River-glass** — fully alive, long-seasoned, rare
- **Shape encodes category:**
  - Hexagon — governance and contribution
  - Shield — security and safety
  - Compass rose — mapping and GIS
  - Flame — mutual aid
  - Book/scroll — knowledge and governance documents
  - Storefront — commerce
  - Mountain silhouette — place and milestone
  - Topographic lines — data and land
- **Rarity is visible:** Founding badges (Phase 0) have a double-ring border and worn-edge treatment — immediately legible as old, rare, and permanent.
- **Profile display:** Badges stack as a visual grid on the UEI profile — like a merit badge sash or a patch jacket. The full story of a participant is visible at a glance.

## 11. What Rank Does NOT Do

- Rank does **not** change vote weight. One UEI = one vote at every rank.
- Rank does **not** automatically increase spendable M$ caps. Caps are governed by the reserve ratio and tier assignment per the Economic Safety Specification.
- Rank does **not** create financial rights, equity interest, or expectation of profit or distribution.
- Rank does **not** exempt any participant from hard safety triggers or legal constraints.

Higher rank reflects more hours worked — which naturally produces more EMS in the recognition ledger and more governance standing over time. That is the whole design. **The commons rewards time and contribution, not status or money.**

## 12. Ms. Allis and the Contribution Game

Ms. Allis surfaces the contribution game through the chatbot UI and governance dashboards. She can tell any participant:

- Current rank and EMS hours to next rank
- Which badges they hold and which are in progress
- Governance rights currently active on their UEI
- Role eligibility status (Steward, Captain, Elder path)
- Their EMS hour breakdown by category
- Network-wide rank distribution (anonymized) for context
- Data stewardship verification ping schedule and response history

Ms. Allis does not control rank assignment — rank is calculated automatically from the recognition ledger. She reports and surfaces; she does not adjudicate.

## 13. Relationship to The Commons Game

The MountainShares contribution game and The Commons daily life game are **two games running on one system.** They share the same UEI, the same recognition ledger, and the same EMS hours.

- **The Commons game** is the on-ramp: daily quests, streaks, merchant visits, trail discoveries, data stewardship, seasonal campaigns. It is light, social, and immediately rewarding.
- **The MountainShares game** is what those daily hours **become** over time: rank, governance standing, role eligibility, visual badges, and permanent record in the commons.

See [The Commons Gamification: The Daily Life Game](../the%20commons/The%20Commons%20Gamification%3A%20The%20Daily%20Life%20Game.md) for the full design of the daily layer, including the three-stage platform arc (Marketplace → Discovery → Full Commerce).
