## 1. Purpose

The MountainShares contribution game is the **long game.** It is civic, longitudinal, and serious. It answers one question: *who has shown up, done the work, and earned the right to help shape this commons?*

Progress is slow by design. Rank cannot be bought, gifted, or gamed. Every level represents real documented hours of real work in real Appalachian communities — logged with UEI, geo, and timestamp on the recognition ledger at 704 Main Street and in IPFS-anchored records.

This game is not about entertainment. It is about building a permanent, auditable record of contribution that translates directly into governance standing, community role eligibility, Safety Champion Program access, and economic recognition within the bounds of the applicable Phase Specification. The ledger remembers everything. That is the design.

## 2. The Currency: EMS Hours

The base unit of the contribution game is one verified hour of qualifying community work, logged as an EMS hour in the recognition ledger.

- Hours accumulate **permanently.** They cannot be spent, transferred, lost, or taken away.
- Hours are logged with full **UEI, geo, org, and timestamp metadata.**
- Hours are the basis of **governance voice** in MountainShares.
- Hours are the basis of **Phase 3 Participation Weight accumulation.**
- Hours are a permanent record of who a participant is in this commons.

EMS hours are earned through qualifying activities defined in the [Program Rules](MountainShares%20Program%20Rules(Draft).md) and fed directly from The Commons game layer. Every qualifying activity in The Commons — mutual aid, education, cultural preservation, mapping, data stewardship, safety champion reporting, events — logs EMS hours into the recognition ledger.

**What EMS hours are not:**
- They are not wages or employment income
- They are not investment instruments or equity
- They are not redeemable for fiat outside of the phase-governed EMS→M$ conversion process
- They are not transferable between UEIs
- They do not vest or appreciate prior to phase threshold clearance

## 3. Rank Progression

Rank is determined solely by accumulated EMS hours and, for certain ranks, account tenure and peer confirmation. It cannot be purchased, assigned, or accelerated by financial contribution.

| Rank | EMS Hours Required | Account Age / Other Condition | What It Unlocks |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Newcomer** | 0 | Any | Wallet, UEI, access to The Commons app, Ms. Allis chatbot |
| **Bronze** | 10 hrs | 30 days active | Voting eligibility (1 UEI = 1 vote), Tier 1 EMS caps, Steward candidacy path |
| **Silver** | 100 hrs | — | Steward eligibility, expanded quest access, regional proposal co-sponsorship |
| **Gold** | 300 hrs | — | Captain eligibility, regional proposal rights, extended Ms. Allis governance analytics |
| **Champion** | 750 hrs | — | Elder nomination eligibility, full Ms. Allis governance analytics, Safety Champion Organization path, Phase 3 Founding Deep PW eligibility pathway opens |
| **Elder** | 750+ hrs, nominated + confirmed by supermajority | ≥ 2 years continuous active | Constitutional review advisory role, Safety and Ethics working group, institutional memory function |
| **Groundbreaker** *(Phase 0 only)* | 10 hrs during Phase 0 | 30 days (Phase 0 only) | Permanent founding cohort status — never available after Phase 1 opens |

> **Tier 2 EMS caps:** Tier 2 caps (verified caregiving and high-priority roles) are assigned by role verification — org co-sign, geo-fence, and Commons trust signals — not by rank alone. Rank history and EMS diversity are strong signals in Tier 2 eligibility review but are not automatic qualifiers.

> **Benefits-Sensitive Accounts:** Rank progression has no special interaction with Benefits-Sensitive Account (BSA) protections. All BSA rules apply uniformly regardless of rank. High rank does not reduce protections or increase exposure.

## 4. Community Champion Role — Fluid by Design

Community Champions are not defined by a permanent job description. Their focus evolves as the program matures and as governance identifies where they are most needed. Champion work earns EMS hours under the same governing rules as all EMS.

| Phase | Primary Champion Focus |
|---|---|
| **Phase 0** | Red-team security testing, governance document review, system hardening |
| **Phase 1 (early)** | Ground truthing — collecting and verifying local data, contact information, place records |
| **Phase 1 (mature)** | Community anchoring, onboarding new participants, local quest coordination |
| **Phase 2+** | Governance leadership, regional stewardship, data quality oversight, mentoring |

Champion responsibilities for any given period are defined through governance and published in the current-phase Champion scope document. The recognition ledger records what was done and when, preserving the full history even as the role evolves.

The Safety Champion Program (see [Safety Champion Program](Safety%20Champion%20Program%20.md)) defines the formal institution-linked tier of this role, including the non-retaliation guarantee, reporting SLAs, and institutional agreement structure. The two documents are complementary: this document governs recognition and rank; the Safety Champion Program governs scope, reporting, and accountability.

## 5. Visual Badge Design Language

Badges are the visible record of who a participant is in this commons. They are designed to feel **earned, place-specific, and Appalachian** — not generic gamification icons.

### Design Principles

- **Material metaphor:** Badges evoke hand-sewn patches, carved wood seals, and stamped metal — things made by hand and worn with pride
- **Color encodes phase:**
  - Phase 0 — **Iron/Charcoal** — forged before the fire, before anyone was watching
  - Phase 1 — **Earth tones (brown, green, clay)** — roots going down, work being done
  - Phase 2 — **Copper/Amber** — things starting to glow, economy coming alive
  - Phase 3 — **Gold/River-glass** — fully alive, long-seasoned, rare
- **Shape encodes category:**
  - Hexagon — governance and contribution
  - Shield — security and safety
  - Compass rose — mapping and GIS
  - Flame — mutual aid
  - Book/scroll — knowledge and governance documents
  - Storefront — commerce
  - Mountain silhouette — place and milestone
  - Topographic lines — data and land
- **Rarity is visible:** Founding badges (Phase 0) carry a double-ring border and worn-edge treatment — immediately legible as old, rare, and permanent
- **Profile display:** Badges stack as a visual grid on the UEI profile — like a merit badge sash or a patch jacket. The full story of a participant is visible at a glance.

## 11. What Rank Does NOT Do

- Rank does **not** change vote weight — one UEI, one vote, at every rank
- Rank does **not** automatically increase spendable M$ caps — caps are governed by the reserve metric system and tier assignment per the applicable Phase Specification
- Rank does **not** create financial rights, equity interest, or expectation of profit or distribution
- Rank does **not** exempt any participant from hard safety triggers, reserve-based reversion, or BSA protections
- Rank does **not** create vested EMS appreciation rights — those are governed by phase thresholds entirely independently of rank
- Rank does **not** establish any founder equity path through H4H or community EMS
- Rank does **not** override the three-box structural separation between H4H, MountainShares Commons, and KTS

Higher rank reflects more verified hours worked — which naturally produces more EMS in the recognition ledger and more governance standing over time. That is the whole design. **The commons rewards time and contribution, not status or money.**

## 12. Phase 3 Participation Weight vs. Rank

These are two distinct systems running on the same underlying activity record. They answer different questions.

| System | Question It Answers | Primary Input | Governing Document |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Rank** | *Who are you in this commons right now?* | Cumulative EMS hours | This document |
| **Participation Weight (PW)** | *How large a share of the Phase 3 surplus did you help build?* | Verified Active Months × Activity Multiplier | Phase 3 Specification |

A participant with 750 EMS hours who took a 2-year break has Champion rank but lower PW than a consistent Bronze participant who never missed a month. Both systems are fair. They measure different things. Neither is more important than the other — they serve different purposes for the commons.

| PW Tier | Verified Active Months | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum | 1–6 | 1.0× |
| Regular | 7–24 | 1.25× |
| Sustained | 25–60 | 1.5× |
| Founding Deep | 61–84 (≥ 5 years continuous) | 2.0× |

## 13. Ms. Allis and the Contribution Game

Ms. Allis surfaces the contribution game through the chatbot UI and governance dashboards. She can tell any participant:

- Current rank and EMS hours to next rank
- Which badges they hold and which are in progress
- Governance rights currently active on their UEI
- Role eligibility status (Steward, Captain, Regional Champion, Elder path)
- EMS hour breakdown by category
- Participation Weight tier and verified active month count
- Network-wide rank distribution (anonymized) for context
- Data stewardship verification ping schedule and response history
- Phase 3 dividend clock status and eligibility projection (when Phase 3 is active)
- Current FVSR value for the calendar year

Ms. Allis does not control rank assignment — rank is calculated automatically from the recognition ledger. She reports and surfaces; she does not adjudicate. All rank calculations are auditable independently of Ms. Allis outputs.

## 14. Relationship to The Commons Game

The MountainShares contribution game and The Commons daily life game are **two games running on one system.** They share the same UEI, the same recognition ledger, and the same EMS hours.

- **The Commons game** is the on-ramp: daily quests, streaks, merchant visits, trail discoveries, data stewardship, seasonal campaigns. It is accessible, social, and immediately rewarding.
- **The MountainShares game** is what those daily hours **become** over time: rank, governance standing, role eligibility, visual badges, Participation Weight accumulation, and permanent record in the commons.

See [The Commons Gamification: The Daily Life Game](../the%20commons/The%20Commons%20Gamification%3A%20The%20Daily%20Life%20Game.md) for the full design of the daily layer, including the three-stage platform arc (Marketplace → Discovery → Full Commerce).

## Appendix B: Definitions

- **EMS Hour:** One verified hour of qualifying community work, logged in the recognition ledger with UEI, geo, org, and timestamp. Permanent, non-transferable, non-spendable as recognition units.
- **Rank:** A participant's standing in the contribution game, calculated automatically from cumulative EMS hours and tenure. Cannot be purchased or assigned.
- **Badge:** A permanent visual record of a specific contribution achievement. Phase-colored, shape-categorized, IPFS-anchored.
- **Groundbreaker:** Phase 0 rank — the only rank that is time-gated to a specific program phase. Cannot be earned after Phase 1 opens.
- **Founding Legacy:** The rarest badge — requires both Phase 0 Groundbreaker status and Champion rank. Cannot be manufactured or replicated.
- **Participation Weight (PW):** Phase 3 dividend allocation score — Verified Months Active × Activity Multiplier. Distinct from rank.
- **Founding Deep:** Highest Participation Weight tier — 61–84 verified active months; 2.0× multiplier.
- **Verification Veteran:** Badge earned by responding to three or more Ms. Allis data verification ping cycles — recognizing the ongoing stewardship of living place data.
- **Regional Champion (Tier 3):** A Phase 2 governance role, eligible at Gold rank, anchoring county-level activation in the regional corridor network.
- **Hard Trigger:** Automatic system rule enforced by the Phase Management Controller. Non-overridable by any governance vote or rank level.
- **Three-Box Structure:** Entity wall separating H4H (charitable anchor), MountainShares Commons/DUNA (community ledger), and KTS (founder commercial engine). Rank and badges do not modify this structure.
