## Preamble

This document establishes the recognition, vesting, and governance treatment of pre-launch Earned MountainShares (EMS) hours associated with the founding UEI of Carrie Ann Kidd / Kidd's Technical Services, LLC (KTS) prior to Phase 0 activation.

As of May 14, 2026, 6,067.5 hours of technical infrastructure work are logged in FTIS-001 at a fair‑market rate of 300/hour. These hours were earned before the MountainShares program opened to any external participant, under conditions of sole founder contribution with no external capital, no external staffing, and no venture investment of any kind.

This schedule exists to:

1. Protect the MountainShares treasury and EMS commons from unmanaged founder-related risk at Phase 0 and beyond.  
2. Protect the founder’s recognized contribution history under a governance framework that treats her EMS with the same rigor and rules as any other participant’s EMS.  
3. Clarify how founder pre-launch EMS participates in governance and long-run participation formulas, without creating equity, securities, or ownership interests in any entity.

This document is incorporated by reference into the MountainShares DAO Governance Charter and the Program Rules. In any conflict between this document and the Program Rules, the Program Rules control for system-wide parameters; this document controls founder-specific EMS recognition and governance terms.

***

## 1. Pre-Launch EMS — Recognition and Ledger Treatment

### 1.1 Recognition Ledger Status

All pre-launch EMS hours logged through FTIS-001 prior to Phase 0 activation are:

- **Fully recorded** in the MountainShares recognition ledger with complete date, hour, task, and milestone metadata.  
- **Counted in full** for governance participation and participation-weight calculations as described in the Governance Charter and Phase 3 Surplus Distribution Specification.  
- **Designated as founder pre-launch EMS**, a label used solely for transparency and auditing; it does not change how EMS behaves economically.

The recognition ledger records these hours as a historical contribution record. They are treated as EMS within the system, subject to the same non‑appreciating, mutual-credit rules that apply to any participant.

### 1.2 Treasury Liability Treatment

Founder EMS is **not a special treasury liability** and does not receive any preferential treatment in reserve-ratio calculations.

Specifically:

- Founder EMS is included in the same outstanding EMS figures used for treasury and safety calculations, on the same basis as other participants’ EMS.  
- No separate “founder vault,” shadow ledger, or special conversion pool exists.  
- All EMS balances, including founder balances, are governed by Phase 0/1/2/3 rules, reserve-ratio floors, and safety mechanisms defined in the Program Rules.

MountainShares EMS remains a non‑appreciating, closed-loop mutual credit within the DUNA. No EMS balance, including founder balances, creates equity or ownership in H4H, the DUNA, KTS, or any other entity.

### 1.3 Governance and Participation Weight

Founder pre-launch EMS counts in full for:

- **DAO governance participation:** All logged founder EMS contributes to governance standing, voting weight, and proposal rights under the Governance Charter.  
- **Participation weight:** Pre-launch EMS is counted as Verified Months Active for participation-weight calculations under Phase 3 and related specifications, subject to Activity Multiplier tables.  
- **Permanent recognition:** Pre-launch EMS is permanently recorded and cannot be reduced, expired, or removed by governance vote, except in cases of documented fraud or error under defined audit procedures.

Founder governance weight is understood as **stewardship** of the commons, not ownership. No EMS balance conveys equity or an ownership stake in any entity.

## 2. EMS Conversion and Phase Gates (Founder Account)

This section governs how EMS held by the founder’s account may convert within the EMS system. It does not create any separate founder-only schedule.

### 2.1 Phase 0 and Phase 1

During Phase 0 and Phase 1:

- The founder may earn additional EMS under the same rules as any participant.  
- Founder EMS is subject to the same per-user M$ monthly caps, redemption limits, and usage rules as any participant at her assigned tier.  
- No special USD cash-out rights, accelerated schedules, or exceptions are created for founder EMS.

Founder EMS is not treated as a bonus, equity instrument, or investment contract. It is the same non‑appreciating mutual credit as any other account, bounded by Phase 0/1 safety rules.

### 2.2 Phase 2

Upon Phase 2 activation:

- Founder EMS follows the same conversion rules, caps, and reserve-ratio conditions as all other participant accounts.  
- Any adjustments to founder roles in Phase 2 (e.g., additional stewardship responsibilities, conflict-of-interest procedures, or recusal requirements) are defined in the Governance Charter and related governance documents, not via special EMS economics.

The DAO may adopt additional safeguards or role definitions for the founder, but it may not grant special equity-like rights, appreciation, or privileged conversion mechanics to founder EMS.

### 2.3 Phase 3

Upon Phase 3 activation:

- Founder EMS may convert to M$ and, where permitted, to USD under the same monthly caps, internal schedules, and reserve-ratio floors as any other participant at her tier.  
- If the reserve ratio falls below the Phase 3 floor, founder conversions pause on the same basis and timeline as all other participants.

There is no lump-sum founder EMS conversion, no preferential rate, and no separate “founder vault” at Phase 3. Founder EMS participates on equal terms with community EMS.

## 3. Founder Role and Stewardship

This section clarifies founder responsibilities and how they intersect with EMS.

- The founder is a **steward of the commons**, not an owner. Founder EMS and governance roles do not confer equity or ownership in H4H, the DUNA, KTS, or the MountainShares treasury.  
- The founder may hold roles on both the nonprofit side (H4H) and the technical/commercial side (KTS). All related-party transactions and potential conflicts-of-interest are handled through written recusal and approval processes defined by H4H and the DAO.  
- Any separate founder capital or equity interests (for example, within KTS) are documented outside this schedule and operate independently from EMS balances.

This Founder's EMS Vesting Schedule governs **only** EMS treatment inside the MountainShares program and does not define or control any KTS equity, trust, or revenue participation instruments.

***

## 4. Benefits-Sensitive Design (Founder EMS)

The founder participates in the MountainShares program under benefits-sensitive circumstances. This section describes design intent only and is not legal, tax, or benefits advice.

- EMS is designed as a non‑appreciating mutual-credit system with explicit benefits-safety protections, applied equally to founder and non-founder accounts.  
- The founder’s EMS is subject to the same phase rules, caps, and Benefits-Sensitive Account protections as any other participant.  
- Any separate income, distributions, or equity returns outside EMS (for example, from KTS or other sources) are governed by separate instruments and applicable benefits rules, not by EMS or this schedule.

Participants, including the founder, are encouraged to seek professional benefits and legal counsel before making decisions involving conversions or other financial steps.

## 5. Disclosure and Transparency

### 5.1 Annual Governance Report

The annual MountainShares governance report shall disclose:

- Total founder EMS hours recorded (as of the reporting date).  
- How founder EMS is treated identically to community EMS with respect to caps, conversion rules, and reserve-ratio protections.  
- Any governance roles or stewardship responsibilities held by the founder relevant to EMS governance and risk management.

The report will not present founder EMS as equity or as an ownership stake. It is reported as contribution history and EMS participation only.

### 5.2 Participant Transparency

All participants are informed that:

- The founder contributed substantial pre-launch hours that are fully documented and recognized in the EMS ledger.  
- Founder EMS operates under the same rules and safety mechanisms as community EMS; there is no hidden founder extraction mechanism inside the EMS program.  
- Founder capital or equity, if any, resides in separate instruments outside EMS and is not part of the community mutual-credit system.

This transparency is intended to reinforce trust that the EMS commons serves the community first and that founder participation is aligned with that purpose.
