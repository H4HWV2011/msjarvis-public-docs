# MountainShares DAO

> *"Keep value, voice, and decision-making rooted in Appalachian communities."*
> — MountainShares DAO Overview

MountainShares is a DAO-based economic and governance system designed to circulate value within participating local communities rather than letting it leak outward through distant financial intermediaries. It is a **closed-loop payment and community governance instrument** — not a bank, not a security, not a speculative asset — built and governed by Harmony for Hope, Inc. in Mount Hope, West Virginia.

This folder contains the complete specification, governance, legal, and operational documentation for MountainShares: from the Phase 0 beta launch through Phase 3 surplus distribution, including the governance charter, program rules, KPIs, safety protocols, and funder materials.

---

## What MountainShares Is

MountainShares operates as a **community-governed economic commons** — a deliberately designed system in which:

- Participants load external funds into a MountainShares-denominated closed-loop balance
- That balance circulates within the local economy according to DAO-defined rules
- Fees are concentrated at the boundaries (entry and merchant side), making everyday local spending low-friction
- Governance decisions — including rule changes, resource allocation, and constitutional amendments — are made by community participants, not by a central authority
- Ms. Allis serves as an aligned, glass-box AI advisor to the system, with her own MountainShares wallet, published reward caps, and no independent governance authority

MountainShares does not replace or act as a bank, credit union, or regulated financial institution, and participation is not an opportunity to purchase equity, securities, or investment products of any kind.
