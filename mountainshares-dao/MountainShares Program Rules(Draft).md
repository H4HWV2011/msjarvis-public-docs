These Program Rules are **incorporated by reference** into the MountainShares Rewards Program Terms and Conditions ("Terms"). In the event of any conflict between these Program Rules and the Terms, the Terms control.

- **MountainShares Rewards Program – Terms and Conditions** (legal framing)

These Program Rules define **changeable operational parameters** — rates, caps, thresholds, limits, and categories — within the bounds set by those higher-level documents. The Parameter Tables document is the live authoritative reference for any value that may change through governance.

### 1.2 Governance of Program Rules

Program Rules may be updated through:

- Decisions of the Harmony for Hope Board of Directors
- Recommendations or votes adopted through the MountainShares DAO, where applicable
- Legal, compliance, risk-management, or safety interventions required by Applicable Law

Changes are implemented and communicated in accordance with the "Governance, Changes, and Termination" sections of the Terms and the DAO Governance Charter. Automatic downward adjustments in response to reserve ratio declines cannot be disabled, delayed, or overridden by any governance vote at any threshold.

### 1.3 Hard Trigger Supremacy

Certain rules in these Program Rules are **hard triggers** — enforced automatically by Ms. Allis and the Phase Management Controller. Hard triggers cannot be overridden by any DAO vote at any threshold, by any H4H Board decision, or by any other governance mechanism. They protect the structural integrity of the commons. Where these Program Rules state that a threshold "cannot be overridden," that statement is absolute.

## 2. Eligibility and Account Types

### 2.1 Geographic Scope

- Phase 1 and Phase 2 deployment is limited to Users and Merchants whose primary residence or operations are located within the State of West Virginia, unless expressly authorized in writing by Harmony for Hope.
- Phase 3 statewide scope continues within West Virginia. Multi-state expansion, if initiated, requires a 75% super-majority DAO vote, 90-day community comment period, legal review, and new state regulatory registration. See Phase 3 Specification.
- Additional regions (for example, broader AppalachianShares or other branches) may be added in later phases as separate governed regions with their own parameter sets and independent reserve structures.

### 2.2 User Eligibility (All Phases)

Baseline eligibility criteria include, at minimum:

- Minimum age: 18 years (or as permitted by applicable law and policy)
- Residency: Primary residence address in the applicable deployment geography
- Identity verification: KYC and other checks as required by risk tier, partner requirements, and Applicable Law
- Governance voting eligibility: Minimum 10 verified EMS hours + 30 days on-platform (one UEI = one vote; no financial stake required)

Specific thresholds and procedures are documented in the Parameter Tables and partner integration documents.

### 2.3 Account Types

**Standard User Account**
Default account type for individuals participating in the Program. Subject to per-tier caps defined in the Parameter Tables.

**Merchant / Organization Account**
For approved local businesses, nonprofits, and public entities that accept and spend MountainShares within The Commons ecosystem. Merchant fee (1.80%) applies to eligible transactions.

**Benefits-Sensitive Account**
For Users who receive or may receive means-tested public benefits. Additional protections, caps, and restrictions apply as described in Section 8. Participation does not require disclosure of benefit status — protections are participant-controlled and opt-in. Phase 3 adds a 60-day advance notice requirement and EMS Recognition Bonus election option before any distribution event.

### 2.4 UEI (Unique Economic Identity)

Each participant in the commons is represented by a single UEI — their persistent identity in the recognition ledger. One UEI = one governance vote. UEIs carry forward permanently across all phases. A suspended or frozen UEI loses governance rights during the suspension period and is ineligible for dividend distribution while suspended. Sybil activity (creation of multiple UEIs to gain disproportionate rewards or distribution share) is a serious violation subject to permanent PW forfeiture and account action.

## 3. Currency Types and Units

### 3.1 Purchased MountainShares (PMS / M$)

- PMS are issued when Users load fiat currency (USD) into The Commons via approved payment channels.
- PMS are fully spendable within the network, subject to limits and fee schedules defined in the Parameter Tables.
- PMS are issued at a nominal internal rate of 1 M$ per 1 USD loaded, less applicable fees, solely for use within the Program.
- The Operational Reserve Ratio governs the backing of all outstanding spendable M$:

```
Operational Reserve Ratio = Treasury USDC Reserve / Total Outstanding Spendable PMS (M$)
```

### 3.2 Earned MountainShares (EMS)

- EMS are credited as rewards for documented and verified community contributions — volunteering, mutual aid, education, cultural work, historic preservation, governance participation, data stewardship, community connectivity node stewardship, and other qualifying activities defined in Section 4.
- EMS are first recorded in an **unlimited recognition ledger** (the EMS vault), then converted into spendable M$ subject to the applicable Phase Specification (base rates, per-user caps, and reserve-ratio-gated limits).
- **EMS is subject to the phase-based, reserve-gated, participant-elected conversion mechanics in Section 3.3, and confers no equity, ownership, or profit-sharing interest.** Phase-based buying-power expansion is conditional, deferred, and subject to automatic reversion. The securities-law treatment of EMS and the conversion mechanism is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether it is or is not a security or investment contract.
- The Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio governs EMS buying-power expansion:

```
Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio = Treasury Reserve / (Total Outstanding EMS + Total Outstanding M$)
```

### 3.3 EMS Buying-Power Expansion Schedule (Phase-Gated)

| Phase | EMS Buying-Power Rate | Activation Gate | Reversion Trigger |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | 1 EMS = $1.00 USD equivalent (baseline) | Default | N/A |
| **Phase 2** | 1 EMS = $10.00 USD equivalent (10×) | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 300% + all formation fees paid | Ratio drops below 300% → automatic reversion to Phase 1 rate |
| **Phase 3** | 1 EMS = **FVSR** in effect on Phase 3 activation date | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 500% + all prior-phase transition costs paid | Ratio drops below 500% → reversion to Phase 2; below 300% → reversion to Phase 1 |

> **Federal Volunteer Service Rate (FVSR):** The IRS-published standard rate for valuing volunteer services, updated annually. The Phase 3 EMS buying-power rate is the FVSR in effect on the date the DAO formally activates Phase 3 — not the rate at document publication. The current rate (2026) is **$36.14**. If Phase 3 activates in a later year, the applicable rate is that year's published FVSR. Ms. Allis surfaces the current FVSR on the dashboard each calendar year. The DAO does not vote on the FVSR — it is an external, IRS-determined figure.

**Reversion cascade (automatic, no DAO vote required):**
- Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below 500%: Phase 3 buying-power expansion reverts to Phase 2 rate ($10) immediately
- Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below 300%: all buying-power expansion reverts to Phase 1 baseline ($1) immediately
- No resident loses their principal EMS balance in any reversion scenario
- Reversion is logged to the durable audit trail with timestamp, triggering metric, and restored parameters
- System re-advances to the higher rate only after the applicable threshold is restored for two consecutive quarters

### 3.4 Units and Denomination

- The base unit is **one (1) MountainShare**, denoted **M$** in internal documents.
- Internal valuations per phase (for in-network buying power) are set out in Section 3.3 and in the Parameter Tables.
- Internal valuations do not establish any external price, redemption right, or profit expectation.

## 4. Earning Rules (EMS)

### 4.1 Qualifying Activities (All Phases)

Qualifying activities for EMS earning may include, but are not limited to:

- Community care and caregiving
- Mutual aid, peer support, and informal social care
- Cultural preservation, arts, and Appalachian heritage work
- Education, learning, teaching, mentoring, and skill-sharing
- Asset mapping, GIS work, data validation, and local knowledge capture
- Participation in approved community events, historic trail activities, and Commons-hosted programs
- **Phase 2 additions:** Regional governance, merchant onboarding, Stage 3 commerce support, county activation leadership, community connectivity node stewardship (siting, deploying, maintaining, and monitoring free public internet nodes)
- **Phase 3 additions:** Dividend clock governance participation, surplus pool transparency reporting, long-term data stewardship anchor roles

> Education, mutual aid, and cultural preservation remain qualifying EMS-earning activities even though they are no longer named fee-allocation buckets. The fee-allocation change in Section 6.4 redirects fee revenue toward free public internet nodes; it does not remove these activities from the commons. They may also be funded through H4H operations or grants.

Each category is governed by:
- **Verification methods** — time-tracking, partner attestations, task confirmations, or digital checkpoints
- **Eligible roles** — organizer, facilitator, participant, documenter, researcher, or governance leader
- **Anti-gaming rules** — dividend farming (simulating activity to inflate Participation Weight) and Sybil activity are prohibited and subject to permanent PW forfeiture

Detailed activity lists and verification procedures are maintained in partner agreements and operational guidelines.

### 4.2 Base Earning Rates by Phase

| Phase | Recognition Rate | Conversion Base Rate Floor | Conversion Base Rate Ceiling | Ceiling Unlock |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | 1 EMS hr per 1 verified qualifying hr | 0.10 M$ / EMS hr | 1.00 M$ / EMS hr | Operational Reserve Ratio ≥ 1.5; DAO vote |
| **Phase 2** | 1 EMS hr per 1 verified qualifying hr | 0.50 M$ / EMS hr | 2.00 M$ / EMS hr | Operational Reserve Ratio ≥ 3.0; DAO vote |
| **Phase 3** | 1 EMS hr per 1 verified qualifying hr | 1.00 M$ / EMS hr | 3.00 M$ / EMS hr | Operational Reserve Ratio ≥ 5.0; DAO vote |

The precise base rate in effect at any time is published via Ms. Allis dashboards, and may vary within the allowed range in response to reserve conditions and governance decisions.

### 4.3 Per-User Caps and Global Caps

Per-user caps for converted, spendable M$ are defined by phase and tier in the Parameter Tables. The governing values are:

**Phase 1:** Tier 1 — 40 M$/mo; Tier 2 — 80 M$/mo
**Phase 2:** Tier 1 — 80 M$/mo; Tier 2 — 160 M$/mo; Tier 3 — 240 M$/mo
**Phase 3:** Tier 1 — 120 M$/mo; Tier 2 — 240 M$/mo; Tier 3 — 360 M$/mo

Weekly and daily caps may be applied in hours relative to the current base rate to smooth issuance across the month.

Global caps: Program Rules may define a maximum aggregate amount of EMS convertible to spendable M$ per region per month, calculated to preserve the reserve ratio thresholds in the applicable Phase Specification. All caps are parameterized and may be adjusted by governance within the safety bands defined in the applicable Phase Specification.

### 4.4 Vaulting and Unlocking

- EMS may be credited but **vaulted** (visible in the recognition ledger but not immediately spendable) in all phases.
- Vaulted EMS is not circulating supply and does not count against the Operational Reserve Ratio until conversion.
- Unlocking of vaulted EMS is tied to: phase activation conditions; governance decisions within allowed bounds; user tiers, ranks, or verified roles; and treasury and reserve conditions.
- **No participant is ever automatically converted.** Each participant controls their own conversion timing in all phases. This is a hard guarantee — the Phase Management Controller cannot force conversion.
- Vaulting and unlocking rules are applied transparently and are subject to audit through Ms. Allis reporting and the public governance ledger.

### 4.5 Phase 3 Participation Weight (Dividend Eligibility)

In Phase 3, EMS earning history is the basis for Participation Weight (PW), which determines each eligible UEI's share of the 7-year community dividend surplus pool:

```
Participation Weight (PW) =
  Verified Months Active (within the 7-year accumulation window)
  × Activity Multiplier
```

| Participation Level | Verified Active Months | Multiplier |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum | 1–6 months | 1.0× |
| Regular | 7–24 months | 1.25× |
| Sustained | 25–60 months | 1.5× |
| Founding Deep | 61–84 months (≥ 5 years continuous) | 2.0× |

The dividend is not a financial instrument — it is a community dividend paid from genuine surplus above the 500% floor, to the people who built the commons. Full dividend mechanics are in the Phase 3 Specification.

## 5. Phase Mechanics and Internal Buying Power

### 5.1 Phase Definitions

| Phase | Internal Buying-Power Rate | Reserve Gate | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 – Launch** | 1 EMS = $1.00 USD equivalent | Default | Active |
| **Phase 2 – Regional Growth** | 1 EMS = $10.00 USD equivalent | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 300% | Conditionally active when gate is open |
| **Phase 3 – Surplus Distribution** | 1 EMS = FVSR (currently $36.14 for 2026) | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 500% | Conditionally active when gate is open |

These internal values are used solely for in-network redemption capacity and accounting. They do not establish any external price, redemption right, or profit expectation. They are conditional, deferred, and subject to automatic reversion.

### 5.2 Phase Transition Conditions

Transitions between phases occur only when internal conditions specified in the applicable Phase Specification are met and verified, including:

- Minimum and sustained **Operational Reserve Ratio** thresholds and stability windows
- **Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio** at or above the applicable phase gate threshold for the required consecutive period
- All prior-phase Treasury Reserve-funded formation and transition costs paid before the higher buying-power rate activates
- Minimum levels of monthly processed volume
- Minimum active UEI counts and community participation thresholds
- Absence of unresolved systemic fraud or reserve-threatening exploits
- Sufficient governance participation and quorum on relevant proposals
- All required WV and federal money-services registrations and licenses (held by the DUNA) confirmed current and in good standing; H4H nonprofit standing current

If there is any difference between this document and the applicable Phase Specification, the Phase Specification controls.

### 5.3 Parameter Ranges and Governance Control

For each phase, the Parameter Tables specify permissible ranges for:
- Global base earning rate (EMS → M$)
- Per-user and per-tier caps
- Promo and bonus pool percentages
- Reserve band thresholds

Parameters may be adjusted by:
- **Automatic mechanisms (hard triggers enforced by Ms. Allis):** Downward adjustments in response to reserve ratio declines. Cannot be disabled, delayed, or overridden by governance.
- **Governance proposals and DAO votes:** Upward or structural adjustments, subject to applicable super-majority thresholds.
- **H4H risk and compliance overrides:** For safety and legal reasons, within documented bounds.

### 5.4 Reversion Rules

- If the Operational Reserve Ratio drops below the applicable phase floor: the Phase Management Controller automatically reverts to prior-phase operational parameters.
- If the Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio drops below the applicable conversion gate threshold: EMS buying-power expansion automatically reverts to the prior phase's rate.
- Both reversion types are automatic, logged, and communicated in plain language to all participants within 24 hours.
- Neither reversion type requires or permits a DAO vote to override.

## 7. Spending and Transfers

### 7.1 Permitted Uses

MountainShares may be used, subject to the Terms and Program Rules, to:

- Purchase eligible goods and services from approved Merchants
- Participate in events, education, cultural programming, and historic trail activities listed in The Commons
- Conduct documented in-platform sales or exchanges between Users where such functionality is explicitly enabled
- **Phase 2:** Full Stage 3 commerce transactions, merchant onboarding exchanges, county activation participation
- **Phase 3:** Dividend distribution elections (M$ credit, USD cash-out, or EMS Recognition Bonus) within the rules of Section 8 and the Phase 3 Specification

### 7.2 Prohibited and Restricted Uses

Program Rules identify categories that are prohibited or subject to additional restrictions, consistent with the Terms, including:

- Illicit or illegal goods and services
- Certain regulated categories (gambling, unlicensed financial services) unless explicitly authorized
- Uses that jeopardize regulatory status or public assistance safety
- **Dividend farming:** Simulating or fabricating community activity to artificially inflate Participation Weight — a serious violation with permanent PW forfeiture

- **Structural boundary breach:** Any mechanism that creates founder equity through H4H, community EMS, or the surplus pool

Ms. Allis and H4H may monitor, flag, and restrict transactions in these categories, with logging and review procedures in the governance documents.

### 7.3 Internal Transfers

- Internal user-to-user transfers are subject to: eligibility of sender and receiver accounts; specific EMS transfer limitations, especially for Benefits-Sensitive Accounts; and transaction minimums, maximums, and rate limits.
- Program Rules specify conditions under which transfers may be paused, reversed, or blocked due to suspected fraud, non-compliance, or safety concerns.
- No transfer may be used to circumvent per-user caps, Benefits-Sensitive Account protections, or the structural separation between EMS/M$ and any founder-side instrument.

### 9.2 Quests and Value Interactions

- "Value interactions" are concrete actions such as fulfilled help requests, event attendance, successful asset mappings, or other documented contributions.
- Program Rules specify which interactions are counted and whether they carry EMS rewards, badges, or other recognition.
- Quests and streaks may be defined to encourage participation in priority activities, consistent with reserve-ratio limits and per-user caps.
- **No quest or streak bonus may circumvent per-user caps.** Bonuses draw from the promo pool and count against monthly caps.

### 9.3 Governance-Sensitive Game Parameters

Program Rules identify which gamification parameters (quest rewards, streak bonuses, rank thresholds) may be adjusted by governance, and within what safe ranges, to prevent exploitation or over-incentivization. Parameters that could affect Phase 3 Participation Weight calculations are subject to the additional constraint that no change may reduce the relative share of long-tenured participants below a governance-defined floor.

## 10. Regional and Branch-Level Parameters

### 10.1 Regional Overrides

- MountainShares Phase 1 and Phase 2 are deployed within West Virginia. Phase 3 continues statewide.
- Multi-state expansion requires 75% super-majority DAO vote + 90-day community comment period + legal review + new state regulatory registration. Each new state activation generates eligible money-services Compliance Draw categories (Stream M) per the Phase 3 Specification.
- Future branches (AppalachianShares, CoastalShares, or other regional commons) may be added as separate governed regions with independent reserve structures, their own parameter sets, and their own UEI populations.
- Program Rules identify which parameters are global (hard triggers, entity structural rules, the EMS reversion cascade and no-forced-conversion guarantee, the free-public-internet public-good guarantee) and which may be overridden at the regional level (activity categories, Benefits-Sensitive Account thresholds, promo pool targeting).

### 10.2 Inter-Region Interchange

If inter-region interchange is enabled:

- Program Rules define permitted exchange mechanisms, caps, and internal rates for cross-region flows.
- Safeguards prevent risk contagion between regions — separate reserves, limits on net flows, and circuit-breaker conditions are required before interchange activates.
- Interchange between regions does not create a path by which value leaves the commons without being recognized as a conversion event subject to applicable fees and reserve checks.

## Appendix A: Definitions

- **Operational Reserve Ratio:** Treasury USDC Reserve ÷ Total Outstanding Spendable PMS (M$). Governs day-to-day continuity of service, per-user caps, and phase commerce feature unlock. Enforced daily by Ms. Allis.
- **Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio:** Treasury Reserve ÷ (Total Outstanding EMS + Total Outstanding M$). Governs EMS buying-power expansion and is the primary conversion gate. Enforced daily by Ms. Allis.
- **Federal Volunteer Service Rate (FVSR):** The IRS-published standard rate for valuing volunteer services, updated annually. The Phase 3 EMS buying-power rate is the FVSR in effect on the date the DAO formally activates Phase 3 — not the rate at document publication. The current rate (2026) is $36.14. If Phase 3 activates in a later year, the applicable rate is that year's published FVSR. Ms. Allis surfaces the current FVSR on the dashboard each calendar year. The DAO does not vote on the FVSR.
- **EMS (Earned MountainShares):** Community contribution recognition credits. Subject to conditional, reserve-gated, participant-elected conversion; not vested; subject to the reversion cascade; confer no equity or ownership interest. Securities treatment under counsel review.
- **PMS (Purchased MountainShares / M$):** Prepaid stored-value units issued on fiat load. Fully spendable within the network subject to caps and fees.
- **UEI (Unique Economic Identity):** Each participant's persistent identity in the recognition ledger. One UEI = one governance vote. Carries forward permanently across all phases.
- **Benefits-Sensitive Account (BSA):** A participant account with enhanced protections due to means-tested benefit receipt. PMS cash-out disabled by default. Conversion timing participant-controlled. Phase 3 adds advance notice and EMS Recognition Bonus election.
- **EMS Recognition Bonus:** Phase 3 optional distribution form — convert dividend to permanent EMS recognition hours rather than M$ or USD. Irrevocable per event. Income/asset treatment under benefits-counsel review.
- **Participation Weight (PW):** Phase 3 dividend allocation score — Verified Months Active × Activity Multiplier. Reflects actual presence and contribution in the commons over the 7-year accumulation window.
- **Founding Deep:** Highest Phase 3 participation tier — 61–84 verified active months; 2.0× activity multiplier.
- **Community Connectivity Nodes:** MountainShares free public internet nodes (Wi-Fi and LoRa community mesh) deployed at trailheads, civic sites, libraries, and community anchors across West Virginia. Funded by fee-allocation Bucket 4 (operations) and Bucket 5 (buildout); kept open to the public as a public good, never metered or paywalled.
- **Reversion Cascade:** Automatic, no-vote-required process by which EMS buying-power expansion reverts to a prior phase's rate when the Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below the applicable threshold.
- **Hard Trigger:** A system rule enforced automatically by Ms. Allis and the Phase Management Controller. Cannot be overridden by any governance vote at any threshold.
- **Deferral Queue:** Ms. Allis-managed queue of approved but reserve-floor-constrained Compliance Draws (Stream N / Stream M). Fire automatically when the treasury can accommodate them without breaching the applicable floor.
- **Three-Box Structure:** The entity wall separating H4H (charitable anchor), MountainShares Commons/DUNA (community ledger and money-services licensee), and KTS (founder commercial engine). No Program Rules provision may create a path through which founder value is realized via H4H or community EMS.
- **Structural Boundary Breach:** Any mechanism, transaction, or governance action that creates founder equity through H4H, community EMS, or the surplus pool. A Critical finding requiring immediate resolution.
