## How to Become a Community Champion

- Read The Commons Terms of Use, Community Guidelines, and the Community Champion / Red Team Participation Agreement.
- Create or log into your Commons account and complete basic profile setup.
- Request or accept an invitation to the Community Champion program (Phase 0 / pilot).
- Review a short orientation (slides, video, or FAQ) explaining scope, reporting channels, and safety expectations.
- Confirm your acceptance of the Community Champion Agreement in-app or via form.
- Receive your Unique Ecosystem Identifier (UEI) and any initial test assignments.
- Begin testing and reporting findings through the designated channels.

---

## How to Become a Safety Champion Organization

- Identify an internal lead (person) who will serve as the primary Safety Champion.
- Review: Community Guidelines, Community Champion Agreement, and Safety Champion Participation Agreement.
- Ensure the lead already has or creates a Community Champion UEI.
- Obtain internal approval (for example, from a director, PI, or department head) to act as a Safety Champion Organization.
- Complete the Safety Champion registration, including:
  - legal name of the organization;
  - contact information;
  - the lead's UEI and role.
- Review and sign any required Safety Champion Participation Agreement and MOU.
- Participate in a brief onboarding session to align on scope, reporting, and priorities.
- Begin scoped safety-testing work and periodic check-ins with the Steward/technical team.

## How to Become a University Commons Node Partner

- Identify a point of contact (faculty/staff) for data, IT, and community engagement.
- Review the **University Commons Node Partnership Overview** and relevant University policies (data governance, IRB, IT security).
- Schedule an exploratory meeting with Harmony for Hope and KTS to align on goals and potential datasets.
- Select an initial pilot scope (for example, heritage + EMS KPIs, resilience shard).
- Work with your IT and research offices to:
  - confirm infrastructure options (on-prem or cloud);
  - identify security and compliance requirements.
- Co-draft:
  - a Data Sharing / Data Use Agreement (DSA/DUA);
  - a Node Operations MOU (including any no-fee terms for software).
- Stand up a small Commons Node instance and validate data flow with test data.
- Launch the pilot in at least one course, lab, or project, and set a date for joint evaluation.

---

## How to Become a Government Commons Node Partner

- Identify an internal champion within the Agency (for example, program manager, data lead, or CIO designee).
- Review the **Government Commons Node & Ms. Allis Partnership Overview** and relevant statutes, policies, and data-governance rules.
- Schedule an exploratory consultation with Harmony for Hope and KTS to define 1–2 concrete use-cases (for example, overdose + resilience, emissions + health).
- Select initial data streams and Commons datasets that are legally and operationally feasible.
- Coordinate with Agency legal, IT, and data-governance teams to:
  - identify requirements and constraints;
  - approve a limited pilot scope.
- Co-draft:
  - Data Sharing / Data Use Agreements for Agency ↔ Commons data;
  - a Node Operations MOU and software license terms (including any no-fee pilot provisions).
- Deploy a pilot Government Node, connect one Agency data source and one Commons shard, and test dashboards or workflows.
- Set up a regular review cadence to evaluate benefits, risks, and options for expansion or formalization.
