# Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts – Plain Language Notice
*(Draft – For Discussion, Not Legal Advice)*

This notice explains special rules for **Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts** in the MountainShares Program. It is a plain‑language companion to the MountainShares Rewards Program Terms and Conditions. In any conflict, the Terms control.

---

## 1. Who This Is For

A **Benefits‑Sensitive Account** is for Users who receive or may receive means‑tested public benefits, such as:

- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- WV WORKS / TANF
- SNAP (food stamps)
- Medicaid or WV CHIP
- HUD or other housing assistance
- State child‑care subsidies (for example, programs administered by MountainHeart or similar providers)

You may ask to have your account treated as Benefits‑Sensitive, and Harmony for Hope may also apply this designation based on information you or a partner agency voluntarily provide, where permitted by law.

---

## 2. Strict Caps and Special Limits

To help reduce the risk that MountainShares might be treated as income or resources for benefits purposes, Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts are subject to **strict caps and limits**, including:

- **No cash‑out by default**  
  - PMS (Purchased MountainShares) cash conversion is disabled for Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts.  
  - In rare and extraordinary cases, limited cash conversion may be allowed only with written approval that is consistent with benefits rules, internal risk controls, and Applicable Law.

- **Balance caps and throttled earning**  
  - Harmony for Hope may set **non‑waivable caps** on total MountainShares balances.  
  - We may **throttle EMS earning** (for example, limit EMS per month) to discourage building large balances that could be treated as countable resources.

- **Transaction and transfer limits**  
  - We may limit or block certain transfers or uses that appear likely to push a Benefits‑Sensitive Account over internal thresholds.  
  - We may delay or reject transactions that appear inconsistent with these safeguards, Program Rules, or Applicable Law.

These caps and limits are Program safety measures only. They do not guarantee that a benefits agency will ignore your MountainShares.

---

## 3. Warnings, Education, and Identification

For Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts, we will:

- Show **clear warnings and prompts** in the interface if your balance or activity approaches internal thresholds that may increase risk.  
- Label the account internally as “Benefits‑Sensitive” so that Program safety rules, limits, and monitoring apply consistently.  
- Encourage you to review materials about how MountainShares may affect public assistance and to talk to a benefits planner or caseworker before relying on MountainShares for essentials like rent, food, or medical bills.

These tools are designed to help you make informed choices, not to give legal, tax, or benefits advice.

---

## 4. What We Can and Cannot Promise

Harmony for Hope:

- **Can**  
  - Design MountainShares as closed‑loop community rewards.  
  - Apply strict caps, throttled earning, and no‑cash‑out rules for Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts.  
  - Provide clear notices, warnings, and documentation for you and for interested agencies.

- **Cannot**  
  - Control how SSA, DHHR, HUD, housing authorities, or other agencies treat MountainShares for eligibility and benefit calculations.  
  - Guarantee that MountainShares will have **no impact** on your benefits.  
  - Take responsibility for reporting requirements that apply to you under benefits rules.

By using a Benefits‑Sensitive Account, you acknowledge that:

- MountainShares **may** be treated as income, in‑kind support, or a resource for benefits purposes; and  
- Holding or using MountainShares **may** affect your eligibility or benefit amounts, including possible reductions, overpayments, or recoupment.

You remain responsible for:

- Reporting your participation, balances, and usage to agencies when required.  
- Asking your caseworker, benefits planner, or legal advisor how MountainShares might affect you before you build or spend larger balances.

---

## 5. Our Intent and Your Safety

The goal of these rules is to **help protect** people on means‑tested benefits while still allowing them to participate in The Commons and the MountainShares Program.

- We design guardrails to reduce risk and make impacts more predictable.  
- We do **not** encourage anyone on benefits to treat MountainShares as savings or as a replacement for guaranteed benefits.  
- If you believe your account should be treated as Benefits‑Sensitive, or you think your designation is wrong, please contact us through the support channels listed in The Commons.

Nothing in this notice is legal advice. You should always rely on guidance from your benefits agencies and qualified advisors when making decisions about your benefits.
