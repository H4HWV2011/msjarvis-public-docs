## 1. Who This Is For

This notice is for anyone who receives or may receive means-tested public benefits, including:

- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- WV WORKS / TANF
- SNAP (food stamps / food assistance)
- Medicaid or WV CHIP
- HUD or other federal and state housing assistance
- State child-care subsidies (for example, programs administered by MountainHeart or similar WV providers)
- Any other program that sets income or asset limits on eligibility

You may ask to have your account designated as a **Benefits-Sensitive Account (BSA)** at any time, and you can remove this designation at any time with one click in your account settings.

**If you are unsure whether this applies to you, you can turn on BSA protections as a precaution.** You control this setting. No verification is required, no questions are asked, and you can change it whenever your situation changes. BSA status is private — it is never shared with other Commons members, never reported to any government agency, and never sold or shared with any third party.

## 2. What EMS Is — And What It Is Not

Understanding EMS correctly is the most important protection available to benefits-sensitive participants. EMS is not what it might appear to be at first glance.

**EMS (Earned MountainShares) is a recognition credit for verified community contribution — one credit per verified hour.**

It is:
- A record in the community recognition ledger of time you contributed to the commons
- Comparable to traditional time bank credits — the same model used by community time banks nationwide
- Non-taxable while vaulted, under time banking legal framework — benefits-counsel review applies; no MountainShares-specific Private Letter Ruling has been obtained
- Permanent and non-transferable — it cannot be sold, gifted, or taken away
- Visible on your dashboard in the recognition ledger

It is **not:**
- Income when you earn it
- A bank balance or savings account
- Convertible to cash automatically — conversion requires your deliberate election
- Worth more than $1.00 equivalent per hour in Phase 1
- Subject to higher buying-power rates unless and until the applicable Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio threshold is cleared and maintained

> **EMS and M$ confer no equity, ownership, membership interest, or profit-sharing interest in H4H, the DUNA, KTS, or any entity. The securities-law treatment of EMS and M$ is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether either is or is not a security.**

**The buying-power rates in later phases are conditional — not guaranteed:**

| Phase | EMS Buying-Power Rate | Condition for Activation |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 (current)** | 1 EMS = $1.00 USD equivalent | Default — active now |
| **Phase 2** | 1 EMS = $10.00 USD equivalent | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 300% for 2+ consecutive quarters; all transition costs paid; automatic reversion if ratio falls below 300% |
| **Phase 3** | 1 EMS = **FVSR** in effect on Phase 3 activation date (current 2026 rate: $36.14) | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 500% for 2+ consecutive quarters; all prior-phase costs paid; automatic reversion if ratio falls below 500% |

No participant — BSA-flagged or otherwise — holds a vested right to Phase 2 or Phase 3 buying-power rates. The higher rates activate only if the treasury achieves the required threshold and remain active only as long as the treasury maintains it. If the treasury falls below the threshold, the system automatically reverts. This is not a risk — it is the safety architecture.

**No equity instrument reaches any resident through EMS, M$, BSA, or any other Commons account mechanism.** Any document, communication, or representation to the contrary is incorrect and should be reported to H4H immediately. Securities-law treatment is under review by securities counsel; this document makes no representation as to whether EMS or M$ is or is not a security.

## 3. What PMS / M$ Is — And What It Is Not

PMS (Purchased MountainShares), denoted as M$ in the system, is the spendable layer of MountainShares.

**M$ is a prepaid stored-value balance — like a prepaid debit card balance.**

It is:
- Created when you deposit your own USD into the system via an approved payment channel
- Spendable inside The Commons for goods and services from local merchants
- Subject to monthly loading caps for BSA accounts (to help you stay within asset limits)
- Available for ATM cash-out subject to program rules, availability, and fees

It is **not:**
- A bank account or savings account
- Earnings or income (it is your own deposited money)
- An investment or equity instrument
- Guaranteed to be available for cash-out in unlimited amounts at any time

For BSA participants, the most important rule is the **monthly loading cap** — BSA accounts have a default cap on how much M$ can be loaded per month, designed to help you stay under the asset limits for programs like SSI ($2,000 individual limit). The current cap value is published in the [Program Rules – Parameter Tables](../mountainshares-dao/Program%20Rules%20%E2%80%93%20Parameter%20Tables.md).

## 4. How BSA Protections Work in Each Phase

### Phase 1 — Current Operation

```
EMS status: Vaulted recognition credits. Non-taxable under time banking framework (benefits-counsel review applies; no MountainShares-specific PLR). Not income. Not assets.
M$ status: Prepaid stored value. Your own deposited money. Subject to BSA loading cap.
Conversion: EMS → M$ conversion available within program caps, but does not activate Phase 2 rates.
BSA loading cap: Active. Defaults to protect SSI $2,000 individual asset limit.
Forced conversion: Never. You always choose.
```

**What BSA protects in Phase 1:**
- M$ monthly loading is capped automatically once you approach the BSA threshold
- No automatic conversion of vaulted EMS — it stays in the recognition ledger
- Benefits counselor referral appears in the app when you approach any Phase 2-related decision

### Phase 2 — Conditional on Treasury Threshold

```
EMS buying-power rate: 1 EMS = $10.00 equivalent — ONLY active if Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 300%
Conversion: You choose whether to convert. How much. When. All, some, or none.
Income timing: Converting EMS to M$ or USD may create countable income in the month you convert — benefits-counsel review applies.
Reversion: If Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below 300%, Phase 2 rate deactivates automatically.
BSA protection in Phase 2: Benefits counselor referral displayed prominently before any conversion.
```

**What BSA protects in Phase 2:**
- Prominent benefits counselor referral before any Phase 2 conversion prompt
- System will not automatically convert vaulted EMS — your election is required for each conversion
- You can convert a small amount first and observe the impact on your benefits before proceeding
- You can disable BSA status if you are no longer receiving benefits and proceed without the referral prompts
- Staged conversion support: the system allows you to schedule partial conversions across months

**Income arises at conversion, not at earning.** Vaulted EMS that you have not converted is not income and is not a countable asset under the time banking framework. The moment you elect to convert EMS to M$ or cash — that is when income may arise, and that is when you must consider your benefits program rules. Benefits-counsel review applies; no MountainShares-specific Private Letter Ruling has been obtained.

### Phase 3 — Conditional on Higher Treasury Threshold

```
EMS buying-power rate: 1 EMS = FVSR in effect on Phase 3 activation date (current 2026 rate: $36.14)
Condition: Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio ≥ 500% for 2+ consecutive quarters; all prior-phase costs paid
Conversion: Same user-choice framework as Phase 2 — you control all, some, or none
60-day advance notice: Sent to all BSA participants before any Phase 3 distribution event
EMS Recognition Bonus: An alternative to cash conversion — described in Section 6 below
```

**What BSA protects in Phase 3:**
- **60-day advance notice** before any Phase 3 distribution event fires — giving you time to consult a benefits counselor before any income is created
- **Projected allocation information** provided before the distribution event, so you can calculate the impact before electing
- **EMS Recognition Bonus election** available (see Section 6) — allows you to receive permanent recognition hours instead of M$ or USD if you need to avoid creating income or assets
- Benefits counselor referral displayed prominently at every Phase 3 decision point
- System will never force a Phase 3 conversion — your election is required

## 5. Managing Your BSA Status

### One-Click Toggle in Account Settings

```
Current BSA status: Enabled | Disabled
One button to change it.
No verification required. No paperwork. No waiting.
```

**When you enable BSA protections:**
- M$ monthly loading cap activates (see current limit in Parameter Tables)
- System throttles loading automatically as you approach the cap
- Phase 2 and Phase 3 conversion prompts include prominent benefits counselor referrals
- Phase 3 advance notices are sent to you 60 days before any distribution event
- EMS vaulted credits are never capped — you can log unlimited hours; they remain in the recognition ledger
- You are not restricted from participating in any Commons activity

**When you disable BSA protections:**
- Monthly loading caps removed (subject to standard tier caps)
- Phase 2 and Phase 3 conversion interfaces open fully without the additional referral prompts
- You are responsible for managing your own benefits impact
- You can re-enable at any time if your circumstances change

**You can toggle BSA status any number of times.** Every change is logged in your private account audit trail with timestamp. The log is visible to you and to H4H compliance only — never to other participants, never to government agencies.

## 6. The EMS Recognition Bonus — Phase 3 Option for BSA Participants

The EMS Recognition Bonus is a Phase 3 election designed specifically for participants who need to avoid receiving income or countable assets at a distribution event.

When a Phase 3 distribution event fires, BSA participants are offered the option to elect the EMS Recognition Bonus **instead of** receiving M$ or USD.

**What the EMS Recognition Bonus is:**
- A permanent addition to your recognition hours in the EMS ledger
- Not M$, not USD, not a cash equivalent
- Not income and not a countable asset under the time banking framework — income and asset treatment is under benefits-counsel review; no MountainShares-specific Private Letter Ruling has been obtained
- Irrevocable per event — once you elect the EMS Recognition Bonus for a specific distribution event, that election cannot be reversed

**When to consider it:**
- You are receiving SSI, SNAP, Medicaid, or other means-tested benefits at the time of the distribution event
- Your benefits counselor advises that receiving M$ or USD at this time would create problems for your eligibility
- You want to continue accumulating recognition in the commons without creating a financial instrument

**When not to elect it:**
- You are no longer receiving benefits at the time of the distribution event
- You have consulted a benefits counselor and confirmed that receiving M$ or USD is safe in your current situation

**The EMS Recognition Bonus is your choice, per event.** It is not a default. It is not automatic. You elect it deliberately, after reviewing the 60-day advance notice and projections, ideally after consulting a benefits counselor.

## 7. Real-World Scenarios

**Scenario 1: You receive SSI and want to participate safely in Phase 1**
- Enable BSA protections in account settings
- Log unlimited community contribution hours — vaulted EMS does not count as income or assets under the time banking framework (benefits-counsel review applies)
- Load small amounts of M$ for local shopping (system enforces the BSA cap automatically)
- Your vaulted EMS hours accumulate in the recognition ledger — building Participation Weight for Phase 3 — without creating any financial exposure

**Scenario 2: Phase 2 activates and you have 50 vaulted EMS hours**
- BSA protections are active; you see a prominent benefits counselor referral before any conversion prompt
- You call WV Legal Aid (1-800-642-8279) and describe your situation
- Counselor advises: you can safely convert 10 EMS this month without exceeding your SNAP income threshold
- You convert 10 EMS, receive 100 M$ ($10 equivalent), spend it at local merchants
- You repeat the process gradually over several months — staying within safe limits at every step

**Scenario 3: You had SSI but just got a job and left the program**
- Disable BSA protections with one click
- Convert vaulted EMS at Phase 2 rates if you choose
- Load larger M$ amounts for shopping
- Six months later, you lose the job and reapply for Medicaid → re-enable BSA protections immediately

**Scenario 4: Phase 3 activates and you have 200 vaulted EMS hours**
- You receive the 60-day advance notice before the distribution event
- Your projected allocation is displayed: 200 EMS × Phase 3 FVSR rate
- You are currently on SSI; you call your benefits counselor
- Counselor advises: take the EMS Recognition Bonus this event; plan a staged cash conversion starting next year after a planned income change
- You elect the EMS Recognition Bonus for this event — the hours are permanently added to your recognition ledger, no income created
- Next year, when your situation changes, you work with your counselor on a staged conversion plan

## 11. Time Banking Legal Framework

Traditional community time banks have established that time credit exchanges are structured as non-taxable reciprocal service exchanges because:

- **Reciprocal service, not employment:** Community members help each other; no employer pays wages
- **Equal value per hour:** All service is valued at the same rate (1 hour = 1 credit), unlike market-rate bartering
- **Community purpose:** Time banks build social capital and community resilience
- **IRS precedent:** Time dollar exchanges have received IRS private letter rulings confirming non-taxable treatment of vaulted credits in certain fact patterns

MountainShares extends this framework digitally:
- EMS credits log community service hours with full auditability, geo-tagging, and timestamp
- M$ represents your own deposited funds — not earnings from time credits
- Phase 2 and Phase 3 conversion is **when countable income may arise** — and you control the timing and amount
- The EMS Recognition Bonus election provides a non-income path specifically for situations where conversion would create benefits exposure

**Legal review status:** H4H legal and benefits counsel are reviewing time bank classification alignment with IRS precedent and prepaid account treatment for benefits purposes; no MountainShares-specific Private Letter Ruling has been obtained. This notice is a draft pending benefits attorney review before Phase 1 activation. Nothing in this notice is legal advice or a legal determination. You should always rely on guidance from your benefits agencies and qualified advisors when making decisions about your benefits.
