# Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts – Plain Language Notice
*(Draft – For Discussion, Not Legal Advice)*

This notice explains how MountainShares is designed to work **for** people on public benefits — not against them. It is a plain‑language companion to the MountainShares Program Terms and Conditions. In any conflict, the Terms control.

---

## 1. Who This Is For

This notice is for anyone who receives or may receive means‑tested public benefits, including:

- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- WV WORKS / TANF
- SNAP (food stamps)
- Medicaid or WV CHIP
- HUD or other housing assistance
- State child‑care subsidies (for example, programs administered by MountainHeart or similar providers)

You may ask to have your account designated as **Benefits‑Sensitive**, and Harmony for Hope may also apply this designation based on information you or a partner agency voluntarily provide, where permitted by law.

**If you are unsure whether this applies to you, assume it does.** During Phase 0 (the invite‑only beta), every account is treated as Benefits‑Sensitive by default. That is a feature, not a restriction — it means the strongest protections are on for everyone from day one.

## 2. How MountainShares Is Designed to Protect You

MountainShares was built from the ground up with people on public benefits in mind. The system is not a bank, not a cryptocurrency, and not a cash program. It is a **community time bank** — a record of the hours you contribute to your community and a way to access community resources in return.

Here is what that means for your benefits:

### Your hours are a recognition record, not a financial account

When you log an hour of community contribution — caregiving, food access work, community education, mutual aid — the system records 1 EMS (Earned MountainShares). That record is:

- **Not cash.** It has no dollar value and cannot be converted to dollars.
- **Not a bank balance.** It cannot be withdrawn, cashed out, or sent to a bank account.
- **Not a countable resource under SSI rules as designed.** The SSA's resource test asks whether something can be converted to cash (POMS SI 01140.208). EMS cannot. It is a participation record, not a financial instrument.
- **Comparable to a time bank credit** — the same model used by community time banks nationwide, which SSA has generally not treated as countable resources.

### The Commons exchange is eligibility‑gated, not price‑denominated

In Phase 1, when The Commons opens, your EMS hours make you **eligible** to access community resources — food shares, skill exchanges, tool libraries, community programs. This is not buying and selling. There are no prices. There are no dollar amounts attached to any exchange.

The system works like this: a community partner offers a resource to members who have logged a certain number of contribution hours. If you meet the threshold, you can claim it. Your EMS hours are your **community standing**, not your purchasing power.

Ms. Allis — the AI steward system that runs MountainShares — is constitutionally prohibited from allowing any offer in The Commons to be denominated as a price in EMS. That is a hard architectural rule, not a policy preference. It cannot be changed by any single person, vote, or administrator.

### Phase progression and what changes at each stage

MountainShares operates in clearly defined phases with hard bars between them:

**Phase 0 — Vaulted EMS (current)**

1 hour of community contribution = 1 EMS, logged and vaulted. Hours can be recorded in 15‑minute intervals (0.25 EMS). There is no conversion, no trading, and no cash value of any kind during Phase 0. This is the strongest possible design for SSI non‑countability.

**Phase 1 — Commons Exchange (opens when The Commons launches)**

EMS hours unlock eligibility for community resource exchanges on the app only. There are no prices, no cash conversion, and no external exchange of any kind. The Commons operates as a closed‑loop community exchange — comparable to a time bank. Ms. Allis enforces the closed loop as a constitutional hard rule that cannot be overridden.

**Phase 2 — Community Currency (future, hard‑bar protected)**

EMS becomes an exchangeable community currency only when two hard bars are both met: the treasury reserve reaches 300% (verified, not estimated), and full regulatory compliance is documented and cleared. These bars cannot be waived by anyone, including Harmony for Hope's board. At the Phase 2 transition, BSA rules are reviewed and updated in full before any conversion capability is activated for Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts.

## 3. What Your Account Actually Restricts — and What It Never Restricts

We want to be direct about what a Benefits‑Sensitive designation does and does not do.

### Participation is never restricted

You can log hours and earn EMS without limit. You can access Commons resources you are eligible for. You can build community standing, governance rank, and participation history. Your BSA designation is private — visible only to Ms. Allis, never to other community members. Your ability to participate in The Commons is never throttled, capped, or restricted because of your BSA designation. Ever.

### What the system manages on your behalf

For SSI recipients specifically, the system applies a soft vault ceiling in Phase 1 — not on earning, but on accumulated balance — to reduce the risk that a large EMS vault could be treated as a countable resource by a caseworker unfamiliar with the system's design. If your vault approaches this ceiling, the app will notify you clearly, explain why, and tell you how to use your eligibility to access Commons resources.

This ceiling never stops you from logging hours. It never removes hours you have already earned. It only affects how a large accumulated balance is managed, and it is set conservatively to protect you, not to limit your community role.

For all other programs — SNAP, Medicaid, WV WORKS, housing assistance — the system does not apply additional restrictions beyond the baseline closed‑loop design, because those programs either have no resource limit (WV SNAP under Broad‑Based Categorical Eligibility) or use income‑based tests that the EMS system does not affect.

### What is never available for Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts

- No cash conversion at any phase until Phase 2, and only after BSA rules are reviewed
- No external exchange of EMS outside the app
- No posted prices or dollar‑denominated transactions inside the app

## 4. What We Can and Cannot Promise

Harmony for Hope **can:**

- Design MountainShares so that EMS has no cash conversion path and no determinable market value — the two factors that determine SSI resource countability under POMS
- Apply the closed‑loop, eligibility‑gated exchange design that gives you the strongest available argument that your EMS is not a countable resource
- Provide clear documentation of the system's design for you, your caseworker, or any agency that asks
- Connect you with benefits navigation resources if you need help understanding how MountainShares might interact with your specific benefits situation

Harmony for Hope **cannot:**

- Control how SSA, DHHR, HUD, or any agency ultimately treats MountainShares in your individual case
- Guarantee that MountainShares will have no impact on your benefits — no program can make that guarantee
- Take responsibility for reporting requirements that apply to you under benefits rules

**What you remain responsible for:** Reporting your participation to agencies when required by your program rules, and asking your caseworker, benefits planner, or WV Legal Aid (1‑800‑642‑8279) how MountainShares might affect your specific situation before building or spending large balances.

## 5. Your Privacy

Your BSA designation is stored in your private identity record — the immutable record maintained by Ms. Allis. It is never visible to other Commons members, never shared with employers, landlords, or community organizations, and accessible only by Ms. Allis for system enforcement purposes, or by court order, or by a formal Privacy Authorization Committee process under DAO governance.

No one in The Commons can see your BSA status. No one can see your EMS vault balance unless you choose to share it. Your participation in The Commons does not create a visible record of your benefits status to anyone in your community.

## 6. Our Commitment

MountainShares was designed for the people of West Virginia — including, and especially, people who are already navigating the difficult intersections of poverty, caregiving, disability, and community need. The Commons is not a program that asks you to risk your benefits in order to participate. It is a program that asks: *what does your community need, and what can you contribute?* — and recognizes that contribution in a way that is designed to protect everything you already have.

We will review this notice and the BSA protections at every phase transition and update them before any new capability is activated for Benefits‑Sensitive Accounts.

If you believe your account should be treated as Benefits‑Sensitive, or you think your designation is wrong, contact us through the support channels in The Commons.

If you need benefits navigation help, contact WV Legal Aid: **1‑800‑642‑8279**
