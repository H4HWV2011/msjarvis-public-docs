## 1. Who This Is For

This notice is for anyone who receives or may receive means‑tested public benefits, including:

- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- WV WORKS / TANF
- SNAP (food stamps)
- Medicaid or WV CHIP
- HUD or other housing assistance

You may ask to have your account designated as **Benefits‑Sensitive**, and you can remove this designation at any time with one click in your account settings.

**If you are unsure whether this applies to you, you can turn on BSA protections as a safety measure.** You control this setting — no verification required, no questions asked, and you can change it whenever your situation changes.

## 2. How MountainShares Is Designed to Protect You

MountainShares was built from the ground up with people on public benefits in mind. The system operates as a **community time bank** — a record of the hours you contribute to your community and a prepaid account system for accessing local goods and services.

Here is what that means for your benefits:

### Your time credits are recognition, not income

When you log an hour of community contribution — caregiving, food access work, community education, mutual aid, data stewardship — the system records 1 EMS (Earned MountainShares time credit). That record is:

- **Not income when you earn it.** Traditional time banks have established legal precedent that time credit exchanges are non-taxable because they represent reciprocal community service ("one hour for one hour"), not employment or bartering for market value.
- **Not a bank balance.** Time credits are vaulted recognition stored in the MountainShares time bank ledger, not money in an account.
- **Not convertible to cash in Phase 1.** EMS time credits remain vaulted (visible but not spendable) until Phase 2, when you can choose whether and how much to convert.
- **Comparable to traditional time bank credits** — the same model used by community time banks nationwide, which the IRS has ruled are non-taxable reciprocal exchanges.

### Your prepaid account is stored value you deposit, not earnings

PMS (Personal MountainShares) is a **prepaid stored-value account** — like a prepaid debit card balance:

- You load it by depositing your own money (via Stripe Payment Gateway)
- You spend it in The Commons for goods and services from local merchants
- You can cash it out via ATM withdrawal ($2.50 fee, 500 PMS daily limit)
- **It's not a bank account or savings account** — it's a prepaid balance you control

**Benefits-Sensitive Account protections cap how much you can load into your prepaid account each month** to help you stay under asset limits for programs like SSI ($2,000 individual limit).

### Phase progression and what changes at each stage

MountainShares operates in clearly defined phases with automatic treasury-backed protections:

**Phase 1 — Time Credits Vaulted (current)**

- 1 hour of community contribution = 1 EMS time credit, logged and vaulted
- Time credits are **non-taxable reciprocal community service exchanges** per IRS time banking precedent
- You can load PMS prepaid balances by depositing USD (capped at 500 PMS/month for Tier 1, 1,000 PMS/month for Tier 2)
- **BSA protection:** If you enable BSA, your prepaid balance is capped at a conservative threshold (DAO-set, defaults to protect SSI $2,000 limit)
- **You control BSA status:** One-click toggle in account settings — enable when you need protection, disable when your situation changes

**Phase 2 — User-Choice EMS Conversion (unlocks when treasury reserve ratio ≥ 1.25 for 60+ days)**

- **You choose** whether to convert vaulted EMS time credits to prepaid value (PMS)
- Conversion rate: 1 EMS = $10 USD equivalent (10× appreciation)
- **This is when countable income may arise** — converting time credits to cash creates income in the month you convert
- You can convert all, some, or none of your vaulted EMS — totally your choice
- **BSA users see benefits counselor referral prominently** before conversion and can:
  - Convert a small amount and test impact on benefits
  - Consult a benefits counselor to calculate safe amounts
  - Disable BSA protections if no longer receiving benefits
  - Continue vaulting for Phase 3 (34.7× appreciation)

**Phase 3 — Higher Appreciation (unlocks when treasury reserve ratio ≥ 1.50 for 60+ days)**

- Conversion rate jumps to 1 EMS = $34.70 USD equivalent (34.7× appreciation)
- Same user-choice framework: convert all, some, or none
- **You can re-enable BSA protections** if your situation changes (job loss, medical crisis, return to benefits)

## 3. Managing Your BSA Status — You're in Control

### One-Click Toggle

**In your account settings:**
- Current BSA status: **Enabled** or **Disabled**
- One button to change it
- No verification, no paperwork, no waiting

**When you enable BSA protections:**
- Prepaid balance (PMS) is capped at a conservative threshold (see current limit in Parameter Tables)
- Monthly prepaid loading is throttled if you approach the cap
- Phase 2/3 conversion prompts include benefits counselor referrals
- **Time credits (EMS) are never capped** — you can log unlimited hours; they just stay vaulted until you choose to convert

**When you disable BSA protections:**
- Prepaid balance caps removed (subject to standard tier caps)
- Phase 2/3 conversion interface opens fully
- **You are responsible** for managing benefits impact
- You can re-enable anytime if circumstances change

### Real-World Scenarios

**Scenario 1: You're on SSI and want to participate safely**
- Enable BSA protections
- Log unlimited time credits (EMS) — these don't count as assets while vaulted
- Load small amounts of PMS prepaid value for local shopping (stay under cap)
- When Phase 2 unlocks, consult a benefits counselor before converting any time credits

**Scenario 2: You just got a job and left SNAP**
- Disable BSA protections with one click
- Convert vaulted EMS time credits if you want (Phase 2+)
- Load larger PMS amounts for shopping
- Six months later, you lose the job and reapply for Medicaid → re-enable BSA protections

**Scenario 3: Phase 3 unlocks and you have 200 vaulted EMS hours**
- That's $6,940 potential value (200 × $34.70)
- You enable BSA, consult a benefits counselor
- Counselor advises staged conversion: 50 EMS now, 50 EMS in 6 months, etc.
- You execute the plan over 2 years to stay under SSI asset limits
- BSA protections stay enabled — system enforces caps automatically

## 7. Time Banking Legal Framework

**Why time credits are designed to be non-taxable:**

Traditional time banks have established that time credit exchanges are non-taxable because:

1. **Reciprocal service, not employment:** Community members help each other; no employer pays wages
2. **Equal value per hour:** All service is valued the same (1 hour = 1 credit), unlike market-wage bartering
3. **Community purpose:** Time banks build social capital and resilience, not taxable income
4. **IRS precedent:** Time dollar exchanges have received IRS private letter rulings confirming tax-exempt status

**MountainShares extends this digitally:**
- Time credits (EMS) log community service hours with full auditability
- Prepaid value (PMS) represents your own deposited funds, not earnings from time credits
- Phase 2+ conversion is **when countable income arises** — and you control timing and amount

**Legal review before Phase 1 launch:** H4H legal counsel confirms time bank classification aligns with IRS precedent and prepaid accounts are not "bank accounts" for benefits purposes.
