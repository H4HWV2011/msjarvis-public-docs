# Risks and Limits – Plain Language Overview
*(Draft – For Discussion, Not Legal Advice)*

This document summarizes key risks and limits for people and institutions participating in The Commons and the MountainShares Program. It is a plain‑language companion to the Commons Terms of Use, MountainShares Rewards Program Terms and Conditions, and related agreements. In any conflict, those higher‑level documents control.

---

## 1. Benefits, Taxes, and Financial Risk

### 1.1 Possible Impact on Benefits

- Participation in the MountainShares Program may affect eligibility for **means‑tested benefits** (for example, SNAP, TANF, housing assistance, disability benefits, or similar programs).  
- Neither Harmony for Hope (H4H), the MountainShares DAO, Kidd's Technical Services (KTS), nor any node partner can guarantee that MountainShares or related activity will be ignored by benefits agencies.  
- Participants are responsible for:
  - understanding how income, assets, or in‑kind support may affect their own benefits; and  
  - seeking advice from caseworkers, legal aid, or other qualified advisors as needed.

### 1.2 Tax Considerations

- MountainShares units, EMS recognition, and related activities may have **tax implications**, depending on jurisdiction and specific use.  
- The project does not provide personalized tax advice.  
- Participants and partner organizations are responsible for their own tax treatment and reporting.

### 1.3 No Guaranteed Financial Value

- MountainShares are not bank deposits, not legal tender, and not guaranteed to have cash value inside or outside the system.  
- There is no promise that anyone will be able to convert MountainShares to cash at any particular rate, or at all.  
- Participation should be understood as commons work and mutual support, not as a guaranteed financial investment or savings program.

## 2. EMS, Rewards, and Internal "Buying Power"

### 2.1 EMS Are Not Wages or Guaranteed

- **Earned MountainShares (EMS)** represent recorded hours or units of commons work.  
- EMS are **not wages, salaries, or employment benefits**, and do not create an employment relationship with H4H, the DAO, KTS, or any partner.  
- There is no guarantee that:
  - a given activity will earn EMS;  
  - EMS will accrue at a particular rate; or  
  - EMS balances will remain unchanged over time (for example, in the event of program rule changes, caps, or safety measures).

> **Founder's EMS:** Pre-launch founder EMS recorded prior to Phase 0 activation are governed by the Founder's EMS Vesting Schedule and DAO Governance Charter, which establish separate conversion terms and protections. The provisions of this section apply to general participant EMS and do not alter or diminish the rights established under the Founder's EMS Vesting Schedule.

### 2.2 Not Cash by Default

- EMS and MountainShares are internal program units.  
- Any ability to use, redeem, or convert them (for example, for goods, services, or discounts) is:
  - defined by Program Rules;  
  - subject to change; and  
  - limited to participating merchants, partners, or program mechanisms.  
- There is no promise that EMS or MountainShares can be converted to cash or used everywhere.

### 2.3 Phases and Internal Buying Power

- References to "phases," "internal buying power," or similar concepts describe **design intentions**, not fixed legal or financial guarantees.  
- Program phases, internal pricing schedules, and conversion curves may:
  - change over time;  
  - be delayed;  
  - be scaled back; or  
  - never occur in the exact form originally described.  
- Governance, safety mechanisms, and legal constraints may require adjustments that affect how EMS and MountainShares behave.

## 3. Governance, Safety, and Limits of Control

### 3.1 Governance Rights Are Limited

- Participation in governance (for example, voting, proposals, feedback) is governed by Program Rules.  
- There is no guarantee that any individual or organization will:
  - have a particular level of influence;  
  - see their preferred proposals adopted; or  
  - prevent changes they disagree with.  
- Safety triggers and legal obligations may override some governance preferences.

### 3.2 Safety Mechanisms May Restrict Use

- To protect users, reserves, and benefits‑sensitive participants, the system may enforce:
  - caps on holdings or conversions;  
  - temporary freezes or slowdowns;  
  - phase‑based or risk‑based limits on certain features.  
- These mechanisms can affect how and when participants can use MountainShares, even if they have valid balances.

## 4. Node Partners (Universities and Agencies)

### 4.1 Not Responsible for Token Value or Returns

- **University Commons Node Partners** and **Government Commons Node Partners**:
  - host and analyze specific datasets;  
  - support research, evaluation, and public‑interest uses.  
- They do **not**:
  - issue MountainShares;  
  - guarantee any financial return or cash conversion;  
  - act as brokers, dealers, or investment advisors.  
- Node partners are not responsible for changes in the value, utility, or legal treatment of MountainShares.

### 4.2 Scoped Data and Responsibilities

- Node partners only receive and process the data defined in their data‑sharing agreements.  
- They are responsible for:
  - their own infrastructure, security, and compliance;  
  - research ethics and data‑use approvals under their policies.  
- They are not responsible for:
  - MountainShares economic design;  
  - treasury operations;  
  - program‑level legal and regulatory filings.

## 5. Open‑Source Components and Service Limits

### 5.1 Open‑Source vs. Service Commitments

- Parts of the system (including some Ms. Allis / GBIM components) may be released under open‑source licenses.  
- Open‑source code availability does **not** guarantee:
  - ongoing hosted service,  
  - support, or  
  - operational uptime.  
- Any commitments to operate nodes, provide support, or maintain services are defined in separate agreements (for example, MOUs, contracts, or program documentation) and may be limited in time or scope.

### 5.2 No Obligation to Continue Services Indefinitely

- H4H, KTS, and other operators reserve the right to:
  - change, suspend, or discontinue services;  
  - modify or replace software components;  
  - adjust or terminate pilot arrangements and no‑fee periods.  
- Material changes will be communicated as described in the relevant Terms and agreements, but there is no obligation to operate any particular service forever.

## 6. Personal and Organizational Responsibility

- Individuals and organizations remain responsible for:
  - complying with applicable laws, regulations, and institutional policies;  
  - assessing how participation aligns with their own risk tolerance and obligations;  
  - seeking independent legal, tax, or benefits advice where needed.  
- Participation in The Commons, the MountainShares Program, Champion roles, or node partnerships is voluntary and at each participant's own risk within the limits of Applicable Law and the governing documents.
