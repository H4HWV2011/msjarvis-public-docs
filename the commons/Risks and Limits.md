> **STRUCTURAL RISK STATEMENT — READ THIS FIRST:**
> MountainShares EMS recognition credits are non-appreciating in the securities-law sense at all times. No EMS balance, M$ balance, or Commons account is an equity instrument, a security, or a guaranteed-appreciation asset. Phase-based EMS buying-power expansion rates are **conditional and revocable** — not guaranteed. No appreciating or equity instrument reaches any participant through H4H, community EMS, or any Commons account. This is not a hedge, a qualification, or a limitation of a promise — it is the governing design.

## 1. Benefits, Taxes, and Financial Risk

### 1.1 Possible Impact on Public Benefits

Participation in MountainShares may affect eligibility for means-tested public benefits, including but not limited to:

- Supplemental Security Income (SSI)
- WV WORKS / TANF
- SNAP (food assistance)
- Medicaid or WV CHIP
- HUD or other federal and state housing assistance
- State child-care subsidies

Neither H4H, the MountainShares DAO, KTS, nor any node partner can guarantee that MountainShares activity will be disregarded by any benefits agency. Each agency applies its own rules to its own programs, and those rules are outside H4H's control.

**How the system is designed to reduce this risk:**
The Benefits-Sensitive Account (BSA) architecture — described fully in [BSA — Benefits-Sensitive Accounts](BSA-Benefits%E2%80%91Sensitive%20Accounts%20.md) — is the primary risk-mitigation tool for benefits-sensitive participants. BSA protections include monthly M$ loading caps, suppression of forced conversions, prominent benefits counselor referrals before any Phase 2 or Phase 3 conversion decision, 60-day advance notice before Phase 3 distribution events, and the EMS Recognition Bonus election for Phase 3. BSA status is participant-controlled, one-click, and free. It does not restrict participation in any Commons activity.

**What BSA does not do:** BSA protections reduce risk but cannot eliminate it. Each participant's individual benefits situation is governed by their benefits agency and their caseworker — not by H4H. Participants receiving means-tested benefits are strongly encouraged to consult a qualified benefits counselor before making any Phase 2 or Phase 3 conversion election.

**Free benefits counseling — West Virginia:**
- WV Legal Aid: 1-800-642-8279
- Disability Rights of West Virginia
- WV PROMISE (Pathways to Independence)

Participants are responsible for understanding how income, assets, or in-kind support may affect their own benefits, and for seeking qualified advice as needed.

### 1.2 Tax Considerations

EMS recognition credits, M$ balances, and related activities may have tax implications depending on jurisdiction and the specific nature of each participant's activity. The time banking legal framework — under which vaulted EMS credits are treated as non-taxable reciprocal community service exchanges while unconverted — is the basis of the system's design, but this framework has not been confirmed by a private letter ruling specific to MountainShares.

**Income may arise at conversion, not at earning.** Converting EMS to M$ or to USD creates income in the month of conversion. Vaulted, unconverted EMS does not constitute income or a countable asset under the time banking framework — but individual tax treatment depends on individual circumstances.

The MountainShares Program does not provide personalized tax advice. Participants and partner organizations are responsible for their own tax treatment and reporting.

### 1.3 No Guaranteed Financial Value

MountainShares units, EMS recognition credits, and M$ balances are not bank deposits, not legal tender, and not guaranteed to have any specific cash value inside or outside the system.

**The phase-based EMS buying-power schedule is conditional, not guaranteed:**

| Phase | EMS Buying-Power Rate | What Makes It Conditional |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1** | 1 EMS = $1.00 equivalent | Default — active now |
| **Phase 2** | 1 EMS = $10.00 equivalent | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio must be ≥ 300% for 2+ consecutive quarters; all transition costs paid; automatic reversion if ratio falls below 300% |
| **Phase 3** | 1 EMS = FVSR in effect on Phase 3 activation date (current 2026 rate: $36.14) | Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio must be ≥ 500% for 2+ consecutive quarters; all prior-phase costs paid; automatic reversion if ratio falls below 500% |

There is no promise that any participant will be able to realize Phase 2 or Phase 3 rates. Those rates activate when the treasury earns them through community circulation — and they revert automatically if the treasury falls below the required floor. Participation should be understood as commons work and mutual support, not as a guaranteed financial investment or savings program.

## 2. EMS, Buying Power, and Vesting — Updated Structure

### 2.1 EMS Are Recognition Credits, Not Wages

Earned MountainShares (EMS) represent verified hours of community contribution recorded in the recognition ledger. They are:

- Not wages, salaries, or employment benefits
- Not a promise of financial return
- Not an equity instrument or a security
- Not convertible to cash at any rate until the participant elects conversion and the applicable phase threshold is active

EMS do not create an employment relationship with H4H, the DAO, KTS, or any partner.

There is no guarantee that a given activity will earn EMS, that EMS will accrue at a particular rate, or that EMS balances will remain unchanged over time in the event of program rule changes, caps, or safety measures.

### 2.2 The Vesting Schedule — Corrected Framework

Previous versions of this document referenced a "Founder's EMS Vesting Schedule" as a distinct instrument governing pre-launch founder EMS. That framing has been superseded by the current structural architecture.

**The correct current structure is:**

- Community EMS — earned by all participants through verified community contribution — is governed by the Program Rules, the applicable Phase Specification, and this document. Community EMS is non-appreciating at all times. No vesting schedule creates separate conversion rights for community EMS that deviate from the phase-threshold-gated framework described throughout this document.
- Founder-side rights — if any — exist exclusively on the KTS side of the three-box structure (H4H / Commons / KTS), under separate counsel-reviewed documentation that is not part of the community EMS system. Those rights do not activate before KTS is formed as a WV LLC with an EIN. No founder-side instrument reaches participants through H4H or community EMS.

Any document, instrument, or communication that references a "Founder's EMS Vesting Schedule" as creating separate conversion rights within the community EMS system is superseded by this structural clarification. If you have questions about how this affects a specific document you have received, contact H4H at 704 Main Street, Mount Hope, WV 25880.

### 2.3 Phases Are Design Intentions, Not Fixed Legal Guarantees

References to phases, internal buying-power schedules, and conversion rates describe the current design of the system — not fixed legal or financial commitments. Program phases, internal pricing schedules, and conversion rates may:

- Change over time through governance
- Be delayed by treasury conditions
- Be scaled back if reserve thresholds are not achieved or maintained
- Revert automatically if the applicable Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below the required floor
- Never occur in the exact form originally described if legal, regulatory, or operational conditions require adjustment

The automatic reversion mechanism is the most important protection in this list. It is not a risk — it is the system protecting participants from a scenario in which higher buying-power rates are sustained longer than the treasury can safely support them.

### 2.4 Phase Reversion Is Not a Failure

If the Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below the applicable threshold, the Phase Management Controller automatically reverts to prior-phase buying-power parameters. This is:

- Non-overridable by any DAO vote or H4H Board decision
- Automatic — no delay, no grace period
- Communicated to all participants in plain language within 24 hours
- Logged to the durable audit trail

A reversion event does not reduce EMS balances, eliminate vaulted credits, or remove governance standing. It means the higher buying-power rate is inactive until the treasury recovers to the required threshold. Vaulted EMS remains in the recognition ledger, accumulating Participation Weight, until the participant elects to convert under whatever phase rules apply at the time of election.

## 3. Governance, Safety, and Hard Constraints

### 3.1 Governance Rights Are Bounded

Participation in governance — voting, proposals, Champion reporting, feedback — is governed by the Program Rules and the DAO Governance Charter. There is no guarantee that any individual or organization will have a particular level of influence, see their preferred proposals adopted, or prevent changes they disagree with.

**Hard constraints that governance cannot override:**
- Reserve-based hard triggers — enforced automatically by the Phase Management Controller; non-overridable at any vote threshold
- Phase reversion — automatic when reserve metric falls below applicable floor; cannot be delayed by governance
- BSA core protections — cannot be reduced by any DAO vote
- The three-box structural separation between H4H, MountainShares Commons, and KTS — cannot be modified through governance to create a founder equity path through H4H or community EMS
- Legal and regulatory compliance obligations — take precedence over any governance decision

These constraints are not limitations on the commons — they are the commons. They exist to protect participants, including the participants who might vote to remove them.

### 3.2 Safety Mechanisms May Restrict Use

To protect participants, reserves, and benefits-sensitive accounts, the system enforces automatic constraints that may limit how and when participants can use MountainShares:

- Per-user monthly earning caps (varies by tier and phase)
- BSA monthly loading caps (for BSA-flagged accounts)
- Reserve-band parameter tightening — when the Operational Reserve Ratio falls into a lower band, caps tighten and promo features may pause
- Phase reversion — EMS buying-power rate drops automatically if the Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio falls below threshold
- Emergency freezes or account restrictions in response to fraud, abuse, or Level 3–4 incidents

These mechanisms can affect how and when participants can use MountainShares even when they have valid balances. They are disclosed in advance through the applicable Phase Specification and Program Rules – Parameter Tables.

### 3.3 The Two Reserve Metrics

Risks related to system functionality depend on two distinct metrics, both calculated daily by Ms. Allis:

~~~
Operational Reserve Ratio = Treasury USDC Reserve / Total Outstanding Spendable PMS (M$)
~~~

~~~
Treasury Reserve Solvency Ratio = Treasury Reserve / (Total Outstanding EMS + Total Outstanding M$)
~~~

Participants can monitor both metrics on the public DAO dashboard. No personal data is displayed on the dashboard — only aggregate system metrics and band status.

## 6. The Three-Box Structure — Risk to Participants

The three-box entity structure (H4H / MountainShares Commons / KTS) is the primary legal and financial protection separating charitable assets, community EMS, and founder-side commercial rights. Participants benefit from this structure because it means:

- H4H's 501(c)(3) charitable assets cannot be redirected to founder-side commercial purposes
- Community EMS cannot function as a founder equity instrument
- The Phase 3 surplus pool belongs to the community — not to founders, not to H4H operating reserves, not to external investors
- Founder-side rights exist only on the KTS side, under separate documentation, and do not activate before KTS LLC formation

**The risk to participants if this structure is breached:** A structural boundary breach — any mechanism that creates founder equity through H4H or community EMS — is classified as a Level 4 Critical incident under the [Audit and Risk Management Plan](../mountainshares-dao/Audit%20and%20Risk%20Management%20Plan.md). It triggers immediate notification to H4H Board, legal counsel, and the DAO community, and requires remediation before any program operations continue. Participants who believe a structural boundary breach has occurred may report it directly to H4H at 704 Main Street, Mount Hope, WV 25880, or through the Champion reporting channel.
