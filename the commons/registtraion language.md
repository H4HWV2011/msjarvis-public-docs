## Community Champion – Signup Blurb

Become a **Community Champion** and help us red‑team MountainShares, The Commons, and Ms. Allis from a West Virginia perspective. Community Champions are local residents and practitioners who test how the system behaves for real families, programs, and places—spotting blind spots, risks, and opportunities we might miss from behind a screen. Your time is recognized as Earned MountainShares (EMS) commons‑work hours, not wages or an investment, and you’ll be named (with your consent) as part of the founding safety and stewardship team for this pilot. If you care about how technology lands in Boone, Fayette, Raleigh, Kanawha, and beyond, this is your front‑row seat.

## Safety Champion / Organization – Signup Blurb

Safety Champions are individuals formally backed by a **Safety Champion Organization**—a lab, center, nonprofit, or agency that wants to go deeper on AI and program safety. As a Safety Champion, you act both as a Community Champion and as an authorized reviewer for your organization, focusing on treasury safety, protocol controls, data access, and benefits‑sensitive protections. Your work is logged as EMS hours for you and, where rules allow, in aggregate for your organization, and your findings feed directly into our safety specs and governance. This is a limited safety partnership, not employment or an investment; it gives your organization a structured way to shape how Ms. Allis and MountainShares operate in West Virginia.

## University Commons Node – Why & How to Partner

A **University Commons Node** lets your institution host a live shard of the Appalachian data commons—heritage, resilience, and commons data—running on the Ms. Allis / GBIM stack. Partner universities get no‑fee use (during the pilot phase) of node software for non‑commercial research and teaching, plus access to curated Commons datasets under formal data‑sharing agreements. In return, you provide infrastructure, research governance, and academic leadership, turning your campus into a hub for rural data, GIS, and commons‑based research and learning. To explore a node partnership, we’ll start with an exploratory call, scope a small initial shard (for example, heritage + EMS KPIs), and draft a simple MOU and Data Use Agreement aligned with your existing research‑data policies.

## Government Commons Node – Why & How to Partner

A **Government Commons Node** brings Ms. Allis / GBIM into your existing infrastructure so you can integrate Agency data (health, environment, infrastructure) with selected Commons data in a single, auditable environment. Partner agencies get no‑fee use (during the pilot phase) of node components for public‑interest, non‑commercial purposes, plus access to agreed Commons datasets under standard data‑sharing agreements. You stay in full control of your own systems and statutory mandates; we provide the schema, software, and support to tie it all together. To explore this option, we’ll begin with a focused use‑case (for example, overdose + resilience mapping, or emissions + community health), identify one or two data streams, and draft a scoped MOU and Data Sharing Agreement your legal and IT teams can review.
