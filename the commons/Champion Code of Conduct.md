# Champion Code of Conduct
*(Draft – Supplements Community Guidelines and Champion Agreements)*

This Code of Conduct sets extra expectations for **Community Champions** and **Safety Champions** (“Champions”) in addition to:

- The Commons **Community Guidelines and Moderation Policy**  
- The **Community Champion / Red Team Participation Agreement**  
- The **Safety Champion Participation Agreement** (for Safety Champions and their organizations)  

If there is any conflict, the higher‑level Terms, Program Rules, and Champion Agreements control.

---

## 1. Higher Standard of Conduct

Champions are held to a higher standard than regular users because they:

- Have early or deeper access to systems, data, and designs.  
- Are explicitly trusted to help identify and reduce risks for others.  

Champions are expected to model:

- Respectful, good‑faith communication, even in conflict or disagreement.  
- Careful handling of sensitive information, including vulnerabilities and user data.  
- Honest reporting of findings and clear separation between testing and exploitation.

---

## 2. Behavior and Communication

In addition to the Community Guidelines, Champions agree to:

- Avoid using their role to intimidate, harass, or pressure other participants or staff.  
- Be transparent about when they are speaking as Champions (for example, in testing or feedback contexts) versus as regular users.  
- Disclose any conflicts of interest that might reasonably affect their testing or recommendations.  

Champions must not:

- Misrepresent their authority, expertise, or relationship to Harmony for Hope, MountainShares, or Ms. Jarvis.  
- Use their role to gain unauthorized access to systems, data, or decision‑making processes.

---

## 3. Testing, Reporting, and Confidentiality

Champions agree to:

- Keep safety testing within the permitted scope defined in their Champion Agreement.  
- Use the designated channels to report vulnerabilities, design issues, and risks.  
- Provide enough detail to reproduce an issue without unnecessarily exposing user identities or sensitive content.  

Champions must not:

- Publicly disclose unpatched vulnerabilities or sensitive implementation details without explicit agreement.  
- Use any vulnerability to steal assets, damage systems, or harm users, merchants, or partners.  
- Share access credentials, private keys, or internal tools with anyone not authorized to use them.

---

## 4. Use of EMS, Reputation, and Influence

Champions understand that:

- EMS hours are recognition for commons work, not wages or guaranteed compensation.  
- Any governance voice they have comes from Program Rules (for example, one‑UEI‑one‑vote), not special Champion status.  

Champions must not:

- Trade on their Champion status to promise outcomes (for example, “I can get your proposal passed”) or sell influence.  
- Use EMS, governance tools, or insider knowledge to manipulate the system for personal or organizational gain.

---

## 5. Compliance and Revocation of Champion Status

Champion roles depend on ongoing trust.

- Champions remain fully subject to the Community Guidelines, Commons Terms of Use, MountainShares Terms, and their Champion Agreements.  
- Violations of this Code, the Community Guidelines, or applicable law may result in:
  - warnings or corrective guidance;  
  - temporary limits on Champion activities;  
  - suspension or permanent revocation of Champion status;  
  - account‑level moderation actions (including suspension) where appropriate.  

Revocation of Champion status:

- Does not erase EMS hours already logged.  
- Does not remove ongoing confidentiality obligations related to unpatched vulnerabilities or sensitive information.  

---

## 6. Acknowledgement

By participating as a Community Champion or Safety Champion, you acknowledge that:

- You have read and understood this Code of Conduct.  
- You agree to abide by these expectations in addition to all other applicable Terms, Program Rules, and Champion Agreements.  
- You understand that Champion status is a privilege grounded in trust and may be revoked if you do not uphold these standards.
