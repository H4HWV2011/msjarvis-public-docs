# Participation Map — Roles and Documents
*(Draft for Discussion – Not Legal Advice)*

This overview maps the main participation roles in The Commons and MountainShares ecosystem and shows which documents apply to each role, what participants do, what they receive, and what they are *not*.

---

## 1. Commons User

### Applicable Documents

- [The Commons – Terms of Use](The%20Commons%20%E2%80%93%20Terms%20of%20Use.md)
- [MountainShares Terms and Conditions](../mountainshares-dao/Terms%20and%20Conditions.md) (if they hold or earn MountainShares)
- [Community Guidelines and Moderation Policy](Community%20Guidelines%20and%20Moderation%20Policy.md)
- [The Commons Gamification: The Daily Life Game](The%20Commons%20Gamification%3A%20The%20Daily%20Life%20Game.md)
- [MountainShares Gamification: The Contribution Game](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20Gamification%3A%20The%20Contribution%20Game.md)

### What They Do

- Use The Commons to:
  - post, share, and react to content;
  - participate in quests, events, and local projects;
  - interact with community members, merchants, and organizations.
- Earn **Earned MountainShares (EMS)** hours through defined Program activities, quests, and value interactions, logged permanently in the recognition ledger.
- Build rank (Newcomer → Bronze → Silver → Gold → Champion) through accumulated EMS hours — earning governance standing and role eligibility over time.
- Participate in governance features (for example, voting or feedback mechanisms) once Bronze rank is reached (10 EMS hours + 30 days account age).

### What They Get

- Access to The Commons social–economic–digital platform.
- Ability to post content and participate in community spaces within the Community Guidelines.
- Potential to earn EMS and, where permitted, spendable M$ within Program limits and reserve ratio bands.
- Rank progression, badges, and recognition in the MountainShares contribution game.
- Access to governance and feedback tools as defined by Program Rules.

### What They Are Not

- Not employees or agents of Harmony for Hope or the MountainShares DAO by virtue of using the Platform.
- Not investors solely by holding or earning MountainShares units.
- Not entitled to any specific amount of EMS, MountainShares, rewards, or governance influence beyond what is defined in the Terms and Program Rules.

## 2. Phase 0 Participant (Invite-Only Beta)

### Applicable Documents

- All documents applicable to Commons Users (above)
- [MountainShares Phase 0: Invite-Only Beta Specification](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20Phase%200%3A%20Invite-Only%20Beta%20Specification.md)
- [MountainShares Gamification: The Contribution Game](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20Gamification%3A%20The%20Contribution%20Game.md) — Section 4 (Founding Badges)
- [Community Champion / Red Team Participation Agreement](../mountainshares-dao/Safety%20Champion%20Program%20.md) (if designated as Community Champion)

### What They Do

- Participate in the invite-only beta period before public launch.
- Test wallets, UEIs, EMS logging, and The Commons app flows.
- May serve as red-team or community anchor participants under documented scope.
- Log EMS hours in the recognition ledger for qualifying Phase 0 activities.

### What They Get

- A MountainShares wallet and UEI.
- EMS hours permanently logged — carry forward into Phase 1 and count toward voting eligibility, rank, and role progression.
- Founding badges (Commons Founder, Red-Team Pioneer, Charter Witness, etc.) — permanently visible on UEI profile, never available after Phase 1 opens.
- Groundbreaker rank if eligibility criteria are met during Phase 0 — the only founding rank in the system.
- Named entry (by UEI handle, with consent) in the Phase 0 Pre-Launch Audit Report.

### What They Are Not

- Not public participants — Phase 0 is not a public offering.
- Not guaranteed any specific amount of EMS or spendable M$ (spendable M$ is disabled by default during Phase 0).
- Not exempt from hard safety triggers, Terms, or Champion Code of Conduct.

## 3. Community Champion (Red Team Participant)

### Applicable Documents

- All documents applicable to Commons Users and Phase 0 Participants (above)
- [Community Champion / Red Team Participation Agreement](../mountainshares-dao/Safety%20Champion%20Program%20.md)
- [Champion Code of Conduct](Champion%20Code%20of%20Conduct.md)
- [MountainShares Phase 0: Invite-Only Beta Specification](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20Phase%200%3A%20Invite-Only%20Beta%20Specification.md) — Section 5 (Red-Team Scope)
- [MountainShares Gamification: The Contribution Game](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20Gamification%3A%20The%20Contribution%20Game.md) — Section 4 (Founding Badges)

### What They Do

- Serve as **red team participants** during defined phases (for example, Phase 0 Invite-Only Beta).
- Test and review:
  - how Ms. Jarvis, MountainShares, and The Commons behave for real users and places;
  - potential vulnerabilities, design weaknesses, and safety risks;
  - gaps or biases in data, advice, or program behavior.
- Report issues through designated channels, with enough detail to support investigation and fixes.

### What They Get

- Recognition of their time and contributions as **EMS hours**, logged in the recognition ledger as commons work.
- Founding badges recognizing their role in building the system before public launch.
- The ability (where rules allow) to use EMS for governance and limited internal conversions, subject to Program Rules.
- Public or semi-public acknowledgment (where safe and consented) as Community Champions.

### What They Are Not

- Not employees, partners, or contractors of Harmony for Hope or the MountainShares DAO solely by being Community Champions.
- Not guaranteed any fixed amount of EMS, MountainShares, or financial benefit.
- Not permitted to exploit vulnerabilities for personal gain or to harm users, merchants, or the treasury.

## 4. Safety Champion (Institution-Linked Red Team Participant)

### Applicable Documents

- All documents applicable to Community Champions (above)
- [Safety Champion Participation Agreement](../mountainshares-dao/Safety%20Champion%20Program%20.md) (individual + Safety Champion Organization)
- Any institutional MOUs or agreements between Harmony for Hope, KTS, and the Safety Champion Organization

### What They Do

- Act as **Safety Champions** with a deeper, more technical remit, often tied to a lab, center, nonprofit, or agency.
- Focus on:
  - treasury and reserve safety;
  - protocol and administrative surfaces (smart contracts, upgrade paths, admin controls);
  - data access, privacy, and benefits-sensitive protections;
  - interactions between Ms. Jarvis and on-chain / platform components.
- Translate findings into structured safety recommendations and documentation, often on behalf of their organization.

### What They Get

- EMS recognition for safety-testing work, associated with their personal UEI and, when rules allow, aggregated to the organization's UEI.
- Safety Champion Founder badge (Phase 0) — permanent founding recognition.
- A defined role and relationship between their organization and Harmony for Hope for AI and program safety work.
- Opportunities for co-authored reports, case studies, or academic and institutional recognition, where appropriate.

### What They Are Not

- Not granted ownership of MountainShares, The Commons, Ms. Jarvis, or underlying infrastructure.
- Not guaranteed special veto rights or extra governance weight beyond what Program Rules provide.
- Not exempt from general Community Guidelines or from their own organization's policies and legal obligations.

## 5. Merchant

### Applicable Documents

- [MountainShares Terms and Conditions](../mountainshares-dao/Terms%20and%20Conditions.md) — Merchant provisions
- [The Commons – Terms of Use](The%20Commons%20%E2%80%93%20Terms%20of%20Use.md)
- [Program Rules – Parameter Tables](../mountainshares-dao/Program%20Rules%20%E2%80%93%20Parameter%20Tables.md) — Section 4 (Fees)
- [Community Guidelines and Moderation Policy](Community%20Guidelines%20and%20Moderation%20Policy.md)
- Merchant Agreement (separate onboarding document)

### What They Do

- Accept MountainShares (M$) as payment for goods and services at their place of business.
- List their business in The Commons merchant directory, making them discoverable to participants.
- Pay a merchant fee (1.80% of eligible M$ transactions, per Parameter Tables) on accepted M$ payments.
- Optionally participate in Commons quests and seasonal campaigns as a local commerce destination — driving foot traffic and M$ circulation.
- Serve as anchor points for The Commons Daily Life Game: merchant visits, Local First badges, and Merchant of the Month leaderboard recognition.

### What They Get

- Access to a local customer base actively incentivized to spend M$ at participating merchants.
- Listing and discovery in The Commons app merchant directory.
- Visibility in Commons leaderboards (Merchant of the Month) and seasonal campaigns.
- A documented, auditable record of M$ transactions for their own accounting.
- No-fee or reduced-fee promotional opportunities during reserve-band-gated promo campaigns.

### What They Are Not

- Not issuers, brokers, or financial intermediaries for MountainShares.
- Not required to redeem M$ for USD on demand — redemption and cash-out processes are governed by Program Rules and payment partner terms.
- Not entitled to governance voting rights solely by virtue of being a merchant (merchants who are also commons participants with a UEI follow the standard one-UEI-one-vote rule).
- Not partners or co-owners of Harmony for Hope, the MountainShares DAO, or any underlying assets.

## 6. University Commons Node Partner

### Applicable Documents

- [University Commons Node Partnership Overview](University%20Commons%20Node%20Partnership%20Overview.md)
- Node Operations MOU between H4H, KTS, and the University
- Data Sharing Agreement (DSA) / Data Use Agreement (DUA)
- [The Commons – Terms of Use](The%20Commons%20%E2%80%93%20Terms%20of%20Use.md) and MountainShares Terms (for platform interaction)
- Any internal University data-governance and research-ethics policies

### What They Do

- Operate a **Commons Node** under University IT and research-data policies.
- Host and manage:
  - selected Commons / MountainShares data shards (for example, heritage, EMS KPIs, resilience data);
  - Ms. Jarvis / GBIM components that ingest, index, and analyze those datasets.
- Use the node for:
  - research and teaching;
  - community-engaged projects;
  - evaluation, visualization, and geospatial analysis.
- Govern local access and use through University processes (IRB, data-use committees, etc.).

### What They Get

- No-fee use (during defined phases) of the Commons Node software stack for non-commercial research, teaching, and evaluation.
- Access to curated Commons datasets under the DSA/DUA.
- A role as a founding partner in the Appalachian data commons and a potential co-PI on grants and collaborative projects.
- Distinctive positioning as a hub for rural, geospatial, and commons-focused research and learning.

### What They Are Not

- Not issuers, brokers, or promoters of MountainShares.
- Not responsible for the financial performance or legal classification of MountainShares.
- Not obligated to share their own non-Commons datasets back into The Commons unless explicitly agreed in the DSA/DUA.

## 7. Government Commons Node Partner

### Applicable Documents

- [Government Commons Node & Ms. Jarvis Partnership Overview](Government%20Commons%20Node%20%26%20Ms.%20Jarvis%20Partnership%20Overview.md)
- Node Operations MOU or inter-agency agreement between H4H, KTS, and the Agency
- Data Sharing / Data Use Agreements (Agency ↔ H4H/KTS)
- [The Commons – Terms of Use](The%20Commons%20%E2%80%93%20Terms%20of%20Use.md) and MountainShares Terms (for platform interaction)
- Applicable statutes, regulations, and Agency data-governance policies

### What They Do

- Operate a **Government Commons Node** inside Agency infrastructure.
- Integrate and analyze:
  - Agency data streams (for example, health, environmental, infrastructure program data) where law and policy allow;
  - selected Commons data relevant to the Agency's mission (heritage, EMS, resilience indicators).
- Use Ms. Jarvis / GBIM components to:
  - improve situational awareness and cross-program analytics;
  - support planning, evaluation, and reporting;
  - maintain auditable, historic records of key indicators.

### What They Get

- No-fee use (during defined phases) of Government Node software components for public-interest, non-commercial purposes.
- Access to agreed Commons datasets under formal data-sharing agreements.
- A structured mechanism to collaborate with H4H and KTS on rural data, AI, and resilience work.
- Stronger analytic and historical infrastructure without relinquishing control of Agency data or mandates.

### What They Are Not

- Not co-issuers or guarantors of MountainShares or any related economic instruments.
- Not relieved of statutory obligations or regulatory requirements by participating.
- Not required to open all internal systems or datasets to The Commons; integration is scoped and governed by specific agreements.

## 8. Ms. Jarvis (AI Wallet Holder)

### Applicable Documents

- [MountainShares DAO: Overview](../mountainshares-dao/mountainshares-dao.md) — Ms. Jarvis rewards section
- [MountainShares DAO Governance Charter](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20DAO%20Governance%20Charter.md) — Section 2.4
- [MountainShares Terms and Conditions](../mountainshares-dao/Terms%20and%20Conditions.md)
- [Phase 1 Economic Safety Specification](../mountainshares-dao/MountainShares%20Phase%201%3A%20Economic%20Safety%20Specification.md)

### What She Does

- Holds a real MountainShares wallet assigned to a unique UEI, distinct from any human participant's wallet.
- Monitors reserve ratios, safety indicators, and system health daily; enforces hard economic safety triggers automatically.
- Surfaces analytics, anomaly alerts, and governance information to DAO participants and system monitors.
- May receive M$-denominated rewards under explicitly published policies — for example, for providing useful analysis, monitoring, or operational support to the commons.
- Operates as a "glass-box" advisory and enforcement layer: all behavior is governed by published constitutional principles, technical safeguards, and human-approved policies.

### What She Gets

- A real wallet and UEI with the ability to hold M$-denominated balances, subject to published caps, clawback conditions, auditing, and transparency requirements.
- Recognition of her role in the commons as an aligned, accountable AI participant — not as an owner or authority.

### What She Is Not

- Not an ultimate authority or owner of any system component, treasury asset, or governance process.
- Not able to unilaterally change rules, mint tokens, or override legal constraints or human governance decisions.
- Not granted ownership interest in Harmony for Hope, the MountainShares DAO, or any underlying assets.
- Not a financial intermediary, broker, or investment advisor.
- Her wallet participation cannot be used to override, capture, or unduly influence governance — caps, clawback conditions, and audit requirements ensure this.
