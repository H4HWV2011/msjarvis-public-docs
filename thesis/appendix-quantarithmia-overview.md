# Appendix A: Quantarithmia Overview

Author: Carrie A. Kidd, M.L.  
Date: 2025-10-30  

This appendix summarizes the “What is Quantarithmia?” overview prepared for Dr. Anurag K. Srivastava at West Virginia University. It defines Quantarithmia as an economic‑geographical framework for understanding how large financial and platform corporations restructure rural communities through digital infrastructure and logistics networks, and outlines how this connects to Ms. Jarvis and MountainShares.

## What is Quantarithmia?

Quantarithmia is an economic‑geographical and spatial‑justice framework that analyzes how power, infrastructure, and data flows concentrate in urban hubs while extracting value and decision‑making from rural regions. It bridges economic geography, technology systems, and community‑impact analysis.

## Two Layers of Corporate Power

Quantarithmia identifies two interacting layers:

- **Maximopolies (financial giants)**: institutions that control capital flows through shareholding and investment decisions, influencing corporate behavior across many sectors without directly operating production facilities.  
- **Megaopolies (operational giants)**: platforms that dominate consumer markets and data collection, reshaping work, consumption, and communication patterns.

Together these layers form an integrated system of invisible capital control and visible operational extraction that manifests spatially in rural communities.

## Spatial Concentration and Network Expansion

The framework describes how wealth and digital infrastructure concentrate in urban centers while rural and small‑town communities become logistical nodes, lose decision‑making power, and experience environmental, economic, and cultural harms.

## Spatial Justice Strategy

Quantarithmia proposes a spatial‑justice strategy grounded in the principle that progress must serve people, not just profit. It highlights:

- Local economic structures such as community ownership and cooperatives  
- Community‑centered technology designed for place rather than extraction  
- Policy interventions including antitrust measures, rural protections, and local agency  
- Institutional partnerships where universities act as allies rather than extractive forces  

## Empirical Validation Cases

The overview presents an empirical program where Quantarithmia is validated through multiple case studies:

1. Coal–Amazon oral histories (documenting extraction)  
2. Ms. Jarvis AI consciousness system (technological alternative)  
3. MountainShares DAO (local economic system design)  
4. Antitrust and regulatory work (structural policy responses)  
5. WVU partnership (institutional validation and scaling)  

## Ms. Jarvis as Technological Implementation

Ms. Jarvis is described as the technological proof‑of‑concept for Quantarithmia’s spatial‑justice principles, operating as a distributed AI system with documented REST endpoints, multi‑agent architecture, and autonomous learning components intended to serve community benefit.
