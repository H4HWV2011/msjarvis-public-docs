# Appendix B: Quantarithmia Journal Paper

Author: Carrie A. Kidd, M.L.  
Title: *Quantarithmia: A Transdisciplinary Framework for Spatial Justice in Rural Digital Economies – Theory, Implementation, and Empirical Validation Through Community‑Centered Technology*  
Date: 2025-11  

This appendix summarizes the full Quantarithmia journal paper, which formalizes the theoretical framework, methodology, and case‑study program connecting MountainShares, Ms. Jarvis, and Appalachian communities.

## Abstract (Summary)

The paper presents Quantarithmia as a transdisciplinary framework integrating economic geography, econophysics, distributed artificial intelligence, and spatial‑justice theory to analyze and transform patterns of power concentration in rural digital economies. It argues that financial and operational corporate power systematically extract wealth from rural regions while concentrating decision‑making in urban centers, and proposes community‑controlled technological and economic alternatives.

## Theoretical Foundations

The framework synthesizes:

- Classical social theory (Durkheim on anomie, Marx on capital concentration and primitive accumulation, Smith on division of labor, Kant on persons as ends)  
- Contemporary economic geography and spatial theory  
- Complexity science and distributed systems theory  

These strands are combined to analyze rural decline, digital platform penetration, and new forms of infrastructural control.

## Two‑Layer Extraction System

The journal elaborates the Maximopolies/Megaopolies model:

- **Maximopolies**: financial institutions exercising “fictitious capital” power through investment and ownership structures.  
- **Megaopolies**: operational platforms whose network effects and data accumulation restructure everyday life and communication.

Together, they yield a two‑layer system of invisible finance and visible platforms that produces spatially uneven development in rural regions.

## Implementation via MountainShares and Ms. Jarvis

The paper details an empirical program where Quantarithmia is implemented and evaluated through:

- **MountainShares DAO** – a blockchain‑based decentralized autonomous organization serving 55 Appalachian counties, designed around transparency and community governance.  
- **Ms. Jarvis** – a distributed AI system with 23 LLMs operating under democratic governance, with public APIs, monitoring, and documentation.  
- **Participatory action research** – Appalachian communities as co‑designers and primary beneficiaries, combined with quantitative metrics and qualitative cultural‑impact assessment.

Public endpoints and monitoring dashboards are cited for verification and reproducibility.

## Contributions

The journal claims contributions to economic geography, technology governance, rural development, and ethical AI by:

- Providing analytical tools to understand extraction dynamics in rural digital economies  
- Offering practical methodologies for democratic, community‑controlled technology deployment  
- Proposing evaluation frameworks for measuring economic, cultural, and democratic impacts
